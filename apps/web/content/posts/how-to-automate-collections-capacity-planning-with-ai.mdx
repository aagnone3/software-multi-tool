Most collections teams assign work based on habit.

The same collector books are reviewed in the same order each cycle, even when risk and cash opportunity have changed.

AI can help teams plan capacity dynamically so collector time goes where it matters most.

## Why collections capacity planning breaks down

Three things usually cause the problem:

- Workload is assigned by account count, not expected cash impact
- Team bandwidth changes weekly, but plans are static
- Priority lists are rebuilt manually and too late

The result is predictable: high-risk accounts get touched late, and low-value tasks consume prime collector time.

## What AI can automate in planning

A practical AI workflow can automate five planning tasks:

1. **Risk scoring** for open AR based on aging, dispute history, and broken promises
2. **Cash opportunity ranking** by expected collectible value this week
3. **Collector capacity matching** based on available hours and account complexity
4. **Worklist generation** with prioritized accounts and suggested next actions
5. **Daily rebalancing** when new risk signals appear

This turns planning from a weekly spreadsheet exercise into a live operating loop.

## Set guardrails before automation

AI planning should run inside clear rules:

- Define minimum touch frequency by risk segment
- Protect strategic accounts from over-aggressive escalation
- Separate dispute handling from standard collections queues
- Require manager review for high-value strategy changes

Guardrails keep automation consistent with policy and customer experience.

## Rollout approach that works

Start small and measure quickly:

1. Pilot with one collector pod for 4–6 weeks
2. Compare AI-prioritized worklists against current assignment method
3. Track recovered cash per collector hour and promise-to-pay kept rate
4. Expand once the pilot shows stable lift

This gives leaders proof before scaling team-wide.

## Metrics to track

Measure both efficiency and outcomes:

- Cash collected per collector hour
- High-risk account touch timeliness
- Promise-to-pay kept rate
- Backlog growth by aging bucket

If efficiency improves while delinquency trends flatten, the capacity model is working.

## Final takeaway

Collections capacity is too expensive to allocate by instinct.

AI helps AR teams direct limited collector time toward the accounts most likely to move cash and reduce risk—without adding headcount.
