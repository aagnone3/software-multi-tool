Law is one of the most document-intensive professions in the world. Contracts, briefs, depositions, discovery documents, client intake forms, meeting notes — lawyers spend more time reading, writing, and organizing paperwork than actually advising clients.

AI tools are changing that calculus. Here's how law firms are using AI to work more efficiently in 2025.

## The Documentation Burden in Legal Practice

The average attorney spends:

- **3-4 hours per day** reviewing and drafting documents
- **1-2 hours per day** on administrative tasks that don't require a law degree
- **Significant billable time** on work that could be delegated to AI

For small and mid-size firms without large support staff, this overhead is especially painful. AI tools can absorb the rote, repetitive parts of legal document work — leaving attorneys free to do the high-judgment work that clients actually pay for.

## How Law Firms Are Using AI Today

### Contract Review and Analysis

Contract review is among the most time-consuming tasks in legal practice. An AI document analysis tool can:

- Extract key clauses (liability, termination, payment terms, IP ownership)
- Flag unusual or potentially problematic language
- Summarize multi-party agreements into concise overviews
- Compare multiple contracts for consistency

What might take an associate 2-3 hours per contract can be reduced to 20-30 minutes of focused review with AI assistance.

### Meeting Transcription and Note-Taking

Client meetings, depositions, internal strategy sessions — legal professionals take a lot of notes. AI meeting summarizers can:

- Transcribe recorded meetings with speaker identification
- Produce structured summaries with key decisions and action items
- Create searchable transcripts for future reference
- Reduce the need for a dedicated note-taker

This is particularly valuable for depositions and client intake calls where accurate documentation matters.

### Document Summarization

Legal documents are long. Discovery packets can run thousands of pages. An AI summarizer helps attorneys:

- Get the substance of a lengthy document in minutes
- Identify the most relevant sections before diving deep
- Produce client-ready summaries of complex agreements
- Summarize regulatory filings, compliance documents, and policy materials

### Invoice and Expense Processing

Legal billing is notoriously complex. AI invoice processing tools can:

- Extract itemized charges from vendor invoices
- Match expenses to client matters
- Flag unusual billing patterns
- Reduce errors in accounts payable workflows

## Best Practices for AI in Legal Work

### Verify Everything

AI tools make errors. In legal work, errors matter. Use AI as a first draft and first pass — always verify key claims, clause interpretations, and factual summaries against the source document.

### Maintain Client Confidentiality

Use AI tools that are compliant with professional responsibility obligations around client confidentiality. Understand what happens to uploaded documents — are they stored, used for training, or processed ephemerally?

### Start with Lower-Stakes Work

Internal administrative tasks, non-client-facing documents, and summarization of public filings are good starting points before using AI on sensitive client matters.

### Document Your Process

If AI assisted in document preparation, know when and how to disclose that in your practice. Guidance varies by jurisdiction and is evolving quickly.

## Practical Workflow: Reviewing a New Contract

Here's how a firm might use AI to review a vendor services agreement:

1. **Upload the contract** to an AI analysis tool
2. **Request a summary** identifying parties, key dates, obligations, payment terms
3. **Flag potential issues** — non-standard liability caps, missing IP assignment, unusual termination triggers
4. **Export the summary** and review against the original document
5. **Use as a basis** for annotating the document and preparing markup

Total time saved: 60-90 minutes per contract.

## The ROI for Legal Practices

For a 5-attorney firm billing $400/hour:

- 2 hours saved per attorney per day = $800/attorney/day
- At 5 attorneys, that's $4,000/day in recoverable or redirectable billable time
- Even capturing 25% of that time represents $1,000/day in additional capacity

AI tools in this category typically cost a few hundred dollars per month — the ROI is compelling.

## Getting Started

The practical path for a law firm considering AI tools:

1. Start with one repetitive task — document summarization is usually the easiest entry point
2. Test with non-sensitive documents to understand output quality
3. Build a process for verification so AI output always gets a human review
4. Expand to contract analysis and meeting transcription as confidence grows

AI won't replace lawyers. But lawyers who use AI effectively will outcompete those who don't.
