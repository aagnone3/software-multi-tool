Law firms bill by the hour — which means anything that makes lawyers faster directly improves profitability. AI has become one of the most practical levers for legal productivity in 2025.

But legal work is high-stakes. The AI tools that actually get adopted in law firms are the ones that assist lawyers rather than replacing their judgment.

Here's what's working.

## Where AI Is Actually Useful in Legal Work

### Contract Review and Analysis

Reading through 40-page contracts looking for problematic clauses is exactly the kind of work AI handles well. Not because it catches everything — but because it dramatically shortens the time a lawyer needs to spend doing first-pass review.

AI contract analysis tools can:
- Flag unusual or non-standard clauses
- Identify missing standard provisions
- Summarize key terms (payment, termination, liability, IP ownership)
- Compare against your standard template
- Highlight redlines and changes between versions

A lawyer still needs to make the final call on everything. But getting to "this clause is worth flagging" in 30 seconds instead of 10 minutes is a real productivity multiplier.

**Good for:** M&A due diligence, vendor agreement review, real estate transactions, employment contracts

### Document Summarization

Case files, depositions, expert reports, financial records — legal matters involve enormous amounts of text. AI document summarization lets associates quickly extract the key facts without reading every word.

This is especially useful for:
- **Deposition summaries** — long transcripts distilled to key testimony
- **Expert report summaries** — technical documents made accessible
- **Case file onboarding** — new attorneys getting up to speed on complex matters
- **Client matter summaries** — keeping partners informed without reading everything

### Meeting and Call Notes

Client calls, depositions, internal strategy sessions — legal professionals spend significant time on audio-based communication. AI meeting summaries can:

- Transcribe recorded calls automatically
- Identify action items and follow-ups
- Create client-ready call summaries
- Support billing review with time-stamped activity logs

### Legal Research Assistance

AI research tools (like Harvey, Lexis+ AI, and Westlaw Precision) can surface relevant case law faster than traditional keyword search. These are specialized legal research tools that connect to legal databases — different from general-purpose AI.

The value: faster research, more comprehensive coverage, and better confidence that you haven't missed an important precedent.

## What AI Tools Can't Do in Legal Work

It's worth being clear about where AI falls short in legal contexts:

**AI cannot give reliable legal advice.** General-purpose AI tools sometimes confidently state incorrect legal conclusions. Lawyers who rely on AI output without verification create serious liability risk.

**AI makes things up.** This is the "hallucination" problem. AI models can generate citations that don't exist or misrepresent what a case actually held. Every AI-produced legal research output needs to be verified against the actual source.

**AI doesn't know your jurisdiction.** Generic AI tools don't know whether you're practicing in New York or Texas, state court or federal court. Outputs need to be validated for your specific legal context.

The pattern that works: AI handles first-pass review and summarization; lawyers validate, make judgment calls, and sign off.

## Practical AI Workflow for Legal Teams

Here's how the highest-adoption law firms are structuring their AI workflows:

### For Contract Review
1. Upload agreement to AI contract analyzer
2. Review the AI's flagged issues and summary
3. Cross-reference against your standard checklist
4. Make edits and add attorney judgment where needed
5. Produce final redline or markup

Time saved: typically 50-70% of first-pass review time on standard agreements

### For Document Review in Litigation
1. Use AI to categorize documents by relevance and issue
2. Human review focuses on the AI-flagged high-priority documents
3. AI summary of depositions before attorney review
4. Use AI to identify inconsistencies across multiple deposition transcripts

Time saved: varies widely, but 30-50% reduction in review hours is common

### For Client Updates
1. Record client status calls with permission
2. Generate AI summary of call
3. Convert to client-ready update memo
4. Attorney reviews and approves before sending

## Getting Started

The easiest entry point is contract review. Pick a standard, low-stakes agreement type (vendor NDAs, software subscription agreements, commercial leases) and run 10 through an AI analyzer. Compare the AI output to what your attorneys would normally flag.

You'll quickly identify where the AI is useful (consistent first-pass coverage) and where it needs attorney guidance (jurisdiction-specific issues, complex or novel legal questions).

From there, expand to higher-stakes agreement types as you develop confidence in the workflow.
