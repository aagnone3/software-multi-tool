If you're reviewing contracts, invoices, or reports manually, you've probably wondered: is AI actually better? Or is it just faster but less accurate?

The honest answer: it depends on what you're reviewing. Here's a practical breakdown.

## Speed: AI Wins by a Wide Margin

Manual document review speed varies by document length and reviewer experience. Typical benchmarks:

| Document Type | Manual Review | AI Review |
|---|---|---|
| 5-page contract | 45–90 minutes | 15–30 seconds |
| 50-page vendor agreement | 4–8 hours | 30–60 seconds |
| Batch of 100 invoices | 8–16 hours | 5–10 minutes |
| Customer survey (500 responses) | 10–20 hours | 2–5 minutes |

For high-volume, repetitive review tasks, AI is 100–500x faster with no degradation in accuracy over time.

## Accuracy: Context-Dependent

Here's where the nuance comes in. AI document review is excellent at:

✅ **Extracting structured data** (invoice amounts, dates, vendor names)
✅ **Identifying standard clause types** (limitation of liability, auto-renewal, termination for convenience)
✅ **Sentiment classification** (positive/negative/neutral in feedback)
✅ **Flagging patterns** (duplicate invoice numbers, unusual payment terms)

AI is weaker at:

⚠️ **Novel legal structures** it hasn't seen before
⚠️ **Ambiguous language** requiring contextual judgment
⚠️ **Jurisdiction-specific nuances** in contract law
⚠️ **Intent inference** (what did the parties mean vs. what they wrote)

The practical implication: AI does 80% of the work with 95%+ accuracy. A human expert reviews the flagged items. You spend 90% less time, with no reduction in quality for the critical decisions.

## Cost: AI Is Usually 10–50x Cheaper

Manual review costs:
- **Internal staff**: $30–150/hour depending on role
- **Outside counsel**: $200–600/hour for legal review
- **Offshore review**: $8–25/hour with QC overhead

AI review costs (with Software Multi-Tool):
- **Contract review**: A few cents per document
- **Invoice processing**: Fraction of a cent per invoice
- **Feedback analysis**: Pennies per 100 responses

For a company that processes 500 invoices per month, the cost difference is often $2,000–5,000/month in time savings.

## When to Use AI Document Review

Use AI review for:
- **First-pass contract review** before attorney engagement
- **Invoice data extraction** from PDFs and images
- **Customer feedback synthesis** from surveys
- **Meeting notes** and action item extraction
- **News monitoring** and competitive intelligence

Keep manual review for:
- **Final sign-off** on high-stakes contracts
- **Ambiguous language** that AI flagged but couldn't resolve
- **Novel deal structures** without precedent
- **Regulatory filings** requiring certified accuracy

## How to Get Started

The best approach is a hybrid workflow:

1. Run AI review on all incoming documents
2. Review only the flagged/uncertain items manually
3. Use AI output as a first draft for extraction and summaries
4. Apply human judgment to edge cases and final decisions
