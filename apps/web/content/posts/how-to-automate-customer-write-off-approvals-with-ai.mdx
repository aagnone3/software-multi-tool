Write-offs are never just accounting entries.
They are risk decisions.

When approvals happen by email threads and spreadsheet comments, teams lose time, miss context, and create audit gaps.

## 1) Define a clear write-off policy before adding AI

AI cannot fix unclear governance.
Start with explicit policy inputs:

- Approval thresholds by amount
- Required evidence per write-off type
- Escalation paths (finance manager, controller, legal)
- Exceptions that always need manual review

If policy is vague, automation only scales inconsistency.

## 2) Standardize the write-off request payload

Most delays come from missing data. Build a required request structure:

- Customer and account owner
- Total balance and aging buckets
- Collection history summary
- Dispute or service issue status
- Recovery attempts and outcomes
- Proposed write-off amount and rationale

AI works best when every case has the same baseline fields.

## 3) Use AI to create approval-ready case briefs

Instead of forcing approvers to read long notes, generate a concise brief:

- Why balance is considered uncollectible
- What recovery actions were attempted
- Which policy criteria are met
- Confidence score + missing evidence flags
- Recommended approval tier

This cuts review time while making decisions more consistent.

## 4) Route cases by deterministic policy gates

Do not let AI decide alone. Combine AI summaries with hard policy rules:

- **Auto-route to manager** for low-value, complete-evidence cases
- **Escalate to controller/legal** for high-value or disputed cases
- **Hold queue** when mandatory documentation is missing

AI should support routing, while policy enforces authority.

## 5) Add explainability for every recommendation

Approvers should always see *why* a case is prioritized or escalated.

Include structured reason codes like:

- Aging > 180 days
- Multiple failed collection attempts
- Dispute unresolved > 45 days
- Customer inactive/churned

Reason codes make approvals faster and easier to audit later.

## 6) Keep a full audit trail automatically

Every write-off decision should be reconstruction-ready:

- Source data snapshot at decision time
- Generated AI summary and confidence
- Approver identity and timestamp
- Final outcome and policy rule path

This matters for audits, governance reviews, and post-mortems.

## 7) Track decision quality, not just speed

Faster approvals are useful only if outcomes remain sound.

Track:

- Average time-to-decision
- Reopened/reversed write-off rate
- Percentage of cases missing required evidence
- Aging exposure before vs after workflow changes
- Approval consistency across approvers

If speed rises but reversals also rise, tighten evidence gates.

## Common mistakes

1. **Using AI to bypass policy**
   AI should help summarize and route, not override controls.

2. **No mandatory evidence checklist**
   Missing context creates rework and weak decisions.

3. **Treating all write-offs the same**
   Small operational write-offs and strategic account disputes need different paths.

4. **Ignoring feedback loops**
   If outcomes are not fed back into the workflow, recommendation quality stagnates.

## Final take

Write-off approvals should be fast, consistent, and defensible.

AI helps by turning messy account history into clear approval briefs and better routing — but only when deterministic policy controls stay in charge.
