## What Makes Interview Transcription Different

Interview transcription has specific requirements that differ from general meeting transcription:

- **Speaker separation** — who said what matters more than in general meeting notes
- **Accuracy at conversational pace** — interviews often have overlapping speech, fillers, and informal language
- **Timestamps** — researchers need to cite specific moments
- **Export formats** — different research tools require different transcript formats
- **Confidentiality** — many interviews involve sensitive information

## Speaker Separation: The Key Feature for Interviews

Speaker separation (also called diarization) is the ability to automatically identify and label different speakers throughout a recording. It's the most important feature for interview transcription.

Without speaker separation, you get a wall of text with no indication of who said what — forcing you to manually attribute quotes.

With speaker separation, you get something like:

> **Speaker 1 (Researcher):** Can you tell me about your biggest challenge?
>
> **Speaker 2 (Participant):** The hardest part is definitely the documentation...

For multi-person interviews (focus groups, panel interviews), speaker separation becomes even more critical.

## Practical Tips for Better Interview Transcription

**Recording quality matters most:** Even the best AI transcription tools struggle with:
- Background noise (coffee shops, open offices)
- Poor microphone placement
- Strong accents combined with technical jargon
- Multiple overlapping speakers

A decent USB microphone or a Zoom H1n makes a significant accuracy difference.

**File format:** Most AI tools accept MP3, MP4, WAV, M4A. WAV and high-bitrate MP3 typically produce slightly better results.

**Review speaker labels:** Speaker separation automatically assigns Speaker 1, Speaker 2, etc. You'll need to rename these to actual names. This is a 2-minute task vs. hours of manual attribution.

**Combine with synthesis:** The real value for researchers isn't just the transcript — it's having AI identify themes, key quotes, and patterns across multiple interviews. Tools like Software Multi-Tool do both.
