Most close delays are painful.

But unclear close communication is what makes leadership lose confidence.

AI can help finance teams automate executive commentary so updates are faster, more consistent, and tied to real close risk.

## Why close commentary often breaks down

In many teams, commentary is assembled manually from multiple spreadsheets, chat threads, and status meetings.

That creates common issues:

- updates are late because narrative writing starts too close to deadline
- tone and detail vary by author
- risk statements are vague and hard to action
- leaders get too much data and not enough decision-ready context

The result is avoidable uncertainty during the most time-sensitive part of the month.

## A practical AI workflow for executive close updates

1. **Define the standard commentary structure**
   Use a repeatable outline: close status, top blockers, financial impact, mitigation plan, and confidence outlook.

2. **Aggregate close signals daily**
   Pull task completion, open reconciliations, unresolved exceptions, and prior-day movement into a single source.

3. **Generate a first draft automatically**
   Have AI produce a concise draft in leadership-ready language, with clear owner/action references.

4. **Highlight delta vs prior update**
   Force the draft to call out what changed since yesterday so leaders can assess trend, not just snapshot.

5. **Route for controller review**
   Keep human approval before sending; AI drafts, finance owns the final message.

6. **Publish by audience**
   Generate one detailed version for finance leadership and one compressed version for executive stakeholders.

## Best places to pilot first

Start with the recurring sections that take the most manual effort:

- daily close status recap
- top 3 blocker summaries
- material variance commentary
- confidence level and forecasted close date

Track impact over 1–2 close cycles:

- time spent preparing daily close updates
- turnaround time from data cutoff to executive send
- number of clarification follow-ups from leadership
- on-time delivery rate for scheduled close updates

## Guardrails that keep quality high

- require source links for every material risk statement
- block publication when required data feeds are stale
- include explicit confidence labels (high/medium/low)
- log all edits between AI draft and final approved version

## What good looks like

When executive commentary is automated well, teams get:

- faster daily updates without last-minute scramble
- clearer communication of risk and mitigation
- more confidence in close governance across leadership

AI should not replace finance judgment.

It should remove narrative busywork so judgment is applied where it matters most.
