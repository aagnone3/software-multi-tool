Most close handoffs fail for simple reasons:

missing context, unclear ownership, and no final quality check before work moves downstream.

AI can automate handoff QA so each close package is validated before the next team touches it.

## Why close handoffs create avoidable rework

Manual handoffs often break because:

- supporting files are incomplete or inconsistent
- assumptions are not documented
- approver notes are scattered across systems
- downstream owners discover issues too late

When this happens late in close, teams burn time chasing context instead of finishing the cycle.

## A practical AI workflow for close handoff QA

1. **Define a handoff quality checklist**
   Standardize required artifacts: reconciliations, commentary, exception notes, approver history, and next-step owner.

2. **Collect handoff inputs automatically**
   Pull task metadata, file attachments, comments, and status transitions into one structured payload.

3. **Run AI completeness checks**
   Validate that required documents exist, fields are populated, and evidence matches the task scope.

4. **Run AI clarity checks**
   Flag vague notes, missing rationale, unclear exception handling, or unresolved reviewer comments.

5. **Score handoff readiness**
   Assign a readiness score (ready / needs fixes / blocked) with explicit reasons and remediation steps.

6. **Route failed handoffs back with action items**
   Send the package back to the originating owner with a short fix list and due time.

7. **Publish audit-ready QA summaries**
   Store pass/fail results and change history so teams can prove control effectiveness during audits.

## Best places to pilot first

Start with handoffs that cause repeat delays:

- accrual package reviews
- intercompany reconciliation signoff
- revenue cutoff support transfers
- cash and balance-sheet account reconciliations

Track results for 1–2 close cycles:

- first-pass handoff acceptance rate
- rework loops per handoff
- average handoff turnaround time
- close-day exceptions caused by handoff defects

## Governance guardrails that matter

- keep human signoff for materiality-sensitive handoffs
- version every handoff package and QA result
- require explicit owner + due date for remediation
- review top recurring QA failure reasons monthly

## What good looks like

With automated handoff QA, teams typically see:

- fewer downstream surprises
- less back-and-forth rework
- faster close task flow between owners
- stronger audit defensibility on close controls
