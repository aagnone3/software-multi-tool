# How to Automate Your Contract Review Process with AI

Contract review is one of the most time-consuming tasks in any business. Legal teams, procurement departments, and operations managers all spend significant hours reading, analyzing, and negotiating contracts — hours that could be spent on higher-value work.

AI contract review tools have matured to the point where they can reliably assist with the bulk of this work. This guide explains how to build an AI-assisted contract review workflow that saves time without creating legal risk.

## The Problem with Manual Contract Review

Manual contract review is:

- **Slow**: A thorough review of a complex commercial contract can take 4-8 hours
- **Inconsistent**: Different reviewers flag different issues depending on their background and attention
- **Expensive**: Outside counsel rates for contract review run $300-$800/hour
- **Error-prone**: Fatigue increases the chance of missing important clauses in long documents

Most organizations have a backlog of contracts waiting for review. Sales deals slow down. Vendor onboarding gets delayed. Compliance obligations get missed.

## Where AI Contract Review Helps Most

AI contract review tools are most effective at:

### 1. Initial Extraction and Summarization

Before a human reviewer reads a 40-page master services agreement, an AI can produce a summary of:

- Parties, effective date, and term
- Payment terms and pricing structure
- Intellectual property ownership and license grants
- Indemnification and limitation of liability caps
- Termination rights and notice requirements
- Data privacy and security obligations
- Governing law and dispute resolution mechanism

This summary lets human reviewers immediately focus on the sections that need attention, rather than reading front-to-back.

### 2. Standard Clause Identification and Flagging

AI systems trained on contract language can identify when standard clauses are missing, unusually favorable to the counterparty, or outside normal market standards. Common flags include:

- Uncapped indemnification obligations
- Missing limitation of liability
- Broad IP assignment language
- Unusual termination-for-convenience rights
- Auto-renewal clauses without adequate notice requirements
- One-sided warranty disclaimers

### 3. Obligation Extraction

Contracts create ongoing obligations — deadlines, reporting requirements, maintenance obligations, payment schedules. AI can extract these into a structured list that can be added to a contract management system.

### 4. Comparison Against Templates and Playbooks

If your organization has standard contract templates or a legal playbook that defines acceptable positions on key terms, AI can compare incoming contracts against those standards and flag deviations automatically.

## Building an AI Contract Review Workflow

Here's a practical workflow for teams that want to use AI for contract review:

### Step 1: Intake and Triage

When a contract comes in for review, immediately run it through an AI analyzer. This takes less than a minute and produces a summary you can use to:

- Assess the complexity and risk level of the contract
- Determine how much human review time to budget
- Identify specific sections that need close attention
- Estimate whether outside counsel is needed

Our [AI contract analyzer](/tools/contract-analyzer) accepts PDF and Word uploads and returns a structured summary within seconds.

### Step 2: Flag Review

Read the AI's flagged items first. Address the highest-risk issues before doing a full read-through. This prevents you from spending time on low-risk sections before you know whether the deal is even viable.

### Step 3: Full Review (Focused)

Complete a targeted review of the flagged sections plus any additional areas relevant to this specific contract type and relationship. The AI summary lets you skip boilerplate sections that don't require negotiation.

### Step 4: Redline and Negotiate

Make your changes. Use the AI summary as a reference when discussing issues with the counterparty — it helps you cite specific clause locations quickly.

### Step 5: Extract and Store Obligations

Before the contract is signed, run a final extraction to pull all ongoing obligations into your contract management system. Set reminders for key dates.

## What AI Contract Review Won't Do

AI cannot replace legal judgment. Specific areas where human expertise remains essential:

- **Evaluating business context**: AI doesn't know whether a particular risk is acceptable given the strategic importance of the deal
- **Novel or complex structures**: Unusual transaction structures may fall outside the AI's training
- **Final legal opinions**: AI cannot provide legal advice or sign off on regulatory compliance
- **Negotiation strategy**: Deciding which issues to fight for and which to concede requires business and relationship judgment

The right mental model is that AI handles the mechanical parts of contract review — reading, organizing, and flagging — while humans handle judgment and decision-making.

## Cost Savings from AI-Assisted Contract Review

Organizations that have implemented AI contract review report:

- **60-80% reduction in time per contract review** for standard commercial contracts
- **Significant reduction in outside counsel spend** for initial review and triage
- **Fewer missed obligations** because AI catches items humans skip when fatigued
- **Faster deal cycles** because review backlogs shrink

For a business reviewing 50 contracts per month, even a 3-hour reduction in review time per contract represents 150 hours of recovered capacity — time that can go to higher-value legal or business work.

## Getting Started Today

You don't need to build a complex system to start saving time with AI contract review. Start simple:

1. **Try a single contract**: Upload your next incoming contract to an AI contract analyzer and compare the output to your own review
2. **Build a checklist**: Use the AI's extraction to build a checklist of what you need to verify in your contract type
3. **Develop your playbook**: Document which AI flags are always deal-breakers vs. negotiable
4. **Scale gradually**: Once you trust the system on standard contracts, expand to more complex agreements
