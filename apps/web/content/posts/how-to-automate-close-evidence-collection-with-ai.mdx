Most close delays are not caused by missing entries.

They are caused by missing evidence.

Teams finish reconciliations, approvals, and variance reviews, then lose hours assembling screenshots, exports, and signoff artifacts across systems. AI can automate that collection loop and keep evidence ready as work happens.

## Why close evidence collection breaks

Close evidence prep usually fails in predictable ways:

- supporting files are saved in personal folders instead of controlled locations
- naming conventions differ by team and month
- links in checklists break or point to stale versions
- final packets are assembled at the end of close under deadline pressure

That creates late-night cleanup, rework during controller review, and extra audit follow-up later.

## A practical AI workflow for evidence collection

1. **Define evidence requirements by close task**
   Map each key task to required support (report export, approval proof, reconciliation workbook, commentary).

2. **Collect artifacts automatically from source systems**
   Trigger pulls from ERP, ticketing, and workflow tools when tasks change to completed.

3. **Classify and name files consistently**
   Use AI to apply a standard naming pattern: period, entity, process area, task, and artifact type.

4. **Validate evidence completeness in real time**
   Flag missing attachments, invalid formats, or outdated timestamps before signoff meetings.

5. **Assemble close-ready evidence packets**
   Generate controller and auditor packet views by area (cash, AP, revenue, intercompany) with source links intact.

6. **Summarize unresolved evidence gaps daily**
   Produce a short open-items report with owners, due dates, and impact level.

## Where to start

Start with one high-friction evidence path, such as:

- revenue recognition support package
- intercompany reconciliation evidence set
- close checklist controls required for SOX testing

A focused pilot creates measurable time savings without changing your full close architecture.

## Controls that keep it reliable

- **Human approval before final packet release:** AI prepares, finance signs off.
- **Immutable versioning:** store packet snapshots by period and process area.
- **Source traceability:** every artifact links back to originating system records.
- **Access controls:** role-based permissions for sensitive financial evidence.

## What success looks like

After 1–2 cycles, teams typically see:

- less end-of-close scrambling for missing support
- faster controller and audit readiness reviews
- fewer evidence-related reopen requests
- cleaner handoffs between accounting, finance ops, and audit stakeholders

---

If your close is technically complete but still hard to defend, automate evidence collection first. It turns close output into audit-ready proof continuously, not at the last minute.
