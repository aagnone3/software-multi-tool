# How to Use AI for Contract Due Diligence (A Practical Guide)

Contract due diligence is one of the most document-intensive tasks in business. Whether you're acquiring a company, onboarding a major vendor, or reviewing a real estate portfolio, you need to know exactly what's in those contracts—and where the risks are.

Manual review is slow. A trained lawyer reads about 20-30 pages per hour on a focused day. A typical M&A deal might have 500-1,000 contracts. The math doesn't work.

AI contract analysis tools have changed this entirely. Here's how to actually use them.

## What AI Can and Can't Do in Contract Due Diligence

**What AI handles well:**
- Extracting standard terms (parties, dates, payment terms, renewal dates, termination rights)
- Identifying clauses that deviate from standard templates
- Flagging missing provisions (e.g., no data breach notification clause)
- Summarizing long contracts into key points
- Comparing clause language across a portfolio of contracts

**What still requires human judgment:**
- Assessing whether a risk clause is acceptable given the deal context
- Negotiating terms
- Legal advice on jurisdiction-specific enforceability
- Strategic decisions about deal structure

The goal isn't to remove lawyers from due diligence. It's to let them spend their time on judgment calls instead of extraction work.

## Step-by-Step: AI-Assisted Contract Due Diligence

### Step 1: Define what you're looking for

Before running any AI analysis, create a list of the terms and clauses that matter for your specific deal:

- **Commercial terms**: Contract value, payment terms, renewal/auto-renew, price escalation
- **Risk terms**: Liability caps, indemnification, warranties, representations
- **Operational terms**: Exclusivity, non-compete, IP ownership, data rights
- **Exit terms**: Termination for convenience, change of control, material adverse change
- **Compliance terms**: GDPR/data processing, regulatory requirements, audit rights

Different deals prioritize different things. An M&A acquisition focuses on liability exposure and change-of-control clauses. A vendor onboarding focuses on data rights and SLA terms.

### Step 2: Run AI extraction on the full contract set

Upload your contracts to an AI contract analysis tool. A good tool will:

1. Parse the document (handling PDFs, Word docs, scanned documents)
2. Identify clause types automatically
3. Extract the relevant text for each clause type you care about
4. Flag clauses that appear unusual or non-standard
5. Generate a structured summary for each contract

This process takes minutes per contract, not hours.

### Step 3: Review the exception report

Don't review every contract in full. Use the AI to surface:

- **Non-standard clauses**: Language that deviates significantly from market standard
- **Missing clauses**: Contracts that lack standard protections (e.g., limitation of liability)
- **High-risk terms**: Unusual liability exposure, uncapped indemnities, broad warranty language
- **Anomalies**: Unusual payment terms, unusually short or long terms, unusual counterparty relationships

Focus your manual review time on the exceptions, not the standard documents.

### Step 4: Generate summary reports

For internal stakeholders (board, deal team, lenders), generate summary reports that translate contract findings into business impact:

- "12 of 47 vendor contracts have uncapped liability provisions"
- "8 contracts have change-of-control clauses that may require consent"
- "3 contracts have automatic renewal provisions expiring within 90 days of closing"

AI tools can generate these summaries automatically from the extracted data.

### Step 5: Create a risk register

Map identified contract risks to a deal risk register:

| Risk | Contracts Affected | Estimated Exposure | Mitigation |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Uncapped liability | 12 vendor contracts | TBD | Negotiate caps pre-close or escrow |
| Change-of-control consent | 8 contracts | Potential termination risk | Notify counterparties pre-close |
| Auto-renewal | 3 contracts | Ongoing obligation | Budget for or negotiate early exit |

## Common Contract Due Diligence Scenarios

### M&A due diligence

When acquiring a company, you typically inherit all their contracts. Key questions:
- What are the top 20 contracts by value, and what are the key terms?
- Which contracts have change-of-control provisions that could terminate or require consent?
- What's the aggregate liability exposure across the portfolio?
- Are there any IP ownership or exclusivity provisions that could affect the business?

AI can scan hundreds of contracts in hours and produce a structured risk summary.

### Vendor/supplier contract review

Before onboarding a major vendor:
- Does the contract include adequate data protection provisions (especially for SaaS tools handling your data)?
- What's the liability cap relative to the contract value?
- What are the SLA terms and remedies for service failures?
- Are there exclusivity or restriction provisions that limit your optionality?

### Real estate portfolio review

Lease review at scale:
- Rent escalation provisions across the portfolio
- Break clauses and renewal options
- Repair and maintenance obligations
- Permitted use restrictions

### Employment contract review

In an acquisition:
- Non-compete and non-solicitation provisions for key employees
- Equity vesting schedules and acceleration terms
- Change-of-control bonuses and golden parachutes
- Restrictive covenant enforceability by jurisdiction

## Choosing an AI Contract Analysis Tool

**What to evaluate:**

**Accuracy**: Does it correctly extract the clause types you care about? Test on a sample set where you already know the answers.

**Coverage**: Does it handle the document types in your portfolio (PDF, Word, scanned PDFs)? Can it handle documents in multiple languages if your portfolio is international?

**Customization**: Can you define custom clause types beyond the defaults? Due diligence needs vary by deal type.

**Output format**: Can it export to Excel or the format your deal team uses for tracking?

**Security**: Contracts contain highly sensitive business information. Understand exactly how the tool handles your data, where it's stored, and who can access it.

**Integration**: Does it integrate with your document management system (iManage, SharePoint, NetSuite)?

## The Time Math

A 500-contract portfolio at 30 minutes per contract = 250 hours of lawyer time.

With AI pre-processing, you review exceptions only:
- AI processes all 500 contracts: 2-4 hours
- Lawyer reviews 50 exception contracts at 30 minutes each: 25 hours
- Lawyer reviews AI-generated summaries for remaining 450: 15 hours

Total with AI: ~42 hours vs. 250 hours manually.

The savings are real. More importantly, the AI review catches things that humans miss when fatigued after reviewing the 200th contract.

## Getting Started

1. Pick one deal type to pilot (start with vendor contracts, which are high-volume and lower-stakes than M&A)
2. Define the 10 terms you care most about extracting
3. Run a pilot on 20-30 contracts you've already reviewed manually, and check accuracy
4. Refine your extraction template based on the pilot
5. Roll out to full contract sets

Contract due diligence is one of the highest-ROI applications of AI in legal and business operations. The technology is mature, the time savings are documented, and the risk of missing something important goes down—not up—when AI flags exceptions your team then reviews.
