# The Best AI Tools for Event Planners in 2025

Event planning involves an extraordinary amount of documentation — vendor contracts, venue agreements, client briefs, run-of-show documents, post-event reports, and dozens of planning meeting notes. AI tools can handle a significant portion of that administrative load.

## Where AI Adds Value for Event Planners

### Vendor Contract Review
Event planners sign contracts with caterers, AV companies, venues, transportation providers, and photographers. Each contract has different cancellation policies, deposit requirements, and liability terms. AI contract analyzers extract the critical terms in minutes instead of hours.

Key clauses to surface:
- Cancellation and rescheduling penalties
- Force majeure provisions
- Payment schedules and deposit requirements
- Damage liability and insurance requirements

### Meeting Summarization
Planning meetings are frequent and often long. AI meeting summarizers turn recordings into structured notes with action items, decisions, and owner assignments — saving 30-60 minutes per meeting.

### Post-Event Documentation
Post-event debriefs are notoriously hard to capture consistently. Record the debrief, run it through an AI summarizer, and get a structured report that can go directly into your client file.

### Expense Report Processing
Events generate receipts from dozens of vendors across days or weeks. AI expense processors can categorize, validate, and summarize expense data before it goes to accounting.

**Before AI:**
An event planner spends 2 hours reviewing a 30-page venue contract, 45 minutes writing meeting notes after a planning call, and 1.5 hours sorting receipts from a 3-day conference.

Total savings: nearly 4 hours on a single event cycle.
