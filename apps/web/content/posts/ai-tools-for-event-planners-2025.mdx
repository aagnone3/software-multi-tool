Event planning involves an enormous amount of paperwork — vendor contracts, catering invoices, AV agreements, venue terms, sponsor contracts — alongside the operational complexity of keeping everything coordinated. Most of that paperwork requires reading comprehension and extraction, not event expertise.

AI tools are helping event planners cut administrative overhead and spend more time on the work that actually requires their judgment.

## Where Event Planners Lose Time

The biggest time sinks in event planning administration:

- **Contract review**: venue agreements, vendor contracts, speaker agreements, sponsor terms
- **Invoice processing**: catering, AV, decor, travel, printing, venue deposits
- **Meeting documentation**: client kickoffs, vendor calls, planning committee meetings
- **Post-event reporting**: attendance summaries, budget actuals, sponsor deliverable confirmation

AI helps with all four.

## 4 AI Tools Worth Knowing

### 1. Contract Analysis Tools

Event contracts have a lot of buried risk: force majeure clauses, cancellation penalties, attrition requirements for hotel blocks, liability provisions, and exclusivity clauses.

AI contract analysis tools can:
- Extract key terms (cancellation deadlines, deposit schedules, minimum guarantees)
- Flag unusual liability or indemnification language
- Compare contracts against prior events to spot scope drift
- Summarize multi-page venue agreements into one-page decision documents

For event planners managing 20–50 events per year, this can save meaningful review time on every event.

### 2. Invoice Processing and Budget Reconciliation

Post-event budget reconciliation often involves reconciling dozens of invoices against budget line items across multiple vendors. AI invoice processing tools extract invoice data, categorize line items, and surface discrepancies automatically.

This is particularly valuable for events with complex sponsor billing, where you're tracking what was delivered against contracted deliverables and billing accordingly.

### 3. Meeting Summarization

Client planning meetings, vendor check-ins, and debrief calls are all easier to manage when you're not manually writing up notes during the call. AI meeting summarizers can turn recordings into structured summaries with action items, decisions, and follow-up owners.

Many event teams now upload audio or video from client calls and share AI-generated summaries with all stakeholders within an hour — which reduces misaligned expectations and missed action items.

### 4. Feedback Analysis

Post-event surveys and attendee feedback forms generate text data that needs to be analyzed. AI tools can process hundreds of open-text survey responses and extract themes, sentiment patterns, and specific suggestions — rather than having someone manually read through every response.

## Getting Started

The easiest starting point is meeting summarization — upload a client call recording and compare the AI summary against your manual notes. If the quality is good, extend it to vendor calls.

Contract review is the second-highest-value use case, especially for planners handling high volumes or complex events where contract terms have significant financial risk.

Don't try to automate everything at once. Find the two or three tasks where AI saves the most time and build from there.

## The Limits

AI doesn't replace the relationship management, creative judgment, and logistical problem-solving that makes event planners valuable. It handles reading, extracting, and summarizing — the administrative work that consumes time without requiring expertise.

That's the right division of labor: AI handles the paperwork, you handle the event.
