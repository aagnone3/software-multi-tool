Event planning is coordinating complexity at scale. You're managing venues, caterers, AV teams, speakers, sponsors, and attendees — all simultaneously, all with hard deadlines.

AI tools won't plan the event for you. But they'll handle enough of the administrative burden that you can focus on the coordination and creativity that actually makes events successful.

## The Administrative Reality of Event Planning

Before getting to the fun parts, event planners spend significant time on:

- **Contract review** — venue agreements, vendor contracts, speaker agreements, sponsor deals
- **Vendor correspondence** — proposals, invoices, confirmations, follow-ups
- **Meeting documentation** — client calls, vendor meetings, planning sessions
- **Budget tracking** — invoices, expenses, deposit tracking, final reconciliation
- **Research** — venue options, vendor capabilities, industry trends, competitor events

Most of this is repeatable, pattern-driven work. AI handles it well.

## Contract Review and Vendor Agreements

Event planning generates a constant flow of contracts: venue agreements with complex attrition clauses, catering minimums, AV service agreements, speaker contracts with detailed requirements, and sponsor packages.

AI document analysis helps you:

- Extract key terms quickly (payment schedules, cancellation policies, force majeure clauses)
- Flag unusual or risky provisions before you sign
- Compare vendor proposals for consistent terms
- Track obligations across multiple vendor agreements

For major events, you might review 20+ contracts. AI lets you process them faster without missing critical clauses.

**Tools to use:** [AI contract analysis tools](/tools/contract-analyzer) work well for vendor agreements.

## Meeting and Call Summaries

Event planning involves constant calls: initial client consultations, venue site visits, vendor briefings, planning committee meetings, post-event debriefs.

AI meeting summarization captures:

- Client preferences and requirements discussed
- Vendor commitments and what was promised
- Action items and who owns them
- Decisions made and the reasoning behind them

This is especially valuable for client calls. When you're in discovery mode, getting every detail right matters. An AI summary means you can focus on the conversation instead of taking notes.

**Tools to use:** [AI meeting summarizers](/tools/meeting-summarizer) work with recorded calls.

## Invoice and Expense Processing

Event budgets involve dozens of invoices: venue deposit, catering final, AV rental, floral arrangements, linens, photography, entertainment. Tracking these manually is error-prone.

AI invoice processing:

- Extracts amounts, vendor names, and line items automatically
- Categorizes expenses against your budget structure
- Flags invoices that don't match contracts or quotes
- Generates expense summaries for client reporting

**Tools to use:** [Invoice data extraction tools](/tools/invoice-processor) handle PDF and image invoices.

## Industry and Venue Research

Staying current on event industry trends matters for client recommendations: new venue openings, catering concepts, technology integrations, attendee experience innovations.

AI news monitoring helps you:

- Track venue openings and closings in your markets
- Follow industry publications and trends
- Monitor competitor events and industry awards
- Stay current on food and beverage trends

**Tools to use:** [AI news analyzers](/tools/news-analyzer) aggregate and summarize industry news.

## Practical Workflow for Event Planners

**During client intake:** Record your initial consultation. Run it through an AI summarizer after. You'll have a structured brief of requirements, preferences, and priorities that feeds directly into your proposal and planning documents.

**During vendor negotiations:** Use AI to review contracts before signing. Focus human attention on the flagged clauses — attrition minimums, cancellation penalties, force majeure — rather than reading every standard clause.

**During event execution:** Track all vendor invoices through AI processing for fast budget reconciliation. You'll close out event finances in hours instead of days.

**After events:** Use AI to analyze post-event feedback and generate summary reports for clients and stakeholders.

## What AI Can't Replace in Event Planning

Event planning is fundamentally relationship-driven. AI doesn't:

- Build vendor relationships that get you preferential booking and pricing
- Manage the moment-of-truth decisions that happen during live events
- Read the room with clients to understand unstated preferences
- Solve the unexpected problems that happen at every event

Your value is judgment, relationships, and creative problem-solving. AI handles the paperwork so you have more capacity for those things.

## Getting Started

Event planners can start with the highest-friction administrative tasks:

1. **Next event contract review** — run vendor agreements through AI analysis before signing
2. **Next client call** — record and summarize the meeting, then check against your notes
3. **Post-event invoices** — batch process through AI invoice extraction for budget reconciliation

Start simple, see where the time savings show up, then expand to more of your workflow.
