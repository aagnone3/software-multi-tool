# How to Automate SOX IT Access Provisioning Reviews with AI

User access provisioning is one of the highest-risk areas in SOX IT general controls. Granting inappropriate access—even temporarily—can create segregation of duties violations, create audit findings, and in the worst cases enable fraud. Yet access provisioning reviews are tedious, manual, and error-prone when done at scale.

AI tools are changing this. Here's how finance and IT audit teams are using AI to automate SOX IT access provisioning reviews without sacrificing control quality.

## The Problem with Manual Access Provisioning Reviews

Traditional access provisioning review involves:

1. Pulling access request tickets from your ITSM system
2. Validating the requester, approver, and business justification
3. Checking the requested role against the user's job function
4. Running SoD conflict checks against a role matrix
5. Verifying that access was provisioned within approved scope
6. Documenting the review and filing evidence

With hundreds or thousands of access events per quarter, even experienced teams struggle to review every provisioning event meaningfully. The result is often a sampling approach that leaves coverage gaps—exactly the kind of thing external auditors flag.

## What AI Can Automate in Access Provisioning Reviews

### 1. Access Request Validation

AI can parse access request tickets and automatically verify:

- **Requester identity**: Is the requester a current employee with a valid role?
- **Approver chain**: Did the right manager or system owner approve the request?
- **Business justification completeness**: Is the justification specific and tied to a business need?
- **Timeliness**: Was access provisioned within the approved window?

This alone eliminates the manual triage step for the majority of routine provisioning events.

### 2. Role Appropriateness Analysis

Given a user's job title, department, and manager, AI can evaluate whether the requested role is appropriate. This is particularly valuable for:

- New hires requesting elevated access in their first 30 days
- Transfers between departments where prior access may no longer be appropriate
- Contractors requesting access beyond their stated engagement scope

AI won't replace your RBAC policy, but it can flag exceptions for human review far faster than a manual process.

### 3. Segregation of Duties Conflict Detection

SoD conflict detection is the most labor-intensive part of access provisioning review. AI can:

- Cross-reference new access requests against the user's existing access profile
- Flag combinations that violate your SoD rule matrix (e.g., a user who can both create and approve payments)
- Prioritize conflicts by risk level so reviewers focus on high-risk violations first

This is especially valuable in ERP environments (SAP, Oracle, Workday) where role combinations are complex and hard to reason about manually.

### 4. Evidence Package Generation

Once provisioning events are reviewed, AI can generate structured audit evidence packages that include:

- Summary of all provisioning events in scope
- Review conclusions for each event (approved/flagged/exception)
- Supporting documentation extracted from the source tickets
- SoD conflict matrix annotations
- Sign-off routing for control owners

This turns what used to be a multi-day documentation effort into an automated output from the review process itself.

## How to Implement AI-Assisted Access Provisioning Reviews

### Step 1: Define Your Review Scope

Start by defining what you're reviewing: new provisioning, access modifications, or elevated privilege requests. Not all provisioning events carry the same SOX risk—focus AI automation on the highest-risk categories first.

### Step 2: Connect Your Data Sources

AI-assisted review requires structured data from:

- Your ITSM/ticketing system (ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, etc.)
- Your identity governance platform (SailPoint, Saviynt, CyberArk, etc.)
- Your ERP or application access log
- Your SoD rule matrix

The cleaner your data, the more effective the AI automation.

### Step 3: Define Escalation Criteria

AI should handle routine, low-risk provisioning events automatically and surface exceptions for human review. Define clear criteria for what requires escalation:

- SoD conflicts above a certain severity threshold
- Access to financially significant systems
- Provisioning events missing required approvals
- Unusual patterns (late-night provisioning, bulk grants, etc.)

### Step 4: Generate and Review the Evidence Package

Review the AI-generated evidence package as a control owner, not as a manual reviewer. Your job shifts from line-by-line review to evaluating the AI's exception flags and signing off on the overall population.

### Step 5: File and Archive

File the completed evidence package in your GRC system with timestamps, reviewer sign-off, and a reference to the AI review methodology. External auditors increasingly accept AI-assisted review as long as the methodology is documented and a qualified reviewer has signed off.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

**Over-relying on sampling**: AI allows you to review 100% of provisioning events—don't default back to sampling just because you can.

**Ignoring role creep**: Access provisioning reviews are one piece of the puzzle. Pair them with quarterly access recertification to catch accumulated privilege drift.

**Treating AI output as final**: AI flags exceptions; humans make the call. Don't skip the exception review step.

**Missing coverage for privileged accounts**: Service accounts, admin accounts, and emergency access IDs often have separate provisioning processes. Make sure your AI review covers them.

## The Bottom Line

SOX IT access provisioning reviews don't have to be a quarterly scramble. With AI, you can move from sampling-based review to comprehensive coverage, catch SoD conflicts before they become audit findings, and generate audit-ready evidence in a fraction of the time.

Start with one access category—new hire provisioning or elevated privilege requests—and validate the AI output against your manual process. Once you trust the model, expand coverage and reclaim the time your team was spending on manual review.
