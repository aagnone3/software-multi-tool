Most journal entry errors are not calculation errors.

They are support-quality errors: weak documentation, missing approvals, vague narratives, or evidence that does not actually tie to the entry posted.

AI helps finance teams validate entry support before reviewer time is consumed.

## Where journal entry review usually breaks

Common failure points include:

1. **Missing required support** for manual entries
2. **Generic explanations** that do not justify the accounting treatment
3. **Approvals without context** or incomplete reviewer evidence
4. **Late-cycle entries** posted with compressed review windows

These issues create rework and increase audit friction.

## What to automate first

The goal is to standardize support quality checks, not replace accounting judgment.

### 1) Support completeness checks

AI can verify each entry package includes required evidence by entry type:

- source document attached
- account mapping rationale
- period and entity context
- preparer and reviewer attestations

Incomplete packages should be routed back automatically before formal review.

### 2) Narrative quality validation

Many entries include explanations that are technically present but operationally weak.

AI can flag narratives that are:

- too short to explain the business event
- missing basis for assumptions or accrual estimates
- inconsistent with attached support values
- copied from prior periods without updates

This improves reviewer efficiency and consistency.

### 3) Policy alignment screening

AI can check entries against close and accounting policies:

- materiality threshold triggers
- restricted account combinations
- required secondary approval for sensitive accounts
- posting cutoff compliance

Potential policy exceptions can be escalated before posting or before close sign-off.

### 4) Audit trail packaging

Each validated entry can produce a structured control trail:

- checks performed
- exceptions identified
- remediation actions and timestamps
- final approval chain

That turns audit support retrieval into a fast lookup process instead of ad hoc reconstruction.

## Practical rollout plan

1. Start with manual and top-side entries in one business unit.
2. Run AI validation in parallel with current review for 1-2 closes.
3. Tune exception rules to reduce false positives.
4. Expand to accruals, reclasses, and high-risk accounts.
5. Add dashboards for unresolved support exceptions by owner.

## Metrics that show value

Track outcomes tied to close quality and control maturity:

- % journal entries passing support checks on first submission
- average reviewer turnaround time per entry
- number of support-related rejections per close
- late-entry exception count
- audit comments related to journal entry documentation

## Final take

Journal entry control quality depends on support quality discipline. AI gives finance teams a scalable way to enforce support standards, surface weak entries early, and make close review faster and more defensible.
