Month-end close slows down when journal entries wait in inboxes, approvers miss context, or reviewers find issues too late. AI can automate routing and quality checks so entries move faster with less back-and-forth.

## Why approval workflows break during close

Most teams still run approvals through email and chat. That creates common problems:

- Approvals are assigned manually and inconsistently
- Supporting documents are missing or incomplete
- High-risk entries are not escalated quickly enough
- Status tracking is fragmented across tools

The result is slower close cycles and higher audit risk.

## What to automate first

Start with repeatable controls that remove bottlenecks:

1. **Risk-based routing**
   Route low-risk entries to standard approvers and escalate high-value or unusual entries automatically.

2. **Supporting-document checks**
   Validate that required backup files are attached before the entry reaches an approver.

3. **Policy validation**
   Flag entries that violate account mapping, threshold, or timing rules.

4. **SLA timers and escalation**
   Trigger reminders and escalation if an entry sits too long without action.

5. **Audit-ready logs**
   Capture who approved what, when, and why in a searchable trail.

## Example AI-assisted approval flow

A practical close workflow can look like this:

- Analyst submits entry package
- AI classifies risk based on amount, account, and historical patterns
- AI verifies supporting documentation completeness
- Entry routes to the correct approver queue
- Approver receives a concise AI summary of changes and risk signals
- If untouched past SLA, workflow escalates automatically
- Final approval and rationale are logged for audit

This preserves human control while removing manual coordination work.

## Metrics to track

Measure improvement with clear operational KPIs:

- Average approval turnaround time
- Percentage of entries returned for missing evidence
- Number of escalations per close cycle
- Close completion date variance month to month
- Audit exceptions tied to journal approvals

Improvements in these metrics usually show up within one or two close cycles.

## Implementation tips

- Start with one entity or one entry class before scaling
- Keep approval policy rules explicit and versioned
- Use AI summaries to assist decisions, not replace reviewers
- Review exception patterns monthly and tune routing rules

## Final takeaway

Automating journal entry approval workflows with AI does not remove finance controls — it strengthens them. Teams close faster, approvers get cleaner packets, and audit trails become easier to defend.
