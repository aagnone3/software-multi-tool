# How to Automate Employee Onboarding with AI

First impressions matter. Studies consistently show that employees who have a structured onboarding experience are significantly more likely to stay beyond their first year.

The problem? Onboarding is time-intensive. For HR teams already stretched thin, delivering a great experience for every new hire often means cutting corners somewhere.

AI tools can help you automate the operational parts of onboarding — paperwork, document review, training summaries, and more — so you can focus on what humans do best: building relationships and answering real questions.

---

## What Can Be Automated in Onboarding?

Not everything should be automated. New hire check-ins, manager 1:1s, and culture introductions need a human touch. But a lot of onboarding is administrative:

- Reviewing and processing new hire paperwork
- Summarizing policy documents and benefits guides
- Transcribing and summarizing orientation sessions
- Processing expense submissions from relocation or equipment purchases
- Extracting key terms from employment agreements
- Analyzing onboarding survey feedback

These are exactly the tasks where AI tools excel.

- Transcribe the recorded session
- Generate a structured summary with key topics, action items, and links to resources
- Create a searchable knowledge base entry for future new hires

### Step 2: Extract Key Terms from Offer Letters and Contracts

New hires often have questions about their employment agreements. Save yourself (and legal) repetitive back-and-forth by proactively summarizing key terms:

- Base salary and bonus structure
- Equity terms (vesting schedule, cliff, exercise window)
- Non-compete and non-solicitation scope
- PTO policy and accrual
- At-will employment language

### Step 3: Process Equipment and Relocation Expenses

Remote hires often submit a wave of expenses in their first two weeks — home office setup, relocation, and equipment. Automate the categorization and processing with an expense tool:

- Upload receipts or expense reports
- Automatically categorize by type (equipment, travel, meals, office supplies)
- Flag anything that needs manager approval

### Step 4: Analyze Onboarding Survey Responses

Most companies send a 30/60/90-day survey to new hires. The responses are goldmines for improving your onboarding process — but reading them individually is time-consuming.

Use a Feedback Analyzer to:
- Upload open-ended survey responses
- Extract common themes and sentiment scores
- Identify specific friction points (e.g., "unclear IT setup process" or "manager intro felt rushed")

### Step 5: Summarize Training Sessions

Compliance training, product walkthroughs, and tool tutorials are often delivered live or via recording. Use AI to create structured notes that new hires can reference later:

- Upload or transcribe the training recording
- Generate a structured summary with key takeaways and action items

## Real Results: What Automating Onboarding Looks Like

Imagine a new hire joining your company. In the old workflow:
- HR sends 15+ documents to review manually
- A 2-hour orientation session has no written summary

With AI automation:

- Expense report is categorized and approved in a day
- 30-day survey themes are surfaced for the full people team to review

The difference isn't just efficiency — it's a materially better new hire experience.

## What Not to Automate

A few things should stay human:
- **Manager 1:1s** — these are relationship-building moments, not check-the-box tasks
- **Culture introductions** — new hires need to see the humans behind the company
- **Sensitive HR conversations** — anything involving accommodations, personal situations, or concerns should stay personal

Use automation to clear the operational clutter so these moments get the attention they deserve.
