Many close delays are not caused by accounting complexity.

They happen because review packages arrive with **incomplete evidence**, inconsistent narratives, or unclear ownership.

AI can help teams standardize close package review so controllers spend less time chasing basics and more time making decisions.

## Why close package review is a bottleneck

Even disciplined teams still deal with recurring friction:

- support files attached in different formats and naming styles
- reconciliations missing key tie-outs or reviewer notes
- explanations that are technically correct but hard to scan quickly
- last-minute back-and-forth between preparers and reviewers

When this repeats across dozens of accounts, review queues pile up.

## A practical AI-assisted review workflow

1. **Define a standard package checklist by account type**
   Capture required support, explanation structure, and signoff expectations for each close area.

2. **Ingest package artifacts into one review context**
   Pull reconciliations, JE support, subledger tie-outs, and commentary into a consistent input format.

3. **Run completeness checks automatically**
   Flag missing attachments, missing reviewer fields, stale data references, and unsupported balances.

4. **Generate a concise reviewer summary**
   Draft one-page account narratives covering what changed, why it changed, and what needs attention.

5. **Score review risk and route by priority**
   Prioritize packages with high materiality, unusual movements, or unresolved exceptions.

6. **Track follow-up items to closure**
   Convert review comments into explicit action items with owner, due date, and status.

## Where to start

Start with one high-friction area, such as:

- accrual-heavy expense accounts
- intercompany clearing packages
- revenue-related reconciliations with frequent adjustments

A focused pilot makes adoption easier and gives you measurable before/after outcomes.

## Controls that keep automation safe

- **Human signoff remains required:** AI prepares, humans approve.
- **Immutable review trail:** preserve package versions, comments, and decision timestamps.
- **Materiality-aware checks:** avoid over-alerting on low-impact differences.
- **Template governance:** update required fields through controlled change management.

## What success looks like

Within 1–2 close cycles, teams usually see:

- fewer review returns due to missing documentation
- faster first-pass reviewer turnaround
- clearer handoffs between preparers and approvers
- less close-week fire drilling for controller teams

---

If reviews are slowing your close, automate the package completeness and summary layers first. That gives reviewers immediate leverage without changing your core accounting policies.
