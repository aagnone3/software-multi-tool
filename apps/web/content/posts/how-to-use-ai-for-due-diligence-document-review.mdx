# How to Use AI for Due Diligence Document Review

Due diligence — whether for M&A, investment, or vendor qualification — generates enormous document volume under time pressure. Data rooms fill with contracts, financials, employment agreements, IP assignments, compliance records, and regulatory filings.

AI tools that handle document extraction and summarization are changing the throughput economics of due diligence.

## What Makes Due Diligence Hard

The challenge isn't just volume — it's the combination of volume, variety, and time pressure. Reviewers need to:

- Extract specific terms from hundreds of contracts
- Identify risk patterns across a document set
- Prioritize where human attention goes
- Create structured summaries for deal teams and decision-makers

Manual review at this scale requires large teams working under deadline pressure — which increases both cost and error rate.

## Where AI Helps in Due Diligence

### Document Triage and Classification

AI can classify documents by type (employment agreements, customer contracts, IP assignments, regulatory filings) and route them to reviewers with the right expertise. Triage that takes days manually runs in minutes with AI.

### Key Term Extraction

For contracts, AI extracts the terms that matter for diligence: change of control provisions, assignment restrictions, exclusivity clauses, termination rights, IP ownership, governing law, liability caps.

Instead of reading every contract, reviewers work from structured extracts and focus attention on flagged items.

### Risk Pattern Identification

Across a large contract set, AI can surface patterns: which agreements have unusual indemnification language, which customer contracts have non-standard termination rights, which vendor agreements have problematic IP assignment terms.

Pattern identification at volume is something AI does well and humans do poorly.

### Summary Generation

For lengthy agreements, AI generates summaries that include: parties, term, key commercial provisions, notable risk language, and flagged deviations from standard terms. Deal teams get structured summaries instead of raw documents.

### Financial Document Extraction

AI invoice and financial document processors extract structured data from financial records: revenue figures, expense line items, outstanding liabilities, customer concentration data.

## The AI Tool Stack for Due Diligence

**Contract Analyzer** — Core extraction tool for the contract review workstream. Pulls key terms, flags risk language, and summarizes agreements at volume.

**Document Summarizer** — For regulatory filings, board minutes, and other lengthy documents where summary-first review is more efficient than full reads.

**Invoice Processor** — For financial due diligence workstreams reviewing vendor invoices and financial records.

**Meeting Summarizer** — Captures management interview notes and diligence call summaries for the deal record.

## Practical Workflow

### Phase 1: Triage
Upload document set. AI classifies documents, flags high-priority items (customer contracts with change-of-control provisions, IP assignments, regulatory correspondence), and builds the review queue.

### Phase 2: Extraction
AI extracts key terms from priority document types. Reviewers get structured extracts instead of raw documents. Review time shifts from extraction to analysis.

### Phase 3: Pattern Analysis
Use extracted data to identify patterns across the document set. Surface concentration risks, unusual term clusters, and flagged language for escalation.

### Phase 4: Human Review and Judgment
Human reviewers focus on flagged items, complex agreements, and judgment calls. AI handled the extraction layer.

### Phase 5: Summary Reporting
AI-generated summaries plus reviewer notes create the diligence output package.

## What AI Won't Do

AI doesn't provide legal advice, assess business risk, or make deal recommendations. It removes the document processing layer so deal teams and legal reviewers focus on what requires judgment.

The quality of AI-assisted due diligence depends on having clear extraction targets and a well-defined exception review process. AI triage without human follow-up on flagged items doesn't improve outcomes.

## ROI in Practice

Teams using AI for due diligence report:

- 40-60% reduction in first-pass document review time
- More consistent coverage across large document sets
- Faster turnaround on initial risk summaries
- Ability to handle higher deal volume with the same team

The time savings compound in competitive processes where faster review translates to faster decisions.
