Preparer-reviewer handoffs are where many close timelines quietly slip.

Work is technically done, but not review-ready. Reviewers ask for context that should already be attached. Files bounce back and forth, and teams lose hours in avoidable backtracking.

AI can make handoffs structured, fast, and easier to audit.

## Why handoffs break during close

Most handoff delays come from a few recurring issues:

- Workpapers don’t follow a consistent completion standard
- Review notes are scattered across chat, email, and spreadsheets
- Supporting evidence isn’t linked to each conclusion
- Ownership for open items is unclear after first review

When those gaps stack up across many close tasks, timelines drift.

## What a strong handoff process should produce

A high-quality preparer-reviewer handoff should include:

1. **A clear task summary** with scope and period covered
2. **Linked supporting evidence** for key balances and adjustments
3. **Structured commentary** on judgments and unusual items
4. **A clean open-items list** with owner and due date

If those four outputs are present every cycle, review time drops and rework falls.

## Where AI helps most

### 1) Readiness checks before submission

AI can evaluate whether required sections are complete before a preparer marks work as ready for review.

### 2) Standardized reviewer packets

AI can assemble a concise packet: what changed, why it changed, what evidence supports it, and what still needs attention.

### 3) Open-item extraction

From reviewer notes, AI can extract action items, owners, and deadlines into a standardized tracker.

### 4) Risk prioritization

AI can flag handoffs with elevated risk signals—large adjustments, weak support linkage, or unresolved prior-period items.

## A practical implementation pattern

Use a 5-step handoff workflow each close cycle:

1. **Complete** preparer checklist and attach support
2. **Validate** package completeness with AI readiness rules
3. **Generate** reviewer summary + issue list
4. **Route** to reviewer with SLA-based priority
5. **Track** revisions until final signoff

Start with one high-volume area (for example, accruals or prepaid reconciliations) before rolling out broadly.

## Guardrails for audit defensibility

To keep this control-safe:

- Require human signoff for all material judgments
- Preserve version history for each handoff package
- Keep source links for each major conclusion
- Enforce explicit closure of all review notes before completion

AI should speed execution, not replace accountability.

## Metrics to track over 2–3 close cycles

- Median review turnaround time per task
- Number of reviewer follow-up requests per handoff
- Percentage of handoffs accepted on first pass
- Late close delays caused by unresolved review comments

These metrics show whether the workflow is truly improving.

## Final takeaway

Automating close preparer-reviewer handoffs with AI reduces friction where close timelines most often break.

With clear standards, structured summaries, and strong ownership tracking, finance teams can review faster without weakening control quality.
