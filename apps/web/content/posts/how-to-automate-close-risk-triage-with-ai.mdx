Most close teams do not fail because they miss all risks.

They fail because they treat **every exception like the same priority**.

AI helps by triaging close risks early and routing the highest-impact items to the right owners before they become end-of-cycle fire drills.

## Why close risk triage breaks down

During close, the queue fills with issues from multiple systems and teams:

- unreconciled balances
- unusual period-over-period movements
- journal entries missing support
- unresolved intercompany mismatches
- late adjustments from upstream teams

Without consistent triage, low-impact noise competes with genuinely material risks.

## A practical AI-assisted close risk triage workflow

1. **Define a risk scoring model for close exceptions**
   Combine amount, account criticality, aging, ownership, and prior recurrence into one score.

2. **Normalize exception data across sources**
   Pull issues from reconciliations, JE review queues, subledger controls, and variance checks into a shared schema.

3. **Classify exception type automatically**
   Tag each issue as documentation gap, timing difference, policy breach, calculation anomaly, or upstream dependency.

4. **Prioritize by business impact, not queue order**
   Route high-score exceptions first to senior reviewers and controllers.

5. **Draft resolution guidance for owners**
   Generate concise next-step recommendations with required evidence and approval path.

6. **Escalate aging high-risk items before close cutoff**
   Trigger reminders and escalation when critical issues remain unresolved near signoff windows.

## Where to start first

Start with one close stream where delays are common, such as:

- balance sheet reconciliation exceptions
- large manual journal entries
- intercompany differences

A narrow rollout creates measurable cycle-time gains without redesigning your whole close process.

## Controls to keep triage trustworthy

- **Human approval remains mandatory:** AI scores and routes; people decide.
- **Transparent scoring:** each risk score shows contributing factors.
- **Materiality thresholds:** prevent over-escalation from small-value noise.
- **Audit-ready logs:** retain triage inputs, score history, decisions, and timestamps.

## What success looks like

After 1–2 close cycles, teams usually see:

- earlier visibility into high-risk close blockers
- faster issue ownership and resolution
- fewer last-day surprises before signoff
- more predictable close timelines month over month

---

If close pressure is rising late in each cycle, start by automating risk scoring and escalation for one high-friction exception queue. That usually creates fast, visible wins.
