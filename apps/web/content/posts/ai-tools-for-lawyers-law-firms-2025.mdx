Legal work is document-heavy by design. Contracts, case files, discovery documents, client correspondence — the volume is enormous. AI is changing how law firms handle that volume.

## What AI Can Do for Law Firms

### Contract Review and Analysis

Reviewing contracts manually is one of the most time-consuming parts of legal practice. AI contract analysis tools can:

- Extract key terms, dates, and obligations automatically
- Flag unusual or non-standard clauses
- Compare contract versions to spot changes
- Identify missing provisions based on contract type

A contract that takes 2 hours to review manually can be summarized in minutes. Lawyers still need to interpret and advise — but the initial extraction and flagging work shifts to AI.

### Document Summarization

Discovery produces thousands of pages. Due diligence requires reviewing dozens of agreements. AI document summarization condenses:

- Long contracts into key-point summaries
- Case documents into timeline-ready facts
- Research memos into actionable recommendations
- Client documents into onboarding briefs

### Invoice Processing

Law firm billing is complex. AI invoice processing can:

- Extract line items, dates, and amounts from vendor invoices
- Categorize expenses for client billing
- Flag billing code inconsistencies
- Automate accounts payable workflows

## The Business Case

Mid-size law firms typically have 2-4 associates spending significant time on document review and administrative tasks. At $200-400/hour associate billing rates, shifting even 20% of that time to AI-assisted workflows creates substantial capacity.

## Tools That Work for Legal

**Contract analysis:** AI contract review tools that extract obligations, parties, and key terms without requiring legal training to operate.

**Meeting summarization:** After client calls and depositions, automatic transcription and summarization so you have accurate records without manual note-taking.

**News monitoring:** Track case-relevant regulatory changes, industry news, and legal developments automatically.

## Implementation Considerations

Law firms have specific requirements:

**Confidentiality:** Client documents must stay confidential. Verify that any AI tool you use doesn't train on your data and offers appropriate data processing agreements.

**Accuracy requirements:** Legal work has zero tolerance for material errors. Use AI for extraction and summarization, with lawyer review before any output is used professionally.

**Workflow integration:** The best AI tools fit into existing workflows rather than requiring complete process changes.

## Getting Started

The practical starting point for most law firms is document summarization. It's low-risk (the output is a summary, not an action), immediately useful, and builds confidence in AI-assisted workflows before moving to higher-stakes applications like contract review.
