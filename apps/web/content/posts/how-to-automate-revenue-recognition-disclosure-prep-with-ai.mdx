If your team dreads revenue recognition disclosure season, you're not alone.

The work is usually spread across contracts, billing systems, spreadsheets, and hand-written notes. By the time disclosures are drafted, reviewed, and revised, your team has burned days that could have gone into analysis.

AI won’t replace judgment on technical accounting, but it can remove a lot of repetitive prep work.

## Where disclosure prep usually breaks down

Most delays happen in the same places:

- pulling contract and amendment terms from multiple files
- reconciling performance obligations with billing milestones
- collecting period-over-period movement details
- drafting narrative explanations from raw numbers
- fixing formatting and consistency issues late in the cycle

These are exactly the kinds of tasks AI is useful for: extraction, structuring, summarization, and consistency checks.

## A practical AI-assisted workflow

A simple workflow that works for most finance teams:

1. **Collect source documents**
   Upload contracts, amendments, billing schedules, and prior disclosures.

2. **Extract key terms**
   Use AI to pull effective dates, obligations, pricing structure, and recognition triggers into a structured table.

3. **Summarize changes this period**
   Have AI compare current-period terms and balances to prior period and surface meaningful deltas.

4. **Generate first-draft disclosure text**
   Produce draft narrative sections from the structured output (not from raw documents).

5. **Run consistency checks**
   Validate references, totals, and terminology against your source data.

6. **Finalize with reviewer oversight**
   Keep accounting leadership responsible for policy interpretation and final sign-off.

## What to automate first

Start with the highest-friction repetitive steps:

- contract clause extraction for revenue terms
- movement summary tables for deferred/unbilled balances
- draft narrative for "what changed this quarter"
- review checklists for disclosure consistency

These usually cut preparation time quickly without changing control ownership.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Skipping source traceability:** every AI output should map back to source files.
- **Mixing policy decisions with draft generation:** AI drafts, people decide.
- **Automating everything at once:** start with one disclosure section and expand.
- **No review rubric:** define what reviewers must validate before approval.

## What good looks like

A strong implementation gives you:

- faster first drafts
- cleaner tie-outs across tables and narrative
- fewer late-cycle rewrites
- more time for actual technical review

That’s the point: less manual assembly, more informed accounting judgment.

---

If your team is preparing revenue recognition disclosures manually each period, start by automating extraction and first-draft assembly. You’ll feel the time savings almost immediately.
