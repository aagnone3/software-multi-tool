Journal entry review is one of the highest-friction steps in the close process.

Teams need to move fast, but every rushed review increases risk: miscoded entries, unsupported adjustments, duplicate accruals, and approval gaps that only surface later during audit prep.

AI can reduce that pressure by handling first-pass screening and exception prioritization, so reviewers spend time on *judgment* instead of repetitive checks.

## Why manual JE review breaks at scale

As transaction volume grows, manual review starts to fail in predictable ways:

- Review queues become too large near cutoff dates
- High-risk entries look identical to low-risk entries in spreadsheets
- Support docs are scattered across systems and emails
- Review comments are inconsistent and hard to audit
- Senior reviewers become bottlenecks for routine approvals

The issue is rarely policy design. It is operational throughput.

## What AI should do in a JE review workflow

A strong AI-assisted review layer should:

1. **Classify entries by risk** (materiality, unusual account combinations, timing, description anomalies).
2. **Check required fields and supporting links** before review assignment.
3. **Flag policy exceptions** using rule + pattern detection.
4. **Generate concise reviewer summaries** with why an entry was flagged.
5. **Route entries to the right reviewer tier** based on risk score.

What AI should *not* do automatically:

- Final approval/sign-off for high-risk entries
- Policy overrides
- Posting corrections without human authorization

Keep accountability with finance leadership.

## A practical rollout plan

### Phase 1: Standardize JE metadata

Before automation, define required metadata:

- preparer
- approver
- entry type (accrual, reclass, correction)
- linked support document(s)
- business rationale

Without this foundation, AI scoring will be noisy.

### Phase 2: Add automated pre-review checks

Run every new entry through a pre-review gate:

- missing support docs
- unusual debit/credit pairings
- amount variance vs historical ranges
- late-posting outside expected windows

Entries that fail checks are tagged before human review starts.

### Phase 3: Add reviewer copilots

For each flagged entry, AI drafts:

- risk summary
- likely reason for flag
- suggested follow-up questions

Reviewers can accept/edit these notes and keep a cleaner audit trail.

## Metrics to track after rollout

Use measurable outcomes, not just anecdotal speed:

- Median JE review time
- % entries auto-cleared as low risk
- % high-risk entries reviewed within SLA
- Number of post-close reclass/correction entries
- Audit findings tied to JE review controls

If review time drops while post-close corrections also drop, the automation is working.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Scoring risk without finance-policy input
- Deploying one global threshold across all entities/teams
- Ignoring reviewer feedback loops (model never improves)
- Treating AI output as final decision instead of triage support

AI is best used as a prioritization engine, not a compliance shortcut.

## Final takeaway

Journal entry review does not have to be a last-mile fire drill every close.

Use AI to triage risk, enforce metadata completeness, and surface the right context to the right reviewer at the right time. Keep humans in control of approvals and policy calls.

That is how you speed up close *and* strengthen control quality.
