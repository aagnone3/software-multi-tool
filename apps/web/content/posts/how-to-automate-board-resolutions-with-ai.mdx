# How to Automate Board Resolutions and Corporate Minutes with AI

Corporate secretaries and legal teams spend significant time managing board governance documents — drafting resolutions, taking and transcribing meeting minutes, and extracting specific authorizations from years of board records. AI tools are now capable of automating much of this work.

## Why Board Document Management Is Ripe for AI

Board governance documents have several characteristics that make them well-suited for AI automation:

- **Highly structured format** — resolutions follow predictable templates; minutes have consistent sections (call to order, quorum, old business, new business, adjournment)
- **Repetitive content** — many resolutions are routine (officer authorization, banking resolutions, equity grants) and follow standard language
- **High search value** — companies frequently need to find specific authorizations buried in years of board records
- **Transcription-heavy** — in-person or remote board meetings need accurate transcription before minutes can be drafted

## Use Cases for AI in Board Document Management

### Meeting Transcription

Recording and transcribing board meetings is a prerequisite for accurate minutes. AI transcription tools can convert meeting recordings into text in minutes — including speaker identification when multiple voices are present.

The output feeds directly into the minutes drafting process, replacing hours of manual note-taking.

### Minutes Drafting

AI tools trained on corporate document language can take a meeting transcript or agenda and draft initial minutes in standard corporate format. The corporate secretary reviews and refines rather than writing from scratch.

A typical workflow:
1. Record the meeting (audio or video)
2. Run through AI transcription with speaker separation
3. Paste transcript into AI drafting tool with meeting agenda
4. Review and edit AI-drafted minutes
5. Circulate for approval

This compresses a process that might take 2-3 hours into 30-45 minutes.

### Resolution Drafting

Standard board resolutions — banking resolutions, officer authorization, equity grant resolutions, contract approval — follow known templates. AI can generate first drafts based on the type of resolution needed and the specific parameters (amount, party names, effective date).

### Historical Record Search

One of the highest-value AI use cases for corporate secretaries is searching historical board records. When someone asks "do we have board authorization to open an account with this bank?" or "when did the board approve the current officer compensation?" — AI can surface the relevant resolution from years of records in seconds rather than hours.

### Document Extraction for Due Diligence

M&A due diligence requires reviewing board minutes and resolutions from target companies to verify:
- Corporate authorization for major transactions
- Equity issuance history
- Material contracts and commitments approved
- Change of control provisions addressed

AI document extraction tools can scan hundreds of pages of board records and extract this information in structured format — dramatically accelerating due diligence timelines.

## Setting Up an AI-Assisted Minutes Process

**Step 1: Standardize your templates**

Before automating, ensure you have clean standard templates for minutes and common resolutions. AI works best when it has a clear format target.

**Step 2: Choose a transcription tool**

For meetings with multiple speakers, a speaker-separation transcription tool provides the most useful output — identifying who said what rather than producing an undifferentiated transcript.

**Step 3: Establish a review protocol**

AI-generated board documents need legal review before they become official records. Establish a clear protocol for who reviews AI drafts and what standards they apply.

**Step 4: Build a searchable archive**

If your historical board records aren't already in searchable format, a document conversion project (scanning physical documents, extracting text from legacy PDFs) is a prerequisite for AI-powered historical search.

## Quality and Legal Considerations

Board resolutions and minutes are legal documents. A few important considerations when using AI:

**Always have counsel review** — AI drafts should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before execution, especially for complex authorizations.

**Preserve the original record** — the signed, executed resolution or approved minutes are the official record. AI tools assist in drafting; they don't replace the formal approval process.

**Confidentiality** — board discussions often include sensitive strategic information. Use AI tools with strong confidentiality and data security practices.

**Jurisdiction-specific requirements** — corporate governance requirements vary by state and country. Ensure AI-drafted documents meet applicable formality requirements.

## Practical Impact

Corporate secretaries who have integrated AI tools for board document management typically report:

- **Minutes drafting time reduced by 60-70%** — from 2-3 hours to under an hour
- **Historical search time reduced by 90%+** — from hours of manual file review to seconds
- **Fewer errors** — AI consistently applies proper formatting and required legal language
- **Faster turnaround** — boards can receive approved minutes sooner after meetings

## Getting Started

The easiest entry point is using AI for meeting transcription. The next time you have a board meeting, record it and run the audio through an AI transcription tool with speaker separation. Compare the output to what you would have produced through manual note-taking — you'll immediately see the time savings.

From there, use the transcript to draft minutes with AI assistance, and you'll have a sustainable workflow for ongoing board governance support.
