Venture capital firms evaluate hundreds of deals per year. AI tools are becoming essential for processing deal flow at scale without proportionally scaling the team.

## The VC Document Problem

A mid-size VC firm processing 500 deals/year handles:
- 500+ pitch decks (40-80 pages each)
- Hundreds of one-pagers and exec summaries
- Data room documents for portfolio companies
- LP reports and quarterly updates
- Industry research and market reports

Manual review of this volume is impossible. Something gets missed.

## AI Use Cases in VC

### 1. Pitch Deck Analysis

AI can extract key information from pitch decks: market size claims, team backgrounds, traction metrics, competitive positioning, funding ask. A structured summary takes seconds instead of 20 minutes.

### 2. Due Diligence Document Review

Data rooms contain hundreds of documents. AI can review contracts, financial statements, and corporate records, flagging unusual terms or missing information before human lawyers spend billable hours on the same task.

### 3. LP Report Generation

Quarterly LP reports require synthesizing data from multiple portfolio companies. AI can process company updates and generate narrative summaries for LP communications.

### 4. Market Research Synthesis

Competitive landscape research involves reading dozens of articles, reports, and filings. AI summarization reduces a day of research to an hour.

### 5. Meeting Notes from Founder Calls

AI transcription of founder calls creates structured notes capturing product updates, risks raised, commitments made, and follow-up items — without the analyst scrambling to take notes during the call.

## Implementation by Fund Size

**Solo GP / Micro-VC:**
Start with pitch deck summarization. Immediate ROI on first-pass filtering.

**Small Fund ($50-100M):**
Add meeting transcription and LP update drafts.

**Mid-Size Fund ($100M+):**
Full document workflow automation including data room review assistance.

## What to Look For in AI Tools

- Multi-document processing (batch multiple decks at once)
- Consistent output format for easy comparison across deals
- Support for financial documents (not just plain text)
- Audit trail for what was analyzed when
- Integration with existing deal tracking systems
