Legal operations is the discipline of running a law department like a business — managing outside counsel, controlling costs, improving processes, and using technology to scale legal capacity without proportional headcount growth.

AI is becoming a core tool for legal ops teams in 2025. Here's what's actually working.

## Contract Review and Abstraction

Contract review is one of the highest-volume, most automatable tasks in a legal department. Legal ops teams are using AI to:

- **Abstract key terms** from NDAs, MSAs, SOWs, and vendor contracts (parties, effective date, term, governing law, termination rights, liability caps)
- **Flag non-standard clauses** that deviate from the company's standard playbook
- **Identify renewal and expiration dates** across a large contract portfolio
- **Summarize long agreements** for business stakeholders who need the key points without reading the full document

A legal ops team managing 500+ vendor contracts annually can save hundreds of hours per year with AI-assisted abstraction, even with human review for complex agreements.

## Matter Intake and Routing

Intake is often the most manual step in legal ops: someone submits a request, it arrives in email or a shared inbox, and someone manually categorizes it, assigns it, and logs it in a matter management system.

AI intake tools can:

- Read incoming requests and classify them by matter type (employment, commercial contract, IP, regulatory)
- Route to the right attorney or paralegal based on practice area
- Extract key details (business unit, urgency, outside counsel needed y/n) for the matter record
- Generate acknowledgment emails with expected response times

This reduces intake processing time from hours to minutes.

## Meeting Summaries for Legal Holds and Privilege Logs

Legal holds and privilege documentation require accurate records of when decisions were made, who was involved, and what was discussed. AI meeting summarization tools can:

- Generate structured summaries of legal team meetings with action items and decision log
- Identify when legal hold discussions occurred and what documents were in scope
- Create privilege log entries with attorney participants, subject matter, and date

These records have evidentiary value in litigation, so accuracy matters — but AI-generated first drafts that humans review are still faster than manual documentation.

## Outside Counsel Invoice Review

Outside counsel billing is a major cost center in legal ops. AI tools can:

- Extract billing entries from law firm invoices (timekeeper, date, hours, rate, narrative)
- Flag billing guideline violations (block billing, vague descriptions, non-reimbursable expenses)
- Calculate effective rates and identify patterns of overbilling
- Compare invoices against matter budgets

Legal bill review software exists for large enterprises, but smaller legal ops teams can start with general AI document processing tools for basic invoice analysis.

## Practical Tool Stack for a Lean Legal Ops Team

You don't need an enterprise legal tech platform to start. A practical starting stack:

1. **AI document processor** — for contract abstraction and invoice extraction
2. **AI meeting summarizer** — for legal team meetings and board presentations
3. **AI expense processor** — for outside counsel disbursements and legal team T&E
4. **Matter management spreadsheet or simple system** — for tracking open matters

## What AI Doesn't Replace in Legal Ops

- Legal judgment and risk assessment
- Attorney-client privilege decisions
- Outside counsel relationship management
- Regulatory strategy and compliance design

Legal ops is about making legal work more efficient. AI handles the document processing, routing, and data extraction. The judgment stays with humans.

## Getting Started

The fastest wins for legal ops teams new to AI:

1. Pick the highest-volume document type (usually NDAs or vendor contracts)
2. Use AI to extract a standard set of key terms for the next 20 contracts
3. Compare results against what your team would have extracted manually
4. If accuracy is acceptable, scale to your full incoming volume

Most teams find accuracy sufficient for first-pass extraction within a few months, with human review focused on exceptions and complex agreements.
