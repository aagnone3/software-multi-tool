Journal entries are not usually blocked by posting mechanics. They are blocked by support.

People spend hours gathering source files, screenshots, calculations, and approval notes into one reviewable packet. Then they do it again when reviewers or auditors ask follow-up questions.

AI can automate most of that assembly work so your team spends time on judgment, not document hunting.

## Why support packets create close delays

In many teams, support is scattered across systems:

- invoices and contracts in shared drives
- calculations in spreadsheets
- explanations in chat threads
- approvals in email
- policy references in separate docs

Even when the entry is correct, the evidence is fragmented.

## A practical AI-assisted workflow

1. **Define packet requirements by entry type**
   Create templates for accruals, reclasses, amortization, reserves, and intercompany entries so required evidence is explicit.

2. **Pull source artifacts automatically**
   Use AI + workflow rules to collect referenced files, transaction lists, and policy links based on entry metadata.

3. **Generate a clear support narrative**
   Draft a concise explanation: what the entry does, why it is needed this period, and how the amount was calculated.

4. **Validate internal consistency**
   Check that the support ties to the JE amount, period, entities, and accounts before sending to reviewers.

5. **Package for review and audit reuse**
   Produce a standard packet with evidence links, owner, reviewer, timestamp, and version history so the same packet can be reused in audit requests.

6. **Route exceptions back to preparers**
   If data is missing or mismatched, route the packet back with specific gaps rather than generic rejection comments.

## What to automate first

Start with high-volume entries that are repetitive but still review-sensitive:

- monthly accruals
- prepaid amortization
- fixed asset depreciation adjustments
- recurring intercompany true-ups
- payroll reallocations

These typically deliver fast cycle-time wins without changing accounting policy.

## Guardrails that keep quality high

- **No blind posting:** AI prepares packets; humans approve entries.
- **Traceability by default:** every narrative sentence should map to source evidence.
- **Template governance:** finance leadership owns required support fields by entry type.
- **Immutable review trail:** track who edited what and when.

## What success looks like

Teams usually see measurable improvements within one or two close cycles:

- shorter JE review turnaround
- fewer reviewer clarification loops
- faster audit sample response times
- more consistent support quality across preparers

---

If your close still depends on manual packet assembly, start with one recurring entry type and automate only support collection plus first-draft narratives. That narrow rollout is usually enough to prove value quickly.
