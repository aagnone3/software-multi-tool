Customer onboarding is one of the most document-intensive processes in any business. Contracts, welcome calls, training sessions, configuration reviews, kickoff meetings — each touchpoint generates documents and information that needs to be processed, organized, and acted on.

Most of this processing is manual, repetitive, and slow. AI tools are changing that for the document-heavy parts of onboarding.

## The Document Problem in Customer Onboarding

A typical enterprise customer onboarding might involve:

- Reviewing and executing a contract (often 20-50 pages)
- Signing an MSA, SOW, or SLA
- Running a kickoff call and distributing notes
- Conducting discovery interviews and documenting requirements
- Creating implementation guides and documentation
- Running training sessions and producing summaries

Most teams have one or two people managing these documents manually. The bottleneck isn't the relationship work — it's the administrative overhead.

## Where AI Helps in Onboarding

### Contract and Agreement Review

When a new customer is about to sign, someone needs to review the final contract to confirm it matches what was negotiated. AI contract analysis tools can quickly extract:

- Key commercial terms (pricing, payment terms, renewal)
- Service commitments and SLAs
- Data handling requirements
- Termination and exit provisions
- Any non-standard clauses that need attention

This isn't a replacement for legal review, but it gives the customer success team a quick summary before the contract is executed and helps catch issues before they become problems.

### Kickoff Call Notes and Action Items

The kickoff call is where success often starts or ends. Getting the notes right — capturing what was promised, what was agreed, what the customer's priorities are — matters a lot.

AI meeting summarizers can transcribe and summarize kickoff calls automatically. The output typically includes:
- Key customer priorities and success metrics
- Technical requirements and configuration decisions
- Action items with owners and timelines
- Open questions that need follow-up

This turns a 60-minute kickoff into a 5-minute review and distribution of AI-generated notes.

### Discovery and Requirements Documentation

Discovery sessions generate a lot of information that needs to be organized into requirements documents or implementation plans. AI tools can take meeting transcripts or recorded sessions and produce structured documentation drafts that are then edited by the account manager.

### Training Session Summaries

When you run training sessions for new customers, AI tools can summarize the session and produce a written guide that participants can reference later. This reduces the burden on training staff to produce documentation separately from delivering training.

## Practical Workflow for AI-Assisted Onboarding

### Before Contract Execution

1. Upload the final contract to an AI analyzer
2. Extract key commercial terms and any non-standard clauses
3. Confirm against what was negotiated
4. Flag any discrepancies before execution

Time saved: 1-2 hours per contract.

### After the Kickoff Call

1. Record the kickoff call (with customer permission)
2. Upload to an AI meeting summarizer
3. Review and edit the generated summary
4. Distribute to all stakeholders within hours of the call

Time saved: 30-60 minutes per kickoff.

### During Implementation

1. Record discovery sessions and design reviews
2. Use AI summaries to draft requirements documents
3. Have the account team edit for accuracy and completeness
4. Maintain living documentation that's updated after each session

Time saved: 2-4 hours per documentation artifact.

## What AI Doesn't Replace

**Relationship judgment**: AI can document what customers say, but understanding what they actually need — which is often different — requires human judgment and experience.

**Escalation decisions**: When something goes wrong during onboarding, AI tools don't help with deciding how to respond, who to involve, or how to manage the customer relationship.

**Configuration and technical work**: AI tools help with documentation of technical decisions but don't do the actual implementation work.

**Customization decisions**: Understanding which product features actually solve the customer's problem requires product knowledge and customer understanding that AI tools don't have.

## Getting Started

The easiest entry point is kickoff call summarization. For your next kickoff, record the call and run it through an AI meeting summarizer. Compare the output to what you would have written. If it's close, add this to your standard onboarding process.

From there, try AI contract analysis on the next customer contract that comes in. See what the tool surfaces and whether it catches things you might have missed or took time to find manually.

The ROI here is fastest for teams running high volumes of onboarding — if you're onboarding dozens of customers per month, even small time savings per customer add up quickly.
