Reconciliations are often completed on time.

Sign-offs are where close timelines slip.

AI can help route reconciliations to the right reviewers faster, surface high-risk items earlier, and keep an auditable trail without adding more manual coordination.

## Why sign-off routing breaks in close

Most teams run into the same patterns:

1. Reconciliations are assigned by habit instead of risk
2. Reviewer queues become uneven near close deadlines
3. Escalations depend on ad hoc Slack or email pings
4. Control evidence is scattered across files and comments

That combination creates late approvals and weak audit traceability.

## Build an AI-assisted sign-off routing model

### 1) Define deterministic routing rules first

Start with explicit policy:

- Which reconciliations need one vs. two-level review
- Materiality thresholds and risk categories
- Required reviewer roles by account type
- SLA targets by close day and risk tier

AI should execute policy consistently, not replace governance.

### 2) Enrich each reconciliation with risk context

Before assigning, AI should attach structured signals:

- Balance change vs. prior period
- Count and age of open reconciling items
- Manual journal activity tied to the account
- Prior-period exceptions or repeat issues

This gives routing decisions useful context.

### 3) Route by capacity and skill, not just owner

A practical routing strategy:

- Keep normal items with default owners
- Send high-risk items to designated senior reviewers
- Balance workloads dynamically as queues grow
- Trigger fallback reviewers when SLA windows tighten

This avoids last-day sign-off pileups.

### 4) Automate escalation when SLA risk appears

Escalation should be automatic when:

- Item idle time exceeds threshold
- Reviewer queue exceeds capacity target
- Deadline proximity increases risk of miss

Escalations should include account risk summary, missing evidence, and required next action.

### 5) Preserve complete control evidence

Each routed reconciliation should store:

- Initial assignment and rationale
- Reassignment/escalation events with timestamps
- Reviewer comments and sign-off decisions
- Linked support artifacts and exceptions

That audit trail is often as valuable as the speed gain.

## Example sign-off routing record

- **Account:** Prepaid expenses
- **Risk tier:** Medium
- **Initial route:** Staff reviewer
- **Escalation trigger:** SLA threshold hit at T-10 hours
- **Escalated route:** Senior reviewer
- **Outcome:** Approved with exception note and follow-up task

## Common rollout mistakes

- Routing all reconciliations with the same priority
- Ignoring reviewer capacity constraints
- Escalating without clear ownership handoff
- Measuring close speed but not review quality

Routing automation should improve control outcomes, not just task throughput.

## KPIs to track

- Median sign-off turnaround time
- SLA breach rate by risk tier
- Escalation volume and resolution time
- Repeat exceptions on previously signed accounts

These metrics show whether routing quality is improving over time.

## Final takeaway

AI-assisted reconciliation sign-off routing works when policy rules are clear, risk context is structured, and escalation is automatic.

Start with one close cycle, measure bottlenecks, then expand across account groups.
