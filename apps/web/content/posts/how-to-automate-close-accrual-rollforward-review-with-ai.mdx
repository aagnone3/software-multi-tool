Accrual rollforwards are one of the most important close controls and one of the easiest to rush.

When teams are under deadline pressure, they often validate only ending balances, not movement quality. That leaves hidden issues behind: stale accruals, missing reversals, duplicated entries, and unexplained swings.

AI can automate the first-pass review so your team spends time on real exceptions instead of manual tie-outs.

## Why accrual rollforward reviews break down

Manual reviews usually fail in predictable ways:

1. **Aging accruals stay open too long** because no one flags them consistently.
2. **Reversal and rebook patterns drift** across teams or entities.
3. **Variance explanations are inconsistent** and hard to audit later.

Most of this is not a judgment problem. It is a workflow consistency problem.

## What AI should check in every rollforward

A practical AI review should run the same logic every close period.

### 1) Movement integrity

Validate opening balance + activity = ending balance, with checks for:

- missing expected movement categories
- duplicate movement lines
- sign/amount anomalies
- unexpected account/entity combinations

### 2) Aging and stale-item risk

Flag accruals that exceed expected age windows and classify by risk:

- amount materiality
- account sensitivity
- repeated carry-forward behavior
- proximity to reporting milestones

### 3) Reversal and rebook consistency

Detect broken patterns in:

- reversal timing
- rebook completeness
- partial reversal behavior
- description/reference consistency

### 4) Variance narrative quality

Generate draft commentary for reviewer approval:

- what changed
- why it likely changed
- what evidence supports that conclusion
- what action is required

This creates a clean audit trail without extra manual writing.

## Implementation pattern that works

Use a lightweight control loop:

1. Export accrual movement data each close day.
2. Define account-level policy rules and thresholds.
3. Run AI classification and exception scoring.
4. Route high-risk items first to reviewers.
5. Capture reviewer decisions to improve future triage.

Keep deterministic controls for hard policy checks. Use AI for prioritization, summarization, and exception packaging.

## Metrics to track after rollout

Measure outcomes, not activity:

- accrual exceptions per close
- unresolved aged accrual count
- time-to-clear high-risk exceptions
- post-close adjustments linked to accrual errors
- reviewer hours spent on low-risk items

If low-risk review time drops while control quality improves, the workflow is working.

- **Document Analyzer** to extract and normalize close policy rules
- **Meeting Summarizer** to capture close review decisions and owners
- **News Analyzer** to track accounting guidance changes affecting accrual treatment

## Final takeaway

Accrual rollforward review is too important to run as a last-minute spreadsheet exercise.

AI gives teams a repeatable way to surface meaningful exceptions, prioritize risk, and improve close reliability without adding headcount.
