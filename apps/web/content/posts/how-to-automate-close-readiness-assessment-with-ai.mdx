Most close delays are visible before close starts.

The problem is that teams spot those signals too late because readiness checks are manual, inconsistent, or scattered across systems.

AI can automate close readiness assessment so blockers are flagged early — before the deadline pressure hits.

## Why close readiness is often judged too late

In many teams, readiness gets reviewed through status meetings and spreadsheet updates.

That usually means:

- dependencies are marked complete but evidence is missing
- high-risk accounts look "green" until late adjustments appear
- unresolved prior-period issues are not carried forward clearly
- cross-team blockers are discovered after close has already started

By then, every issue is more expensive to fix.

## A practical AI workflow for readiness assessment

1. **Define what "ready" means by close area**
   Create explicit readiness criteria for reconciliations, accruals, revenue, intercompany, and disclosure prep.

2. **Collect readiness signals automatically**
   Pull task status, unresolved comments, aging exceptions, dependency states, and evidence completeness into one feed.

3. **Score readiness per workstream**
   Let AI classify each area as ready, at risk, or blocked based on missing artifacts, open exceptions, and dependency gaps.

4. **Generate blocker summaries with owners**
   Output a short risk summary that includes owner, due date, and specific action needed to restore readiness.

5. **Escalate based on materiality and timeline risk**
   Prioritize issues by impact to close dates and financial statement risk, not just task count.

6. **Publish a daily readiness digest**
   Send one concise report to finance leadership with trend movement (improving, flat, deteriorating) by workstream.

## Best places to pilot first

Start where missed readiness creates the most downstream churn:

- intercompany close coordination
- revenue cutoff and deferred revenue packages
- accrual-heavy cost centers
- entity-level balance sheet reconciliations

Run for 1–2 cycles and track:

- blockers identified before day 0
- percentage of tasks with complete evidence before close start
- number of late-cycle escalations
- day-by-day readiness trend accuracy

## Governance guardrails that keep this credible

- keep controller review for material risk calls
- preserve an audit trail of readiness scores and source evidence
- require documented rationale for overrides
- review recurring false positives monthly and tune rules

## What good looks like

With automated readiness assessment, teams typically get:

- fewer "surprise" blockers during close week
- clearer ownership of unresolved risks
- better prioritization of high-impact fixes
- more predictable close timelines

---

If close still starts with unclear risk visibility, AI readiness assessment is one of the fastest ways to improve execution without adding more meetings.
