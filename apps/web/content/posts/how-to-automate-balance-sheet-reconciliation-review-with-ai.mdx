# How to Automate Balance Sheet Reconciliation Review with AI

Balance sheet reconciliation review is one of the easiest places for close quality to drift.

Accounts get marked complete, but support can be inconsistent, carryforwards go unchecked, and reviewers spend too much time hunting for context. AI helps by making review criteria explicit and repeatable.

## Why reconciliation reviews often become a bottleneck

Even strong teams run into the same issues:

- reviewers get a flood of reconciliations at the same time
- supporting schedules are formatted differently across owners
- aging and unreconciled items are buried in long notes
- prior-period open items are not always carried forward clearly
- escalation decisions are delayed because risk is not surfaced early

The result is predictable: late review cycles and more close-day fire drills.

## What AI can automate in the review workflow

AI is most useful in reconciliation review when it does structured triage:

1. summarize each reconciliation in a consistent reviewer format
2. highlight unreconciled differences and aging risk indicators
3. compare current reconciliations to prior period submissions
4. detect missing support references or incomplete explanations
5. generate prioritized reviewer follow-up questions

This turns review from document-by-document scanning into exception management.

## A practical implementation pattern

### 1) Define a standard review schema

Before automation, align on required fields:

- account name and number
- ending balance and support tie-out result
- unreconciled amount
- aging bucket detail
- owner explanation and action plan

AI performs better when the source format is predictable.

### 2) Run AI-assisted exception extraction

For each reconciliation, extract:

- material unreconciled differences
- stale open items with weak commentary
- missing support or unclear references
- high-risk language ("pending", "will update", "unknown")

### 3) Produce a reviewer-ready risk queue

Generate one consolidated queue with:

- account
- risk level (high/medium/low)
- issue summary
- required action
- due date recommendation

This is where cycle-time gains usually show up first.

### 4) Add a prior-period continuity check

Use AI to compare this month vs prior month and identify:

- open items that disappeared without resolution
- new issues without root-cause commentary
- recurring reconciling items with no reduction trend

### 5) Draft escalation notes automatically

For high-risk reconciliations, auto-draft concise escalation notes for controllers or close leaders to review and send.

## Guardrails that keep automation trustworthy

- Keep materiality thresholds human-owned
- Require human sign-off on all high-risk accounts
- Preserve links to source support for each AI finding
- Log AI-generated review notes for auditability
- Reuse a stable prompt template each close cycle

## Metrics that show if this is working

Track outcomes, not activity:

- average reconciliation review time per account
- number of high-risk accounts identified before close cutoff
- unresolved reconciling items at close
- repeat exceptions month-over-month
- reviewer rework rate due to missing support

## Final take

Automating reconciliation review with AI does not replace accounting judgment. It gives reviewers a faster, cleaner way to focus on exceptions that matter.

For finance teams trying to reduce close risk without adding headcount, this is one of the highest-leverage workflows to automate.
