If you're manually entering data from invoices — vendor name, date, total, line items — into a spreadsheet or accounting software, you're doing something a computer can do in seconds.

Invoice data extraction has been a solved problem in enterprise software for years. But the enterprise tools were expensive, required IT setup, and weren't worth it for a 20-person company.

That's changed. Here's what modern AI invoice extraction looks like and how to pick the right tool.

## What Invoice Data Extraction Does

A good invoice extraction tool takes a PDF, image, or scanned invoice and outputs structured data:

- **Vendor name and address**
- **Invoice number and date**
- **Due date**
- **Line items** (description, quantity, unit price, total)
- **Subtotal, tax, and total**
- **Payment terms**

The output is structured (JSON, CSV, or direct accounting integration) so it can flow into your workflow without re-entry.

## The Manual Alternative (and Why It's Expensive)

Let's put a number on it.

An accountant or bookkeeper earning $25/hour who spends 5 minutes per invoice entering data is costing you $2.08 per invoice in labor. At 50 invoices a month, that's $104/month or $1,248/year — just for data entry.

More importantly, that's time that could go toward analysis, reconciliation, or advisory work — the high-value parts.

## What to Look for in an AI Invoice Tool

### 1. Works on messy PDFs

Not every vendor produces clean, machine-readable PDFs. Some are scans, some are image-heavy, some use non-standard formatting. The tool should handle all of these.

### 2. Extracts line items, not just totals

Many basic tools only extract the total amount. Good tools extract individual line items — which matters for job costing, expense allocation, and auditing.

### 3. Structured output

The output should be machine-readable (JSON or CSV), not a narrative summary. You need data you can import, not text you have to re-parse.

### 4. High accuracy on edge cases

Test the tool with a few difficult invoices: invoices with watermarks, multi-page invoices, invoices with tables that span pages, invoices in foreign currencies.

### 5. Handles common formats

PDF (digital and scanned), JPEG, PNG, TIFF. Most invoice extraction tools handle these; fewer handle multi-page PDFs reliably.

## Common Use Cases

**Accounts payable automation**: Extract invoice data before routing for approval. Eliminates the keying step.

**Three-way matching**: Compare invoice data against purchase orders and receiving records. AI makes the initial extraction faster.

**Expense reporting**: Process vendor invoices alongside expense reports for a complete spending picture.

**Audit prep**: Build a structured invoice archive without manual indexing.

## What AI Invoice Extraction Doesn't Do

- Make approval decisions (that's human judgment)
- Integrate directly with your accounting software out of the box (you'll need to export/import)
- Handle invoices where the amount is genuinely ambiguous (those still need review)

The goal is to eliminate the data entry step — not to eliminate human review of exceptions.
