# How to Use AI for Due Diligence: A Practical Guide for 2025

Due diligence — whether for M&A, investment, vendor selection, or partnership evaluation — involves reviewing enormous volumes of documents under time pressure. AI tools are becoming essential for deal teams that need to work faster without sacrificing thoroughness.

## What AI Can (and Can't) Do in Due Diligence

**AI can help with:**
- Rapid document summarization across large document sets
- Contract key term extraction
- News and reputation research on the target company
- Flagging unusual or missing document categories
- Creating standardized summaries for deal team review

**AI cannot replace:**
- Professional judgment on risk assessment
- Legal interpretation of complex clauses
- Strategic evaluation of business fit
- Verification of factual claims
- Final decisions on deal terms

AI accelerates the information gathering and synthesis phases. Humans make the judgment calls.

## AI-Assisted Due Diligence Workflow

### Phase 1: Document Management

When a data room is opened, the first challenge is making sense of hundreds of documents. Use AI to:

1. **Categorize documents** by type (financial, legal, operational, technical)
2. **Create an index** of what's present and what's missing
3. **Prioritize review order** based on materiality

### Phase 2: Financial Document Review

For financial due diligence, AI document summarizers can process:

- Management accounts and board packs → key financial metrics
- Audit reports → highlighted findings and qualifications
- Revenue contracts → ARR, customer concentration, churn terms
- Expense analyses → normalized EBITDA adjustments

**Time savings:** A 200-page financial data room review that takes 3 days manually can often be compressed to 1 day with AI-assisted summarization.

### Phase 3: Legal Document Review

Legal due diligence involves reviewing dozens of contracts. AI contract analysis helps extract:

- **Key commercial terms**: Payment terms, pricing, exclusivity
- **Risk flags**: Unusual liability caps, assignment restrictions, change of control provisions
- **Expiry and renewal**: Contract terms and notification requirements
- **Customer/supplier concentration**: Dependencies visible in contract volumes

### Phase 4: Commercial and Reputation Research

Before investing or acquiring, you need market intelligence on the target company. AI news analysis helps:

- Compile recent news coverage on the company and its executives
- Analyze competitive position and market trends
- Identify any reputation or litigation news
- Monitor the competitive landscape in the target's market

## Time Savings in Practice

Investment banks and PE firms using AI-assisted due diligence report:

- **40-60% faster document review** on initial pass
- **Fewer missed issues** due to systematic AI coverage
- **Better synthesis** across large document sets
- **More time for judgment calls** as less time spent on mechanical review

## Important Caveats

1. **AI summaries are not legal advice** — have qualified professionals review all material contracts
2. **AI can miss context** — unusual industry-specific terms may not be flagged appropriately
3. **AI can hallucinate** — always verify specific claims against source documents
4. **Jurisdiction matters** — contract analysis should be reviewed by local counsel
