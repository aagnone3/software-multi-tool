Service Level Agreements (SLAs) are where operational expectations become contractual obligations. But in practice, SLA terms are often hard to track because they are buried across long vendor agreements and amendments.

AI can help teams review SLAs faster and reduce missed obligations.

## Why SLA Review Is Hard

SLA documents often include:

- Multiple service tiers and exceptions
- Buried penalty language
- Renewal and notice windows hidden in legal text
- Inconsistent language across vendors

Manual review works, but it is slow and hard to scale.

## What AI Can Extract from SLAs

A solid SLA analysis workflow pulls out:

- Uptime and performance targets
- Response and resolution timelines
- Escalation paths
- Credit/penalty calculations
- Exclusions and force-majeure carve-outs
- Renewal, termination, and notice terms

With structured extraction, ops, procurement, and legal can work from the same source of truth.

## Implementation Workflow

### Step 1: Upload the Agreement Set

Include the main MSA, SLA exhibits, and addenda so AI can analyze complete obligations.

### Step 2: Extract Critical Terms

Generate a normalized table of commitments and risk flags.

### Step 3: Validate High-Risk Clauses

Have a human reviewer verify penalty and termination language before downstream use.

### Step 4: Turn Output Into Tracking

Push extracted obligations into your contract calendar or vendor scorecard process.

## What Teams Gain

- Faster SLA reviews during sourcing and renewal
- Fewer missed notice dates
- Better visibility into enforceable penalties
- Cleaner vendor performance conversations

## Final Thought

AI won't replace legal review, but it gives operations teams a practical way to make SLA analysis repeatable and scalable. If SLA obligations currently live in PDFs and memory, this is one of the fastest governance upgrades you can make.
