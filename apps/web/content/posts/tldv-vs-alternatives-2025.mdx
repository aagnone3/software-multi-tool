tl;dv is a strong choice if your main job is recording Zoom/Meet calls, bookmarking key moments, and sharing highlight reels with your team.

But if your workflow involves more than meeting notes — contracts to review, invoices to extract, expense reports to categorize — you'll quickly hit the wall of what a meeting notetaker can do.

Here's how the landscape looks in 2025.

## What tl;dv Does Well

- **Highlight clipping**: Bookmark moments during a call and share them as short clips
- **Meeting transcription**: Reliable speaker-separated transcripts from Zoom, Meet, Teams
- **CRM push**: Sync meeting notes and action items to HubSpot, Salesforce
- **Searchable library**: Find any past meeting by keyword

If you're a sales team that lives in video calls, tl;dv is purpose-built for you.

## Where tl;dv Falls Short

### 1. You're Processing Documents, Not Just Calls

tl;dv only works with recorded video meetings. If you have a PDF contract, a stack of invoices, or a CSV expense export, tl;dv can't help you.

### 2. You Need Consistent Structured Output

tl;dv produces narrative summaries. If you need to extract specific fields — vendor name, invoice total, due date, line items — you'll need a different tool.

### 3. Non-Sales Teams Have Different Needs

Legal, finance, ops, and HR teams process documents more than they record calls. A meeting notetaker isn't the right foundation for those workflows.
