Variance commentary is one of the most repetitive parts of close.

Teams pull numbers, explain swings, answer follow-up questions, then rewrite the same narratives for different stakeholders.

AI can automate close variance commentary so finance can spend less time rewriting and more time validating the business story behind the numbers.

## Why variance commentary slows close

Most teams hit the same friction points:

- **Manual narrative drafting** from multiple reports and spreadsheets
- **Inconsistent commentary quality** across cost centers and owners
- **Late explanations** that show up after management review has started
- **Weak root-cause clarity** (what changed vs why it changed)
- **Version churn** when commentary is copied between decks, docs, and email

This often creates avoidable review cycles and delays reporting sign-off.

## What AI can automate in variance commentary

### 1) First-pass narrative drafts by account and department

AI can generate draft commentary from period-over-period and budget-versus-actual deltas, including plain-language summaries tailored for non-finance readers.

### 2) Root-cause tagging

AI can classify likely drivers (volume, price, timing, one-time event, reclass, FX) so reviewers get structured explanations instead of free-text paragraphs.

### 3) Threshold-based focus

AI can prioritize only material variances above predefined thresholds, reducing noise and helping teams focus on what requires action.

### 4) Consistency checks

AI can flag commentary that conflicts with numeric trends, lacks owner/action, or duplicates prior-period text without accounting for changed results.

### 5) Multi-audience formatting

AI can transform the same core analysis into:

- executive summary bullets
- department owner action notes
- board-level narrative snippets
- audit-support commentary trails

## Practical rollout plan

1. Define a standard variance commentary template (what happened, why, owner, next step).
2. Start with one high-volume P&L area (for example, SG&A or COGS).
3. Use AI drafts as preparer starting points, not final output.
4. Add reviewer validation checks for material accounts.
5. Expand to full close package once quality metrics stabilize.

## Metrics to track

Track impact with:

- time from close data availability to commentary-ready package
- percentage of material variances with first-pass acceptable commentary
- reviewer reopen rate for unclear explanations
- number of commentary revisions before sign-off
- management report delivery timeliness

## Final takeaway

Variance commentary should explain decisions, not consume your close calendar.

AI helps finance teams generate clearer, faster, and more consistent narratives so close reporting becomes a control-strengthening process instead of a writing bottleneck.
