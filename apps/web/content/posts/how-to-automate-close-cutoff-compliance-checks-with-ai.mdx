Late entries are one of the fastest ways to derail close quality.

The issue is rarely intent. It is usually visibility.

AI can monitor transaction timing, posting patterns, and supporting documentation to surface cutoff risk early—before reviewers discover it at the worst possible moment.

## What cutoff compliance means in practice

Cutoff compliance ensures transactions are recorded in the correct accounting period based on economic activity date, not convenience.

Common risk patterns:

1. Backdated entries posted after period lock windows
2. Revenue or expense recorded in the wrong period due to late source documents
3. Manual journals posted near deadline without sufficient support
4. Reclassifications that mask timing errors instead of correcting them

AI can detect these patterns continuously and route exceptions to the right reviewer.

## Build an AI-assisted cutoff workflow in five steps

### 1) Define clear cutoff rules by transaction type

Start with explicit policy logic:

- Invoices dated after close date cannot hit the closed period without approval
- Manual journals above threshold require documented rationale
- Vendor bills received after lock must follow accrual/reversal policy

If rules are ambiguous, automation will amplify inconsistency.

### 2) Monitor event-time vs post-time variance

Track the gap between:

- Source event date
- Document received date
- Entry posted date

Large or repeated gaps are leading indicators of cutoff risk.

### 3) Score exceptions by materiality and control impact

Not every late post is equally risky. Prioritize by:

- Dollar impact
- Account sensitivity (revenue, accruals, reserves)
- Proximity to filing/reporting deadlines
- Historical correction frequency

This keeps reviewers focused on the exceptions that can materially affect reporting quality.

### 4) Route exceptions with owner + SLA

Every exception should have:

- Assigned owner
- Target resolution time
- Required evidence checklist

Without ownership and SLA, exceptions become a queue, not a control.

### 5) Preserve audit-ready evidence automatically

For each resolved exception, capture:

- Original flag reason
- Reviewer decision and rationale
- Supporting document links
- Timestamped approval trail

That reduces audit prep work and strengthens control defensibility.

## Example cutoff exception alert

- **Issue:** Manual revenue journal posted 7 hours after close cut-off
- **Amount:** $142,000
- **Risk score:** 91/100
- **Required action:** Controller approval + source contract attachment
- **SLA:** Resolve within 4 hours

## Mistakes to avoid

- Creating too many low-value alerts
- Treating all accounts with the same risk threshold
- Allowing overrides without rationale capture
- Measuring alert count instead of exception resolution quality

Cutoff automation should improve judgment quality, not just increase alert volume.

## Metrics to track each close

- Cutoff exception rate by entity/account
- Time-to-resolution for high-risk exceptions
- Percentage of exceptions resolved with complete evidence
- Post-close adjustment rate tied to timing errors

If these trends improve, your cutoff controls are getting stronger and close rework is dropping.

## Final takeaway

AI is best used as an early-warning and routing layer for cutoff compliance.

Use it to detect risk, prioritize exceptions, and preserve evidence. Keep final accounting judgment and approvals with finance leadership.
