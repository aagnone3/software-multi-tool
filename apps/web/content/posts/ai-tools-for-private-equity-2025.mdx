Private equity work is fundamentally document-intensive: reviewing data rooms, analyzing legal agreements, synthesizing management presentations, preparing investment memos, and monitoring portfolio companies. AI tools are increasingly central to PE workflows.

## Where AI Fits in Private Equity

AI tools are most valuable in PE for tasks that are high-volume, document-heavy, and time-sensitive — particularly during deal cycles when teams need to process large data rooms quickly.

Key use cases:

**Due Diligence**
- Reviewing hundreds of documents in a data room
- Summarizing financial statements and projections
- Identifying key terms and risks in legal agreements
- Extracting comparable metrics across multiple companies

**Deal Documentation**
- Reviewing purchase agreements, representations, and warranties
- Summarizing legal memos and opinions
- Analyzing financing documents

**Portfolio Monitoring**
- Summarizing portfolio company board packages
- Extracting KPIs from monthly management reports
- Synthesizing advisor and consultant reports

**Investment Memos**
- Organizing and summarizing research
- Synthesizing management interview notes
- Creating first-draft narrative sections

## Due Diligence Workflow with AI

A typical AI-assisted due diligence workflow for a mid-market PE deal:

**Week 1 — Initial Data Room Triage**
- Upload all data room documents to AI tools
- Run contract analyzer on all legal agreements
- Generate summaries of key financial documents
- Identify gaps and information requests

**Week 2 — Deep Analysis**
- Management interviews (record + AI summarize)
- Deep-dive on flagged contract provisions
- Synthesize advisor and consultant reports
- Cross-reference AI extractions with financial model

**Week 3 — Documentation**
- Compile AI summaries into due diligence summary
- Document flagged issues and reps/warranties implications
- Prepare investment committee materials

AI doesn't eliminate due diligence — it accelerates the document processing phase so analysts spend more time on interpretation and judgment.

## Contract Review in PE Deals

One of the highest-ROI applications for AI in PE is contract review during due diligence. Key provisions to extract:

**Customer contracts:**
- Contract term and renewal
- Revenue recognition triggers
- Change of control provisions
- Most-favored-nation clauses
- Exclusivity arrangements

**Employment agreements:**
- Retention/bonus provisions
- Non-compete and non-solicit terms
- Change of control payments
- Equity arrangements

**Supplier agreements:**
- Sole-source dependencies
- Pricing terms and escalators
- Termination provisions

## Investment Memo Drafting

Investment memos synthesize weeks of due diligence into a compelling investment case. AI tools accelerate the first draft:

1. Upload all meeting summaries, research notes, and document summaries
2. Use AI summarization to identify themes and key findings
3. Organize extractions into investment thesis structure
4. Draft narrative sections around AI-organized insights

The IC memo still requires senior judgment on the investment thesis, but the first draft production time drops significantly.

## Considerations for PE Firms

**Data security:** Due diligence documents are confidential. Evaluate AI tools' data handling policies carefully, particularly regarding whether uploaded documents are used for model training.

**Accuracy verification:** AI contract extraction should always be verified against source documents before inclusion in deal documentation or representations to LPs.

**Process documentation:** As AI becomes part of deal workflows, document your AI-assisted processes for LP reporting and regulatory compliance purposes.
