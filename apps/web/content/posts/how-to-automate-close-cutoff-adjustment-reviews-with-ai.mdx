Cutoff adjustments are one of the most error-prone parts of close.

Teams are reviewing late entries under deadline pressure, often with incomplete context on materiality, policy, and downstream impact. That creates rework, control risk, and delayed reporting.

AI can make cutoff adjustment reviews faster and more consistent.

## Why cutoff reviews break down

Close teams usually see the same bottlenecks:

- **High entry volume near deadline**
- **Inconsistent reviewer judgment across entities**
- **Weak documentation for rationale and evidence**
- **Manual tie-outs to policy and period rules**
- **Late escalation of high-risk adjustments**

These issues don’t just slow close — they increase audit friction later.

## What AI can automate in cutoff adjustment reviews

### 1) Adjustment risk scoring

AI can score each adjustment using factors like amount, account sensitivity, preparer history, and posting timing.

### 2) Policy alignment checks

AI can compare adjustment narratives against accounting policy language and flag entries that need additional support.

### 3) Reviewer worklist prioritization

Instead of first-in/first-out review, AI can rank entries by risk and materiality so reviewers focus on the highest-impact items first.

### 4) Evidence completeness validation

AI can detect missing attachments, unclear explanations, or unsupported assumptions before final approval.

### 5) Escalation and decision summaries

For high-risk items, AI can prepare concise review packets with:

- adjustment summary
- policy references
- impact estimate
- unresolved questions
- recommended approver path

## Practical rollout plan

1. Standardize adjustment metadata (source, rationale, period, approver).
2. Define risk rules and materiality thresholds for AI scoring.
3. Build prioritized reviewer queues by entity/process.
4. Add evidence completeness checks before approver routing.
5. Track post-close exceptions and tune scoring rules monthly.

## Metrics to track

Measure impact over 2-3 closes:

- median review time per adjustment
- percent of high-risk adjustments reviewed before cutoff deadline
- post-close reclassification volume
- audit review notes tied to cutoff entries
- adjustment escalations resolved within SLA

## Final takeaway

Cutoff adjustment review should be predictable, not a last-minute fire drill.

AI helps teams triage risk, enforce policy consistency, and close the period with stronger control confidence.
