# How to Automate ESG Scope 3 Category 10 (Processing of Sold Products) Reporting with AI

Scope 3 Category 10 — processing of sold products — covers emissions from downstream processing of intermediate products sold to other companies. For manufacturers selling components, chemicals, or materials that require further processing, this category can represent a significant portion of total Scope 3 emissions.

The challenge: collecting accurate downstream processing data requires coordinating with hundreds or thousands of customers, each using different processes and reporting formats.

## What Is Scope 3 Category 10?

Category 10 covers emissions that occur when the products you sell are further processed by other companies before reaching end consumers. This applies to:

- **Raw material suppliers** (steel, aluminum, chemicals)
- **Component manufacturers** (electronics parts, automotive components)
- **Intermediate goods producers** (plastic pellets, textile fibers)
- **Specialty chemical companies** (pharmaceuticals, coatings)

The GHG Protocol calculates Category 10 emissions based on: mass of products sold × emission factor for downstream processing activity.

## Why Category 10 Reporting Is Difficult

### Data Collection Challenges

1. **Downstream opacity** — You don't control what customers do with your products
2. **Process variability** — The same component might be processed very differently by different customers
3. **Emission factor gaps** — Industry-specific processing emission factors may not exist
4. **Customer reluctance** — Customers may not want to share proprietary process information

### Manual Process Costs

Without automation, Category 10 reporting typically requires:
- Customer survey design and distribution (2-3 weeks)
- Response collection and follow-up (4-6 weeks)
- Data validation and cleaning (1-2 weeks)
- Emission factor research and application (1-2 weeks)
- Report generation and review (1 week)

**Total: 9-14 weeks per reporting cycle**, with accuracy limited by response rates.

## How AI Transforms Category 10 Reporting

### 1. Intelligent Customer Segmentation

AI can analyze your customer base to prioritize engagement by emissions impact:

```
Customer Tier Analysis:
- Tier 1 (>5% of total processing emissions): Direct engagement, site visits
- Tier 2 (1-5%): Detailed survey + virtual meeting
- Tier 3 (<1%): Automated survey + industry average fallback
```

This focuses your team's effort where it matters most.

### 2. Automated Survey Design and Distribution

AI tools can generate tailored surveys based on product type and customer profile:
- **Process-specific questions** based on your product category
- **Pre-populated fields** using publicly available industry data
- **Multilingual support** for global customer bases
- **Smart routing** that shows only relevant questions

### 3. Data Extraction from Customer Documents

When customers share process documentation, AI can extract key data:
- Energy consumption per unit of processing
- Fuel types and quantities used
- Process efficiency metrics
- Existing emissions estimates

Our [document analysis tool](/app/tools/contract-analyzer) can process engineering specs, energy audits, and process flow diagrams to extract relevant emissions data.

### 4. Emission Factor Research and Application

AI can automate the matching of processing activities to appropriate emission factors:
- Cross-reference against IPCC, EPA, and industry databases
- Flag where industry-specific factors don't exist
- Apply appropriate uncertainty ranges
- Suggest proxy activities for novel processes

### 5. Gap-Filling with Spend-Based Estimates

For customers who don't respond, AI can generate spend-based estimates:
1. Analyze historical purchase data by product category
2. Apply EEIO (Environmentally Extended Input-Output) emission factors
3. Document assumptions and uncertainty ranges
4. Flag estimates for future improvement

### 6. Automated Report Generation

Once data is collected and validated, AI can generate reporting outputs:
- **GHG Protocol conformant summaries** with methodology notes
- **Year-over-year comparisons** with variance analysis
- **Customer-level breakdowns** for engagement reporting
- **CDP submission formats**
- **CSRD-aligned disclosures**

Our [meeting summarizer tool](/app/tools/meeting-summarizer) can also help capture insights from customer engagement calls and translate them into structured data.

## Common Calculation Methods

### Method 1: Supplier-Specific Method (Preferred)
Use actual processing data collected from customers:
- Most accurate but data-intensive
- Requires customer cooperation
- Best for large, concentrated customer bases

### Method 2: Average Data Method
Use industry average processing emission factors:
- More accessible data
- Less accurate for diverse customer bases
- Useful as fallback when customer data unavailable

### Method 3: Spend-Based Method
Use economic activity data with EEIO factors:
- Least accurate but widely applicable
- Good for initial estimates
- Useful when product sales data is available but processing data is not
