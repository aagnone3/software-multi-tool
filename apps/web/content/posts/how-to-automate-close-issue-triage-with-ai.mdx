Close teams rarely struggle because issues exist.

They struggle because issues are discovered in batches, triaged inconsistently, and escalated too late.

AI helps finance teams triage close issues in real time so blockers are routed faster and high-impact items are handled first.

## Why close triage breaks down

Most teams rely on ad-hoc triage in chat threads and spreadsheets. That creates predictable problems:

1. **Severity is subjective** from one reviewer to another
2. **Ownership is unclear** when issues span subledgers or entities
3. **Escalation timing is inconsistent** across close cycles
4. **Too many low-impact issues** consume attention that should go to critical blockers

By the time the team aligns on priorities, valuable close hours are gone.

## What AI triage should do

### 1) Classify issue severity with consistent rules

Use AI to label issues as informational, warning, or critical based on factors like:

- account materiality
- variance size versus threshold
- dependency impact on downstream tasks
- time remaining before cutoff

This gives teams one shared severity framework instead of case-by-case debate.

### 2) Route issues to the right owner automatically

AI can map issue type to expected owner (preparer, reviewer, accounting manager, controller) using your close playbook.

Routing should include:

- primary owner
- backup owner
- SLA by severity
- escalation path if SLA is missed

### 3) Surface blockers before they become fire drills

Build a daily triage digest that highlights:

- newly critical issues
- critical issues with no owner action in the last 24 hours
- blockers affecting multiple close tasks
- issues likely to miss the close window

This keeps leadership focused on what can derail close, not just what is noisy.

### 4) Recommend the next action

For each issue, AI can propose the next best step, such as:

- request supporting schedule
- post adjustment entry
- obtain reviewer sign-off
- escalate to controller

Action guidance shortens resolution time and reduces back-and-forth.

## Rollout plan

1. Start with one close workstream (for example, reconciliations).
2. Define severity rules and escalation SLAs.
3. Run AI triage in parallel with the existing manual process for 1-2 closes.
4. Compare triage quality and response times.
5. Move to AI-led routing with human override controls.

## Metrics to track

Measure improvement with:

- median time from issue creation to owner assignment
- % critical issues resolved before cutoff
- escalation SLA adherence by severity
- number of issues reopened after “resolved” status
- post-close adjustment volume tied to missed triage

## Final take

Close quality depends on fast, consistent prioritization. AI issue triage gives finance teams a repeatable way to detect, rank, and route problems early, so close execution is calmer and more predictable.
