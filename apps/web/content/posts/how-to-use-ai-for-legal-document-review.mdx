Legal document review is one of the most expensive and time-consuming tasks in any business. Whether you're reviewing vendor contracts, employment agreements, NDAs, or compliance documentation, the process typically requires significant attorney time — or it gets rushed and risks slip through.

AI is changing this. Not by replacing lawyers, but by dramatically accelerating the first-pass review that catches most of the important issues.

Here's a practical guide to using AI for legal document review in 2025.

## What AI Can (and Can't) Do for Legal Review

### What AI Does Well

**Extracting key terms and dates:** AI can pull out parties, effective dates, termination dates, payment terms, and other structured information from contracts automatically.

**Flagging unusual or risky clauses:** Train an AI on what "normal" looks like and it gets good at flagging deviations — unusually broad indemnification, missing limitation of liability caps, one-sided termination rights.

**Summarizing long documents:** A 50-page agreement summarized into a one-page overview with key risks highlighted saves significant time for the person who needs to understand it quickly.

**Consistency checking:** AI can check that definitions are used consistently, that referenced exhibits actually exist, and that terms in one section don't contradict terms in another.

**Finding specific provisions:** Instead of reading an entire contract to find the governing law clause, you can ask AI where it is and what it says.

### What AI Doesn't Do Well

**Legal judgment:** Whether an unusual clause is acceptable depends on business context, relationship history, negotiating leverage, and risk tolerance. AI can flag it; only a human can decide.

**Negotiation strategy:** AI won't tell you whether to push back on a term or accept it in exchange for better terms elsewhere.

**Jurisdiction-specific nuance:** Some legal issues depend heavily on which state or country's law governs. AI can identify the governing law clause but won't give you jurisdiction-specific advice.

**High-stakes final review:** For important contracts, AI should accelerate the process, not replace attorney review.

## Step-by-Step: Using AI for Contract Review

### Step 1: Upload and Extract

Start by uploading the document to your AI tool. Let it do an initial pass to:

- Identify the document type (service agreement, NDA, employment contract, etc.)
- Extract the key parties and their roles
- Pull out important dates (effective date, expiration, notice periods)
- Summarize the basic structure

This takes seconds and gives you a foundation for deeper review.

### Step 2: Ask Specific Questions

The best way to use AI for legal review is to ask specific questions about what you need to know:

- "What are the payment terms?"
- "Does this contract have a limitation of liability clause? What is the cap?"
- "What are the termination conditions and notice requirements?"
- "Are there any non-compete or non-solicitation provisions?"
- "What insurance is required and who needs to provide it?"
- "Does the vendor have access to customer data? What are the data protection obligations?"

The more specific your questions, the more useful the AI output.

### Step 3: Request a Risk Assessment

After the initial extraction, ask the AI to flag potential issues:

- Clauses that appear unusual or risky
- Missing provisions that are typically present in this type of agreement
- Inconsistencies within the document
- Terms that are unusually one-sided

This gives you a prioritized list of things to review more carefully.

### Step 4: Generate a Summary

For contracts that need to be shared with non-lawyers (executives, finance, operations), ask AI to generate a plain-language summary:

"Summarize this contract in 500 words for a non-legal audience, highlighting the key obligations, payment terms, and three most important risks."

This summary isn't legal advice — it's a communication tool that helps the right people understand what they're signing.

### Step 5: Human Review of Flagged Items

Use the AI output as your starting point for attorney review. Instead of reading the entire contract from scratch, attorneys can focus on:

1. Flagged clauses with unusual terms
2. Missing provisions
3. Anything the AI flagged as inconsistent or unclear

This is typically 60-70% faster than a cold review of the full document.

## Best Practices for AI Legal Document Review

### Maintain a Review Checklist

Build a standard checklist of provisions you care about for each document type. NDAs, service agreements, employment contracts, and vendor agreements each have different risk profiles. Document what you're looking for so you ask the AI the right questions consistently.

### Calibrate on Known Documents

Before relying on AI for production review, test it on contracts you've already reviewed. Compare what the AI flags to what you flagged. This gives you a sense of the AI's accuracy and what it tends to miss.

### Build Templates for Common Requests

If you review the same type of contract frequently, build a standard prompt template that asks all the right questions in the right order. This saves time and ensures consistency.

### Document the AI's Output

Keep a record of what the AI analyzed and what it flagged for each contract. This audit trail is useful if questions come up later about whether a particular issue was considered during review.

### Know When to Escalate

Use the AI's output to decide when full attorney review is needed. If the AI flags multiple significant issues or the contract involves high value, novel terms, or complex obligations, escalate to legal.

## Common Contract Types and What to Look For

### NDAs (Non-Disclosure Agreements)
Key AI queries: scope of confidential information, exclusions, term length, return/destruction of materials, permitted disclosures, mutual vs. one-sided obligations.

### Service Agreements / Master Service Agreements
Key AI queries: scope of services, payment terms, IP ownership, limitation of liability, indemnification, data processing obligations, change order process, termination rights.

### Employment Agreements
Key AI queries: compensation and benefits, at-will vs. term, non-compete scope and duration, non-solicitation provisions, IP assignment, arbitration clauses.

### Vendor/Supplier Agreements
Key AI queries: pricing and payment terms, service levels and remedies, insurance requirements, data security obligations, audit rights, termination for convenience.

## The Bottom Line

AI won't replace your legal team — but it can make them dramatically more efficient and help non-lawyers understand what they're reviewing before they sign.

The teams getting the most value are the ones who treat AI as the first step in their review process: use it to extract, flag, and summarize, then apply human judgment to what it surfaces.

If you're spending significant time on contract review and not using AI yet, there's a meaningful opportunity to recover that time while improving the quality of your reviews.

Start with one document type, build a review workflow, and expand from there.
