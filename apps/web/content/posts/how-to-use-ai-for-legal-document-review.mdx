Legal document review is one of the most expensive line items in any business's legal budget. At $300–$500 per hour for outside counsel, a stack of contracts to review can cost thousands before a deal closes.

AI is changing this — not by replacing lawyers, but by making them dramatically faster.

## What AI Can (and Can't) Do for Legal Document Review

**AI can:**
- Extract key clauses (termination, indemnification, limitation of liability)
- Summarize contract terms in plain language
- Flag missing standard provisions
- Compare documents against template language
- Identify unusual or potentially risky terms

**AI cannot:**
- Provide legal advice
- Make final legal judgments
- Understand jurisdiction-specific nuances perfectly
- Replace a lawyer's review for high-stakes agreements

Think of AI as your first-pass reviewer that does the tedious extraction work, leaving lawyers to focus on judgment.

## Common Legal Documents AI Can Help Review

### Vendor and Supplier Contracts
Before signing with a new vendor, you need to understand what you're committing to. AI can quickly surface:
- Payment terms and late fees
- Auto-renewal clauses
- Termination for convenience vs. cause
- Liability caps and indemnification scope

### NDAs and Confidentiality Agreements
NDAs often have subtle differences that matter enormously. AI can flag:
- One-way vs. mutual confidentiality
- Duration of obligations
- Definition of "confidential information"
- Carve-outs that weaken protections

### Employment Agreements
Before signing or offering employment contracts, AI can extract:
- Non-compete and non-solicitation scope
- IP assignment breadth
- Severance terms
- At-will vs. defined-term employment

### Lease Agreements
Commercial leases are notoriously complex. AI can summarize:
- Base rent and escalation schedules
- CAM charges and expense pass-throughs
- Permitted use restrictions
- Renewal options and rights of first refusal

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
SLAs define what vendors owe you. AI can extract:
- Uptime guarantees and measurement methodology
- Remedies for SLA failures
- Exclusions that make guarantees meaningless
- Reporting requirements

## Step-by-Step: AI-Assisted Contract Review Process

### Step 1: Upload the Document
Upload the contract PDF or Word document to your AI tool. Good AI contract review tools accept multi-page documents and maintain formatting context.

### Step 2: Extract Key Terms
Ask the AI to extract:
- Parties and effective date
- Term and renewal provisions
- Payment terms
- Key obligations of each party
- Termination conditions

### Step 3: Identify Risk Flags
Ask the AI to flag:
- Indemnification provisions that seem unusually broad
- Limitation of liability clauses that cap your recovery
- IP ownership language that might be problematic
- Governing law and dispute resolution provisions

### Step 4: Generate a Summary
Create a plain-language summary you can share with business stakeholders who don't want to read the full legal document.

### Step 5: Human Review of Flags
Have a lawyer or experienced business person review only the flagged items. This is dramatically faster than reading the full document.

## Real Example: A SaaS Vendor Contract

Here's how AI would help you quickly assess a typical 15-page SaaS vendor agreement:

**Without AI:** A paralegal or junior attorney spends 2–3 hours reading the full document, taking notes, and writing a summary.

**With AI:** Upload the document, run the extraction in under 2 minutes, get a structured summary of all key terms and flagged risks. Then a lawyer spends 30 minutes reviewing just the flagged items.

The AI might flag things like:
- "Vendor may modify pricing with 30 days notice" — is that acceptable?
- "Customer indemnifies Vendor for any third-party claims arising from Customer data" — unusually broad
- "Limitation of liability is capped at fees paid in the prior 1 month" — very low cap
- No SLA or uptime guarantee — notable absence

## Practical Tips for Better Results

**Be specific in your prompts.** Ask "What are the indemnification obligations of each party?" rather than just "Summarize this contract."

**Review the AI's output critically.** AI can miss nuances or misclassify clauses. Always have a human verify before signing.

**Create a checklist for your contract types.** If you review a lot of vendor contracts, create a standard list of things you always want extracted. This makes AI review faster and more consistent.

**Use AI to compare against your standard templates.** Ask the AI what differs from your standard terms. This is faster than a manual comparison.

**Document what you find.** Use AI output as a starting point for your contract review notes, not a replacement for them.

## How Much Time Does AI Save?

For a typical 10-15 page commercial contract:

- **Manual review time:** 2–4 hours
- **AI-assisted review time:** 30–60 minutes
- **Time savings:** 60–80%

For businesses reviewing dozens of contracts per year, this adds up quickly — both in direct cost savings and in deal velocity.
