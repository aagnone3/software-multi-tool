# How to Automate Shipping Documentation with AI

Every shipment generates paperwork: bills of lading, freight invoices, packing lists, customs declarations, delivery receipts. For companies shipping at volume, this documentation burden can consume entire teams.

AI document processing is changing the economics of shipping operations.

## Types of Shipping Documents You Can Automate

### Freight Invoices
The highest-volume, most automatable document type. AI extracts: carrier name, invoice date, invoice number, shipping dates, origin/destination, line items (freight charges, fuel surcharges, accessorials), total amount due.

**Benefit**: Match invoices against POs and contracts automatically, catch billing errors before payment.

### Bills of Lading
Extract shipper, consignee, carrier, commodity description, weight, piece count, PRO number, and delivery terms.

**Benefit**: Automatically create shipment records and trigger downstream processes.

### Customs Documents
Extract HS codes, country of origin, declared values, and classification data from commercial invoices and customs declarations.

**Benefit**: Speed up customs clearance and reduce classification errors.

### Proof of Delivery
Extract delivery date, consignee signature, condition notes, and exceptions.

**Benefit**: Automatically close shipment records and trigger billing.

## Step-by-Step Automation Approach

### Step 1: Identify Your Highest-Volume Document Type
Most companies start with freight invoices — typically the highest volume and most standardized.

### Step 2: Choose an AI Extraction Tool
Look for tools that handle your specific document types and integrate with your TMS or ERP. Invoice processing AI tools work well for freight invoices.

### Step 3: Define Your Data Fields
Specify exactly which fields you need extracted: header fields (vendor, date, invoice #), line items, totals, references.

### Step 4: Set Up Validation Rules
Configure business rules: amount thresholds for manual review, carrier names that must match your approved list, date range checks.

### Step 5: Connect to Your Workflow
Route exceptions to the right people, auto-approve documents that pass all validation, and sync data to your TMS/ERP.

## Expected Results

Companies that automate shipping documentation typically see:
- **70-85%** reduction in manual data entry time
- **50-60%** faster invoice processing (days to hours)
- **3-5%** reduction in carrier overbilling (AI catches errors humans miss)
- **Near-zero** data entry errors vs. 1-3% typical manual error rate

## Getting Started Today

Start small: pick one document type, one carrier, and automate just that flow. Prove the ROI, then expand. Most companies see payback within 3-6 months of deployment.
