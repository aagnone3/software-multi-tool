Most close delays are not caused by preparing reconciliations.

They happen in **review**—when approvers have to verify support, chase missing context, and decide whether an item is truly resolved.

AI can make reconciliation review faster and more consistent by surfacing risk signals and drafting review notes, while humans keep approval authority.

## Why reconciliation review is a bottleneck

Reviewers often spend time on avoidable friction:

- inconsistent supporting documents across preparers
- unclear explanation of outstanding items
- missing evidence for high-risk balances
- repetitive comments on the same quality issues every month

That creates queue buildup late in close when finance leaders need clean signoff.

## A practical AI-assisted review workflow

1. **Standardize review criteria by account type**
   Define required evidence and materiality thresholds for cash, accruals, intercompany, prepaid, fixed assets, and reserve accounts.

2. **Auto-check reconciliation packets before reviewer handoff**
   Use AI to verify that each packet has required support, aging detail, and tie-outs to the trial balance.

3. **Flag likely review exceptions**
   Detect missing support, stale reconciling items, unexplained large movements, and policy mismatches before signoff.

4. **Draft reviewer comments with source references**
   Generate concise comments that cite exact line items or attachments so preparers can resolve issues quickly.

5. **Route high-risk items to senior approvers**
   Escalate based on amount, account criticality, and exception type rather than first-come queue order.

6. **Track recurring defects by preparer and account**
   Build a monthly quality trend so teams can reduce repeat review findings over time.

## Where to start first

Start with accounts that are both high-volume and high-review-friction:

- accrued liabilities
- prepaid expenses
- intercompany reconciliations
- suspense or clearing accounts

These usually produce immediate cycle-time gains because the same review issues repeat each close.

## Controls to keep quality high

- **Human signoff remains required:** AI assists; it does not approve.
- **Evidence-linked flags only:** every issue ties to a source document or record.
- **Materiality-aware routing:** avoid escalating low-value noise.
- **Audit trail preservation:** save generated comments, reviewer decisions, and final signoff notes.

## What success looks like

After one or two close cycles, teams typically see:

- faster review turnaround on critical accounts
- fewer back-and-forth comment loops
- better consistency across reviewers
- clearer signoff evidence for audit and management

---

If your review queue is the close bottleneck, begin with one account cluster and automate pre-review quality checks plus first-draft reviewer comments. That narrow rollout usually shows value quickly.
