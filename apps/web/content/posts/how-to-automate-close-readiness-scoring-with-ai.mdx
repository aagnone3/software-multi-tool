# How to Automate Close Readiness Scoring with AI

Most finance teams know when close is off track.

The problem is they know too late. By the time escalation happens, the only options are overtime and compromise. AI can help by turning fragmented status signals into a daily close-readiness score that highlights risk before deadlines slip.

## Why close readiness is hard to measure manually

Close risk is spread across many moving parts:

- reconciliations in progress
- unresolved close issues
- delayed approvals
- dependencies on upstream teams
- variance explanations still missing

Without a consistent scoring method, status reviews become subjective and reactive.

## What AI can automate in readiness scoring

AI is useful for translating operational noise into consistent risk indicators:

1. classify open tasks by risk level and due-date proximity
2. detect stale work items with no recent owner activity
3. identify dependencies likely to create bottlenecks
4. summarize unresolved blockers by business impact
5. produce a daily readiness score with clear rationale

The score is not the decision-maker. It is the early-warning system.

## A practical rollout pattern

### 1) Define the readiness model first

Start with simple weighted factors:

- critical tasks completed vs total
- high-severity blockers still open
- overdue items count
- dependency risk concentration
- unresolved variance commentary

Keep the model transparent so finance leaders trust the output.

### 2) Standardize close task metadata

Require each task to include:

- owner
- due date
- status
- dependency links
- risk classification

AI scoring is only as good as the structure feeding it.

### 3) Generate a daily readiness snapshot

Have AI produce a short daily view:

- current readiness score
- top 3 drivers of score movement
- highest-risk unresolved items
- expected impact if unchanged for 24 hours

This helps controllers focus review meetings on decisions, not data assembly.

### 4) Trigger targeted escalations

Use thresholds to automate escalation drafts:

- score drops below target range
- critical path task becomes overdue
- same blocker remains unresolved for N days

Escalations should include context, owner, impact, and proposed next action.

### 5) Run post-close calibration

After each close cycle, compare predicted readiness vs actual outcomes:

- did low scores predict delays accurately?
- which signals were most reliable?
- which weights need adjustment?

Calibration improves model usefulness over time.

## Guardrails that matter

- Keep score logic explainable and reviewable
- Require human approval for escalations outside finance
- Preserve links to source tasks behind every score
- Audit score changes and model adjustments
- Treat the score as directional, not absolute truth

## Metrics to track impact

Measure whether readiness scoring improves execution:

- days of advance warning before major delays
- percentage of closes completed on schedule
- number of high-risk blockers identified before cutoff
- escalation response time
- month-over-month volatility in readiness trend

## Final take

Automating close readiness scoring with AI helps finance teams move from "status reporting" to "risk steering."

When leadership gets earlier and clearer signals, close becomes more predictable—and fewer surprises make it to the finish line.
