Weekly collections meetings often stall because managers spend the first half of the call reconstructing account history.

AI can generate a consistent review packet before the meeting so decisions happen faster and follow-up actions are clearer.

## Why manual review prep breaks down

Most teams rely on copy-paste prep from multiple systems. That creates familiar pain:

- **Inconsistent summaries** across collectors
- **Missing context** (last contact, dispute status, promise dates)
- **Slow prep cycles** that consume collector time

When prep quality varies, manager decisions vary too.

## What a strong AI-generated review packet includes

For each account under review, include:

1. **Current AR snapshot** (balance, aging bucket, trend vs prior week)
2. **Recent activity timeline** (contacts, promises, disputes, payments)
3. **Risk flags** (broken promises, stalled disputes, aging acceleration)
4. **Recommended next action** (with owner + due date)

AI should standardize this structure so every account is comparable in one pass.

## High-leverage automation points

### 1) Pre-meeting packet generation

Generate packets on a fixed schedule (for example, 2 hours before weekly review).

Each packet should include only accounts that match defined review criteria:

- high balance above threshold
- moved into a worse aging bucket
- unresolved dispute older than policy limit

### 2) Narrative summaries for faster decisions

Instead of raw notes, AI can produce short decision-oriented summaries:

- what changed since last review
- why risk increased or decreased
- what action is recommended now

This reduces “status reading” time and increases actual decision time.

### 3) Action extraction after the meeting

After decisions are made, AI can convert outcomes into structured follow-up tasks:

- owner
- due date
- action type
- escalation level

That closes the loop and prevents decisions from disappearing into meeting notes.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **No packet template governance**: every collector prompts AI differently.
- **No confidence flags**: uncertain summaries treated as facts.
- **No post-meeting sync**: actions are not written back to the work queue.

Use one reviewed template, require source references, and always route approved actions to the system of record.

## Metrics to track

Measure decision velocity and execution quality:

- prep time per review cycle
- number of accounts reviewed per meeting
- time from meeting decision to owner assignment
- % actions completed by due date
- recovery rate on reviewed accounts

If prep time drops but execution does not improve, the bottleneck is action ownership, not packet quality.

## Bottom line

Collections manager reviews should be decision meetings, not data gathering sessions.

AI-generated review packets give teams consistent context, clearer prioritization, and faster follow-through.

Start with one weekly review workflow and one standardized packet template. Once decisions become faster and cleaner, expand to additional segments.
