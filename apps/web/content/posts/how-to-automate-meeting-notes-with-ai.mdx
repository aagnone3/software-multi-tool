Writing meeting notes is one of those tasks that feels important but takes time you don't have. You're trying to capture everything, organize it, and remember what decisions were made — while still being present in the meeting.

AI can handle this. Not perfectly, but well enough that you'll spend 5 minutes reviewing AI notes instead of 45 minutes writing your own.

Here's how to set it up properly.

## Why Manual Meeting Notes Fail

Before getting into the solution, it's worth understanding why most people's meeting note process is broken:

**The attention problem.** You can't fully participate in a meeting and take comprehensive notes simultaneously. You end up with either poor participation or incomplete notes.

**The recency bias problem.** When you write notes after the meeting, you remember the last 20 minutes much better than the first 40. Important early decisions get compressed or lost.

**The distribution problem.** Meeting notes that live in someone's personal Notion or local docs help no one. Sharing consistently requires extra effort most people skip.

**The review problem.** Notes that include every tangent and sidebar are useless. Good notes require editorial judgment that takes time.

AI doesn't solve all of these, but it solves most of them.

## The AI Meeting Note Workflow

Here's the process that works in practice:

### Step 1: Record the Meeting

Most video conferencing tools (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet) have built-in recording. Enable it and get consent from participants — in most jurisdictions this is required.

For phone calls or in-person meetings, tools like Otter.ai or a simple voice recorder work.

The output you need is either:
- A recording file (MP3, MP4, M4A)
- A transcript (TXT, DOCX, VTT, SRT)

### Step 2: Generate the Transcript

If you recorded audio/video, you need a transcript first. Modern transcription is highly accurate — typically 95%+ for clear audio with distinct speakers.

Options:
- **Zoom/Teams/Meet**: Built-in transcription, available in your account
- **Whisper (OpenAI)**: Free, runs locally, excellent accuracy
- **Otter.ai**: Automatic transcription with speaker detection
- **Descript**: Video-first but good for meeting content

Quality of the AI summary depends heavily on transcript quality. If the transcript is full of errors, the summary will reflect that.

### Step 3: Run the AI Summary

This is where the leverage is. Upload your transcript and let the AI produce a structured summary.

[Software Multi-Tool's Meeting Summarizer](/tools/meeting-summarizer) produces:
- **Executive summary**: 2-4 sentences capturing what the meeting was about
- **Key decisions**: Explicit decisions made during the meeting
- **Action items**: Who agreed to do what by when
- **Participants**: Who was in the meeting and their roles
- **Discussion topics**: Main themes covered, with brief notes

This is infinitely better than raw transcript or most manually written notes because it's structured and scannable.

### Step 4: Review and Distribute

AI output needs a human pass before it goes out. This usually takes 5-10 minutes:

- Confirm action item owners are correctly attributed
- Add context the AI missed (subtext, implicit agreements)
- Remove anything confidential that shouldn't be shared
- Adjust tone if needed

Then send it. This is the step most people skip — getting notes to participants within an hour of the meeting while it's fresh.

## What AI Meeting Summarization Does Well

**Capturing decisions.** When someone says "we're going to do X," AI is good at pulling that out and attributing it correctly.

**Identifying action items.** "I'll follow up on that by Friday" or "Can you send me the data?" — AI catches these reliably.

**Topic organization.** A 90-minute meeting covering 6 topics gets organized into sections automatically.

**Handling long meetings.** 2-hour all-hands that would take 90 minutes to summarize manually? AI handles it the same as a 30-minute call.

**Consistency.** Your AI notes follow the same format every time. Manual notes vary by who's taking them and how much time they had.

## What AI Meeting Summarization Gets Wrong

**Attribution errors.** In meetings where multiple people have similar voices or where crosstalk is common, the AI (and the underlying transcript) gets speaker attribution wrong. Always verify who said what for important decisions.

**Implicit context.** "We're going to do the thing we discussed last week" — the AI doesn't know what last week's thing was. You need to add that context.

**Nuance and subtext.** "We'll consider it" means no. The AI will record it as a maybe. Human review catches these.

**Confidential content.** If sensitive topics come up, the AI doesn't know to omit them from notes sent to a broader audience.

## Meeting Types and How to Handle Each

### 1:1 Check-ins
These are often too informal for detailed notes, but AI-generated action item lists are genuinely useful. Upload the transcript and extract just the commitments.

### Team Stand-ups
Skip AI summarization. Stand-ups should produce action items written in real-time, not summarized afterward.

### Project Planning Meetings
High value for AI summarization. Complex discussions, multiple decisions, many action items. The structured output pays off.

### Client Meetings
Extra care needed. Review thoroughly before sending. Consider whether the full summary or a curated version is appropriate for the client.

### Board/Executive Meetings
Human review is essential. These produce documents with legal and governance implications. AI handles the draft; a human handles the final version.

### Interviews
Excellent use case. Consistent format across all interviews, captured immediately, easy to compare candidates.

## Setting Up Your System

You don't need a complex stack. Here's the simplest version that works:

1. **Always record** — make it a default, not a case-by-case decision
2. **Transcribe immediately after** — don't let recordings pile up
3. **Run AI summary within 1 hour** — while context is still fresh for review
4. **Standard template** — same format every time so recipients know where to look
5. **Send within 2 hours** — while the meeting is still fresh for recipients

The friction usually comes from the transcription step. If your recording tool produces transcripts automatically, you've removed the biggest bottleneck.
