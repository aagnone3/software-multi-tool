Many AR aging meetings consume time without improving cash outcomes.

Teams review the same report, debate priorities, and leave with vague follow-ups.

AI can turn that into an execution loop: pre-ranked risk, clear owner actions, and measurable outcomes week over week.

## 1) Standardize the aging review objective

Before automating anything, decide what the meeting is for:

- Reduce >60-day and >90-day balances
- Unblock invoice disputes
- Escalate high-risk accounts with no movement
- Reallocate collector time to highest expected recovery

If the objective is unclear, automation only speeds up noise.

## 2) Build a deterministic pre-meeting data pack

Your AI output is only as useful as the input package.

Include:

- Aging buckets by customer (current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+)
- Last payment date and amount
- Promise-to-pay status
- Open dispute flags and owners
- Recent outreach attempts and responses

Keep this dataset structured and consistent each week.

## 3) Use AI to risk-rank accounts before the meeting

Don’t start meetings by scrolling spreadsheets.

Have AI generate a ranked list with explicit rationale:

- Exposure amount
- Aging severity
- Engagement trend
- Dispute complexity
- Payment behavior risk signals

Each account should include one recommended next action and owner.

## 4) Generate a meeting agenda that is action-first

A good aging review agenda is short and focused.

Use AI to produce:

- Top 10 accounts needing decision/escalation
- Accounts to deprioritize this week
- Cross-functional blockers requiring finance/sales/legal input
- Decisions required in-meeting vs async

This prevents status updates from taking over the call.

## 5) Auto-draft collector follow-ups after decisions

Meeting decisions fail when follow-up communication is slow or inconsistent.

Use AI to draft:

- Customer outreach emails by account context
- Internal escalation notes for sales/account management
- Dispute handoff summaries for legal/ops

Collectors should approve/send, not write from scratch every time.

## 6) Track commitment-to-outcome conversion

Most teams track tasks completed, not dollars recovered.

Measure both:

- Action completion rate within 48 hours
- Cash collected from reviewed accounts (7/14/30 day windows)
- Roll-rate movement between aging buckets
- Escalation resolution cycle time

Feed this back into your ranking and agenda logic weekly.

## 7) Keep governance and auditability explicit

Automation should increase control, not reduce it.

Log:

- Input data snapshot used for each meeting
- AI-generated recommendations
- Human overrides and final decisions
- Final outreach sent to customers

This protects compliance and makes performance reviews objective.

## Common mistakes

1. **Treating AI recommendations as final decisions**
   Keep approval and accountability with AR leadership.

2. **Overloading meetings with too many accounts**
   Focus on the few accounts where intervention changes outcomes.

3. **Ignoring dispute-root-cause patterns**
   Aging will recur if upstream invoicing/process issues remain unresolved.

4. **No closed-loop measurement**
   If you don’t connect meeting actions to cash impact, you can’t improve.

## Final take

AR aging meetings should be decision systems, not reporting rituals.

Use AI to prep risk-ranked priorities, produce owner-level actions, and close the loop on results. The combination of deterministic policy and automated execution is what turns weekly reviews into cash acceleration.
