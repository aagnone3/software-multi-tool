## The Problem with Manual Contract Review

Contract review is one of the most expensive, time-consuming activities in any organization that does business through written agreements. Whether you're a legal team reviewing vendor contracts, a procurement team processing supplier agreements, or a small business owner signing service contracts, the challenge is the same: agreements are long, dense, and filled with clauses that can have significant consequences if missed.

Manual review has three problems:

1. **Speed** — A thorough review of a 30-page MSA can take 2-4 hours. When you're processing dozens of agreements per week, this creates a bottleneck.

2. **Consistency** — Different reviewers catch different things. What your most experienced attorney flags, a junior associate might miss. AI applies the same criteria every time.

3. **Cost** — Attorney time is expensive. For standard, lower-risk agreements, paying $300-500/hour for manual review is difficult to justify.

AI contract review doesn't eliminate the need for human judgment. It handles the extraction, comparison, and flagging work—so humans can focus on the exceptions that require it.

## What AI Contract Review Actually Does

Modern AI contract review tools perform several distinct functions:

### Extraction
Identifies and pulls out specific clause types: payment terms, termination rights, liability caps, indemnification provisions, governing law, dispute resolution, auto-renewal terms, IP ownership, confidentiality obligations.

### Comparison
Compares extracted terms against your standard positions, flagging deviations. "Their liability cap is 3x fees—your standard is 1x fees" is more useful than just highlighting the clause.

### Summarization
Converts a 50-page agreement into a 1-page executive summary with the most important terms, key dates, and flagged issues.

### Risk Scoring
Some tools score overall contract risk based on the combination of clauses present, allowing you to prioritize which agreements need senior review.

## How to Implement AI Contract Review

### Step 1: Identify Your Review Bottleneck

Before selecting tools, identify where contract review is actually slowing you down. Common bottlenecks:
- Intake processing (just reading to understand what type of agreement it is)
- First-pass risk screening (deciding if legal needs to see it)
- Issue identification (finding the clauses that need negotiation)
- Summary creation (explaining the agreement to non-lawyers)

Different tools solve different bottlenecks.

### Step 2: Start with Document Analysis

The fastest way to get value from AI contract review is to start with extraction and summarization. Upload a contract and ask the AI to identify: payment terms, termination clauses, liability provisions, IP ownership, and any unusual or one-sided terms.

**Software Multi-Tool's Document Analyzer** works well for this. Upload a contract PDF or paste the text, and it returns a structured summary of key provisions. There's no setup required and no training data needed—it works on any contract type from day one.

### Step 3: Build a Review Checklist

Rather than asking "what's in this contract," build a standard checklist of 10-20 provisions you always want to verify. This makes AI output more consistent and easier to review.

Example checklist items:
- Payment terms and invoice schedule
- Termination for convenience notice period
- Liability cap amount
- Indemnification triggers
- IP ownership for work product
- Auto-renewal terms and notice period
- Governing law and dispute resolution forum

Run your AI tool against this checklist for every new agreement.

### Step 4: Establish Human Review Thresholds

Define criteria for when human review is required vs. when AI-assisted review is sufficient:

**AI-only sufficient:**
- Standard vendor agreements under defined value threshold
- Renewals with no changes from prior year
- Agreements where your standard form was not modified

**Human review required:**
- Any deviation from standard liability caps
- Non-standard IP provisions
- Agreements above value threshold
- Any agreement in a regulated context (healthcare, financial services)

### Step 5: Track What AI Misses

Every AI system has blind spots. Build a feedback loop: when human review catches something AI missed, document it. Over time, update your review checklist to prompt the AI to check for those things explicitly.

## Common Mistakes in AI Contract Review

### Treating AI Output as Final
AI extraction is a first pass, not a final review. Use it to identify what needs human attention, not to replace human judgment.

### Not Verifying Extracted Terms
AI can misread or misclassify clauses, especially in non-standard agreements. Always verify critical terms against the source document before relying on them.

### Ignoring Context
A liability cap of $1,000 might be fine in a $500 software contract and unacceptable in a $50,000 services agreement. AI extracts numbers; humans need to assess adequacy.

### Skipping the Exhibit Review
Exhibits, schedules, and statements of work are where obligations often live. Make sure your AI review covers attached documents, not just the main agreement body.
