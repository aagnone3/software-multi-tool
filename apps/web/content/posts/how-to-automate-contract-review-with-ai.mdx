Contract review is one of the most time-consuming tasks in any business. Whether you're a startup founder reviewing vendor agreements or an operations manager handling procurement contracts, the manual process is slow and expensive. AI is changing that.

## What AI Contract Review Actually Does

AI contract review tools analyze contract text and:
- Extract key terms (payment amounts, dates, parties, governing law)
- Identify unusual or high-risk clauses
- Compare terms against standard playbooks
- Flag missing standard provisions
- Summarize lengthy agreements in plain language

What it **doesn't** do: provide legal advice or replace attorney judgment on complex matters.

## Step 1: Identify Which Contracts to Automate First

Not all contracts need the same level of scrutiny. Start with:
- **High-volume, low-complexity contracts**: NDAs, standard vendor agreements, SaaS subscriptions
- **Template-based contracts**: where you're reviewing against a known standard
- **Renewal reviews**: existing contracts where you need to spot changes

Save attorney time for:
- M&A transactions and financing documents
- Novel deal structures
- Contracts with significant negotiation history

## Step 2: Choose the Right AI Tool

Look for tools that:
- Handle your contract formats (PDF, Word, DocX)
- Extract structured data (not just raw text)
- Flag specific clause types you care about
- Maintain data security (especially for sensitive commercial terms)

## Step 3: Define Your Review Checklist

Before automating, document what you currently review manually:
- Payment terms and amounts
- Termination clauses (for cause, convenience, automatic)
- Liability caps and indemnification
- IP ownership provisions
- Confidentiality obligations and duration
- Governing law and dispute resolution

AI can be trained on these priorities, so your automation reflects your actual risk profile.

## Step 4: Establish a Review Workflow

A practical AI-assisted contract review workflow:

1. **Upload** contract to AI tool
2. **Review** the AI-generated summary and extraction
3. **Flag** any items that need deeper attention or negotiation
4. **Escalate** to attorney only items that require legal judgment
5. **Archive** structured data for future reference

This workflow typically cuts initial review time by 60-80% on standard commercial contracts.

## Step 5: Handle Edge Cases and Exceptions

AI review works best on standard commercial language. Watch for:
- **Unusual indemnification structures**: may require attorney review
- **Jurisdiction-specific requirements**: local law variations matter
- **Industry-specific terms**: healthcare, financial services, government contracts have specialized provisions
- **Non-English contracts**: verify your tool handles translation correctly

Build escalation rules into your workflow so edge cases automatically route to appropriate reviewers.

## What to Do with the Time You Save

The point isn't just efficiency — it's what you do with the saved capacity:
- Review more contracts before signing (instead of just accepting vendor terms)
- Negotiate more consistently (with AI flagging non-standard terms every time)
- Maintain better contract records (structured data vs. filed PDFs)
- Reduce legal spend (fewer billable hours on routine extraction tasks)
