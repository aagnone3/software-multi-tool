Variance analysis is rarely the bottleneck.

Variance **commentary** is.

Finance teams can pull actuals quickly, but the narrative layer often becomes a last-mile scramble: inconsistent explanations, repeated manual edits, and limited time for real insight. AI can remove that bottleneck if you implement it with strong controls.

## Why commentary slows reporting cycles

Most teams face the same issues:

1. **Inconsistent structure** across business units.
2. **Manual copy-paste** from spreadsheets into slide decks and board packs.
3. **Late edits** from finance leadership that require rewrites under deadline pressure.
4. **Weak traceability** from written narrative back to source variance data.

The result: slower closes and lower confidence in reporting quality.

## What AI should automate in variance commentary

The goal is to automate the first 80% of the narrative while keeping final ownership with finance.

### 1) Draft first-pass commentary from variance thresholds

AI can generate a baseline narrative when thresholds are triggered, such as:

- revenue variance above tolerance
- margin compression in key segments
- spend overrun by cost center
- volume vs price mix shifts

Each draft should include the metric, delta, likely drivers, and expected follow-up action.

### 2) Enforce a standard explanation template

Use a fixed structure for every commentary block:

- **What changed?**
- **Why it changed?**
- **Is it temporary or structural?**
- **What action is being taken?**

This dramatically improves readability for executives and keeps business-unit submissions comparable.

### 3) Flag weak narratives before leadership review

AI can score commentary quality and highlight drafts that are:

- vague ("timing issues" with no specifics)
- unsupported by source numbers
- missing action owners or timelines
- contradictory with prior month statements

That catches quality issues before they hit CFO review.

## Practical rollout approach

A proven path for implementation:

1. Start with one reporting pack (e.g., monthly business review).
2. Define commentary thresholds and required fields by metric family.
3. Generate AI drafts in a sandbox workflow first.
4. Require human approval and edits before publication.
5. Track turnaround time and revision volume over 2-3 cycles.

After stability, expand to additional entities and reporting packs.

## Control requirements you should keep

- Keep immutable links between commentary text and source data snapshot.
- Store model inputs/outputs for auditability.
- Require named approver signoff for external reporting content.
- Lock commentary once approved to prevent silent late edits.

These controls preserve trust while increasing speed.

## Metrics that prove this is working

Monitor outcomes, not activity:

- commentary cycle time (draft to approval)
- percentage of comments accepted with minor edits
- number of late-reporting narrative changes
- stakeholder rating of commentary clarity

If these trend in the right direction, AI is delivering real reporting leverage.

## Final take

Automating month-end variance commentary with AI gives finance teams faster reporting cycles and more consistent narratives. The key is structured prompts, clear templates, and strong review controls—not fully autonomous reporting.
