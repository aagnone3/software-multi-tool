Wealth managers handle enormous volumes of financial documents — statements, reports, research notes, client correspondence. AI tools are dramatically reducing the time spent on document-heavy tasks.

## The Document Problem in Wealth Management

A typical wealth manager might process:
- Quarterly account statements for dozens of clients
- Research reports from multiple brokerages
- Tax documents and K-1s
- Meeting notes and follow-up action items
- Regulatory filings and disclosures

Manual review of all this is slow, error-prone, and takes time away from actual client relationships.

## How AI Helps Wealth Managers

### 1. Document Summarization

AI can read a 40-page quarterly report and extract the key metrics: performance vs. benchmark, asset allocation shifts, fees charged, notable events. What takes 30 minutes per client now takes 2 minutes.

### 2. Meeting Transcription and Notes

AI transcription tools convert client meeting recordings into structured notes, capturing action items, investment preferences, and risk tolerance discussions automatically.

### 3. Contract and Agreement Review

Client agreements, custodial agreements, and fee disclosures can be analyzed by AI to flag unusual terms, compare against standard language, and summarize key obligations.

### 4. News Monitoring

AI can monitor news feeds for mentions of holdings, sectors, or macroeconomic events relevant to client portfolios, surfacing only what matters.

## ROI Calculation

For a wealth manager with 50 clients:
- Document review: 30 min/client/quarter → 25 hours/quarter saved
- Meeting notes: 20 min/meeting × 100 meetings/year → 33 hours/year saved
- Total: ~65+ hours/year recaptured for client-facing work

At $300/hour billing rate equivalent, that's $19,500+ in recovered time annually.

## Getting Started

The most impactful starting point is usually meeting summarization — it delivers immediate value and requires minimal workflow change. From there, document analysis for quarterly reviews, then contract review.

Tools that support multiple document types (PDF, Word, audio) in one platform minimize context-switching and training overhead.
