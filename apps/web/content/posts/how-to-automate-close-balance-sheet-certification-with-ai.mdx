Balance sheet certification is supposed to be a control.

In many teams, it becomes a manual checkbox exercise at the end of close.

AI can turn certification into a repeatable, evidence-backed workflow that highlights real risk instead of generating more admin work.

## Why manual certification breaks down

Most close teams face the same friction:

1. Accounts are certified without clear evidence standards
2. Ownership is ambiguous across entities and teams
3. Review quality depends on individual diligence
4. Escalations happen late when deadline pressure is highest

The result is uneven control quality and rework late in the cycle.

## Build an AI-assisted certification workflow

### 1) Define certification policy in plain rules

Start with deterministic policy logic:

- Which accounts require certification each cycle
- Materiality and variance thresholds by account class
- Required supporting artifacts per account type
- Certifier and reviewer roles with SLAs

AI should enforce these rules consistently, not invent them.

### 2) Pre-assemble support packets automatically

Before certification opens, AI should gather:

- Current-period reconciliation output
- Prior-period balances and movement analysis
- Open reconciling items with aging
- Relevant journal entry activity and reversals

This removes time wasted hunting for documents.

### 3) Risk-score each account before review

Use AI to prioritize attention where it matters:

- Large unexplained period-over-period movement
- Repeated reconciling items
- Manual journal concentration near cutoff
- Prior audit comments tied to the account

Low-risk accounts can follow a lightweight path. High-risk accounts get deeper review and stricter SLA.

### 4) Generate certifier prompts and reviewer checklists

For each account, AI can provide:

- A concise certification summary
- Key exception highlights
- Missing evidence alerts
- Suggested reviewer questions

This improves review consistency without slowing teams down.

### 5) Route exceptions with clear escalation paths

If evidence is incomplete or risk signals are high, route automatically to:

- Assigned owner
- Reviewer/controller
- Escalation tier when SLA breaches

Every step should be timestamped and attributable.

## Example certification record

- **Account:** Accrued expenses
- **Certification status:** Pending reviewer sign-off
- **Risk score:** High
- **Flags:** Unreconciled item > 45 days, +22% MoM variance
- **Required action:** Attach reconciliations + explain variance driver
- **SLA:** 6 hours to resolution

## Rollout mistakes to avoid

- Treating all accounts as equal risk
- Certifying without minimum evidence requirements
- Using AI summaries without reviewer accountability
- Tracking completion but not exception quality

Certification should improve control outcomes, not just close checklists.

## KPIs to monitor

- Certification completion rate by deadline
- High-risk account resolution time
- Percentage of certifications with complete evidence
- Repeat exceptions by account across periods

These metrics show whether certification quality is actually improving.

## Final takeaway

AI-powered balance sheet certification works when rules are explicit, evidence is standardized, and escalations are automatic.

Build the workflow around control quality first. Speed follows.
