# How to Automate Vendor Contract Review with AI

Vendor contracts are a necessary evil in business. Every supplier relationship, every SaaS subscription, every service agreement comes with a document that needs review.

The problem: most people reviewing these contracts aren't lawyers, and even lawyers charge a lot for contract review time.

AI contract review tools have become genuinely useful for handling the first pass — extracting what matters, flagging what's unusual, and leaving your team to make decisions rather than hunt for information.

Here's how to build this into your workflow.

---

## What AI Can (and Can't) Do for Contract Review

**AI does well:**
- Extracting specific terms (payment, termination, liability, IP, confidentiality)
- Flagging clauses that deviate from standard language
- Generating plain-language summaries
- Comparing similar contracts against each other
- Finding missing standard clauses

**AI still needs human judgment:**
- Evaluating whether unusual terms are acceptable for your specific situation
- Negotiation strategy
- Final approval for high-value or high-risk agreements
- Regulatory compliance in specialized industries

The goal isn't to replace legal review — it's to make the human review faster and more focused.

---

## The Contract Review Workflow (Before AI)

Most teams today handle contract review like this:

1. Contract arrives via email
2. Someone spends 30-60 minutes reading it
3. Questions go back to the vendor (add 2-3 days)
4. Another read-through after revisions
5. Legal reviews the final version (add another week, plus fees)
6. Signature

For a standard vendor agreement, this often takes 2-4 weeks and involves multiple people.

---

## The Contract Review Workflow (With AI)

With AI in the loop:

1. Contract arrives
2. Upload to AI contract analyzer — **5 minutes**
3. AI generates: key terms extract, risk flags, plain-language summary
4. First questions go to vendor with specific clause references — **same day**
5. AI reviews revised version, highlights what changed
6. Legal reviews the AI-flagged risk items only — **focused review, half the time**
7. Signature

The total cycle shortens. The cost of legal review shrinks because legal is reviewing a curated set of issues, not reading the whole document.

## Key Terms to Always Extract

When reviewing vendor contracts, these are the terms that matter most:

### Payment Terms
- Invoice frequency (monthly, annual, milestone)
- Payment due date (net 15, net 30, net 60)
- Late payment penalties
- Auto-renewal pricing

### Termination
- Notice period required
- Termination for cause vs. convenience
- Early termination fees
- Data return/deletion on termination

### Liability
- Limitation of liability clause (cap amount, carve-outs)
- Indemnification obligations
- Insurance requirements

### Intellectual Property
- Who owns work product
- License grants
- IP indemnification

### Confidentiality
- What's covered
- Duration after termination
- Permitted disclosures

A good AI contract analyzer should extract all of these automatically and present them in one place.

## Building a Contract Review Checklist

Even with AI doing the first pass, a consistent checklist helps:

**Before signing any vendor contract:**
- [ ] Payment terms confirmed and acceptable
- [ ] Auto-renewal clause reviewed (set calendar reminder if renewing)
- [ ] Termination notice period noted
- [ ] Liability cap reviewed against contract value
- [ ] Data handling clauses comply with your policies
- [ ] IP ownership confirmed (especially for custom work)
- [ ] SLA terms documented if relevant

The AI review covers the extraction. The checklist ensures you actually act on what it finds.

---

## Common Red Flags to Watch For

AI contract analyzers typically flag these:

**Unilateral modification clauses** — vendor can change terms without your consent

**Perpetual licenses** — vendor retains certain rights indefinitely

**Missing limitation of liability** — you're fully exposed on damages

**Evergreen auto-renewal** — renews automatically with long notice periods

**Broad IP assignment** — any IP created during the engagement belongs to the vendor

**Indemnification asymmetry** — you indemnify the vendor but not vice versa

## When to Still Involve Legal

AI handles the volume work. Legal handles:

- Contracts above a material dollar threshold
- Relationships involving your own IP or customer data
- Agreements with regulatory implications (HIPAA, financial services, etc.)
- Any contract where the flagged risks are significant

Use AI to triage. Send the genuinely complex ones to counsel.
