Most dunning sequences fail for one reason: every customer gets the same message.

A first-time late payer with a strong history should not get the same tone as a chronic late payer who ignores reminders.

AI helps you personalize outreach at scale—if you combine it with clear policy rules.

## 1) Segment accounts before generating any message

Define 3–5 practical segments your team can act on:

- New late payers (first offense)
- Consistently late but responsive accounts
- High-balance strategic customers
- High-risk accounts with repeated broken promises
- Disputed invoices pending resolution

AI outputs should vary by segment tone, urgency, and escalation path.

## 2) Build a policy-safe message framework

Do **not** let AI send unconstrained free-form emails.

Create templates with required fields:

- Invoice references and due dates
- Amount due
- Accepted payment options
- Explicit next step and deadline
- Escalation language approved by legal/finance

AI can personalize context and wording, but the core structure should stay controlled.

## 3) Feed behavior context into prompt inputs

For each outreach, include structured context:

- Days past due
- Prior reminder opens/replies
- Promise-to-pay history
- Payment method on file
- Customer tier / account owner

This allows AI to produce messages that feel informed instead of generic.

## 4) Personalize tone by customer risk and value

Examples:

- **Low risk, long relationship:** collaborative tone + flexible options
- **Medium risk:** firm reminder + clear deadline + support option
- **High risk:** direct escalation language + consequences + strict timeline

The goal is better recovery, not harsher language.

## 5) Use AI to draft subject lines and CTAs with intent

Subject lines should match urgency and clarity:

- "Quick follow-up on invoice #10493"
- "Action needed: payment due this week"
- "Final notice before account escalation"

CTAs should be singular and easy:

- "Pay now"
- "Confirm payment date"
- "Contact us to resolve this invoice"

## 6) Add response handling automation

Personalization only works if replies are handled quickly.

When responses come in, auto-classify into:

- Payment sent / confirmation provided
- Promise to pay requested
- Dispute opened
- Needs payment plan
- Wrong contact / routing issue

Route each class to the correct queue immediately.

## 7) Measure effectiveness by segment, not just total collections

Track:

- Open and reply rates by segment
- Promise-to-pay conversion rate
- Days-to-resolution
- Payment recovery per message sequence
- Escalation rate and write-offs

Segment-level metrics show where messaging is actually helping.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Over-personalizing without legal/compliance review
- Mixing dispute and collections messaging in one sequence
- Sending too frequently without segment logic
- Ignoring account-owner context for strategic customers
- Measuring volume sent instead of cash recovered

## Final takeaway

AI-powered dunning is most effective when it combines policy-safe templates with behavior-aware personalization.

Start with a small segment set, define strict guardrails, and iterate using recovery metrics. That gives you better customer communication **and** faster cash collection without losing control of risk.
