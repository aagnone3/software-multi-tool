Adjusting entries are necessary, but they are also one of the easiest places for close risk to hide. A rushed late-cycle adjustment can be valid and still create downstream reconciliation problems if documentation, owner context, or approval quality is weak.

AI helps finance teams review adjusting entries faster by flagging outliers, summarizing supporting evidence, and routing the right items to the right reviewer before sign-off.

## Why adjusting entry review slows close

Most teams are still handling this with manual scans and ad hoc reviewer notes. That leads to repeat friction:

1. **Large review volumes at the end of close** when time pressure is highest.
2. **Inconsistent reviewer depth** across teams and entities.
3. **Late discovery of weak support** after entries are already posted and referenced.

## What AI should automate in adjusting entry review

The goal is not to auto-approve entries. The goal is to prioritize human attention where judgment matters most.

### 1) Risk-rank entries before review

Use AI + rules to rank entries based on factors such as:

- amount vs normal account activity
- unusual posting timing (late cycle, off-pattern days)
- manual journal source vs system-generated source
- prior issues by account, preparer, or entry type
- missing or weak narrative quality

This gives reviewers a practical queue instead of a flat list.

### 2) Generate compact reviewer briefs

For each high-risk entry, auto-build a short packet with:

- entry summary and business purpose
- linked support docs or references
- variance context vs prior periods
- preparer + approver chain
- recommended reviewer disposition (approve, question, escalate)

That removes repetitive prep work and improves review consistency.

### 3) Standardize disposition outcomes

After review, capture structured outcomes:

- approved as-is
- approved with note
- returned for revision
- escalated for controller sign-off

Those dispositions feed better analytics for next close cycles.

## Rollout plan that works

Keep implementation scoped and measurable:

1. Start with one entity or account family with frequent manual entries.
2. Define initial risk thresholds with accounting leadership.
3. Run AI ranking in parallel for 1-2 closes before hard enforcement.
4. Tune false positives using reviewer dispositions.
5. Expand to full close once reviewers trust the prioritization.

## Metrics to track

Use concrete close metrics, not vanity metrics:

- review cycle time per adjusting entry
- percent of entries reviewed before final close cut-off
- percent of entries returned for missing/weak support
- number of late-cycle escalations tied to manual journals
- rework hours from post-review corrections

If these move in the right direction, your process quality is improving.

## Common implementation mistake

Do not overfit your model to historical approvals.

Historical approvals can include bad habits. Keep explicit policy checks (materiality, support completeness, segregation of duties) as hard gates instead of letting AI confidence alone decide priority.

## Bottom line

Automating adjusting entry review with AI gives finance teams faster triage, better evidence quality, and more consistent sign-off decisions. The practical gain is a tighter close process with fewer surprises at the end.
