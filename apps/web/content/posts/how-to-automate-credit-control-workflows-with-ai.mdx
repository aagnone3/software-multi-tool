Most credit control problems start the same way: decisions are made too late.

By the time a customer is clearly high-risk, you already have overdue invoices.

AI helps finance teams move credit control earlier in the process: score risk continuously, trigger reviews automatically, and escalate with consistent rules.

## 1) Define clear credit tiers and review triggers

Start with simple operational tiers:

- **Low risk:** standard terms, monthly review
- **Medium risk:** tighter limits, biweekly review
- **High risk:** reduced terms, weekly review + approval checks

Then define event-based triggers:

- New customer above a spend threshold
- Two late payments in 90 days
- Rapid increase in open balance
- Repeated invoice disputes

AI should trigger the review workflow the moment these signals appear.

## 2) Use AI risk scoring across payment and behavior signals

Build a lightweight score from data you already have:

- Days sales outstanding (DSO) trend
- % of invoices paid late
- Average lateness days
- Dispute frequency
- Concentration risk (single large open balance)

The goal is not perfect prediction — it is prioritization. A directional score is enough to route attention where it matters.

## 3) Automate review packets for finance approvers

Manual reviews are slow because context is scattered.

AI can compile a single review packet with:

- Current exposure and credit limit usage
- Last 6-12 months payment trend summary
- Recent disputes and root-cause categories
- Recommended action (keep, tighten, escalate)

Approvers can then make faster decisions without digging through multiple tools.

## 4) Route actions automatically after each review

Every review should produce a concrete next step:

- Maintain terms
- Reduce credit limit
- Switch to milestone or partial prepayment
- Require additional approver
- Place account on temporary hold

AI can push these actions directly into your billing/CRM workflow so policy changes happen immediately.

## 5) Measure credit control quality weekly

Track a short set of metrics:

- High-risk account count trend
- Overdue exposure by risk tier
- Time from risk trigger to review completion
- % of reviews completed on schedule
- Bad-debt write-off trend

AI-generated weekly summaries help teams spot drift early and adjust thresholds before problems compound.

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Overcomplicated scoring models**
   If no one trusts the score, no one acts on it.

2. **No trigger ownership**
   Automation without an accountable owner becomes background noise.

3. **Static limits with dynamic behavior**
   Customer risk changes; credit control policy must adapt.

4. **Review without execution**
   Insights are useless if terms and limits are not updated in systems.

## 30-day implementation plan

**Week 1:** Define risk tiers, triggers, and escalation policies.
**Week 2:** Connect AR/payment data and generate baseline risk scores.
**Week 3:** Launch automated review packets and approval flow.
**Week 4:** Add action routing + weekly KPI summary.

Success indicators:

- Fewer invoices reaching 30+ and 60+ days overdue
- Faster response to new risk signals
- Higher review completion rate
- Lower unexpected credit losses

## Final take

Credit control should run as a system, not a quarterly cleanup.

With AI, finance teams can review sooner, act faster, and keep risk inside clear operating boundaries.
