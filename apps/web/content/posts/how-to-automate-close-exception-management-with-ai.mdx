Most close delays are not caused by normal tasks.

They come from **exceptions**: missing support, unresolved variances, late approvals, and broken handoffs.

AI helps teams treat exceptions like an operating system instead of one-off fire drills.

## Why close exceptions pile up

Common patterns:

- **Exceptions are logged inconsistently** across email, chat, and spreadsheets
- **Severity is subjective** and changes person-to-person
- **Ownership is unclear** when issues span accounting, FP&A, and operations
- **Escalations happen too late** because no one sees the full queue
- **Root causes are never codified**, so the same exception returns next month

Without structure, exceptions become invisible until they threaten the close date.

## What AI can automate in exception workflows

### 1) Intake and normalization

AI can parse exception submissions (email, ticket notes, comments) and standardize each item into:

- exception type
- impacted entity/account/process
- materiality estimate
- owner and backup owner
- SLA target and due date

### 2) Severity scoring and routing

Based on predefined rules, AI can score risk and route exceptions automatically:

- low risk → queue for batch review
- medium risk → owner + reviewer notification
- high risk → immediate escalation path

### 3) Daily exception summaries

AI can generate concise updates for close leaders:

- newly opened exceptions
- exceptions at risk of SLA breach
- unresolved high-risk exceptions
- blockers likely to delay sign-off

### 4) Recurrence and root-cause patterns

AI can cluster repeated exceptions by account, team, or workflow and flag top recurrence drivers for process fixes.

## Practical rollout plan

1. Define your close exception schema (type, severity, owner, SLA, status).
2. Centralize exception capture in one workflow tool.
3. Add AI normalization and severity scoring on intake.
4. Implement rule-based escalation thresholds.
5. Review recurrence trends after each close and assign preventive actions.

## Metrics to track over the next 3 close cycles

- exception volume by severity
- percent resolved within SLA
- average time-to-resolution for high-risk exceptions
- number of repeat exceptions by root cause
- close delay attributable to unresolved exceptions

## Final takeaway

Close speed improves when exception handling is systematic, not heroic.

AI gives finance teams a reliable way to classify, route, and resolve exceptions before they turn into end-of-cycle surprises.
