## What Operations Teams Actually Do

Operations teams are responsible for everything that keeps an organization running: vendor management, process documentation, compliance, reporting, facilities, finance operations, and the endless parade of cross-functional initiatives that don't fit neatly in any other department.

The work is diverse, often reactive, and heavily documentation-intensive. AI tools that reduce documentation burden and surface information faster have immediate ROI for ops teams.

### 3. Process Documentation

Documenting processes is one of those things operations teams know they should do more of but rarely find time for. The bottleneck is usually the writing itself—turning what someone knows how to do into a clear, step-by-step document.

AI writing assistants (ChatGPT, Claude) can draft process documentation from:
- Voice recordings of someone walking through the process
- Bullet points or rough notes
- Existing documentation that needs to be updated

The key is to use AI to produce a first draft quickly, then have the process owner review and refine. This cuts documentation time by 60-70%.

## Building an AI-Augmented Operations Workflow

The most effective operations teams don't just use individual AI tools—they build AI into their standard workflows. A few patterns that work well:

**Post-meeting workflow:**
1. Record all significant meetings
2. Run recordings through meeting summarizer
3. Paste structured notes into project management system
4. Assign action items directly from AI output

**Vendor management workflow:**
1. Upload new vendor contracts to document analyzer
2. Extract and store key terms (renewal dates, SLAs, escalators) in a central tracker
3. Set calendar reminders for renewal notice deadlines
4. Review SLA performance summaries before quarterly business reviews

**Expense processing workflow:**
1. Export credit card transactions monthly
2. Run through expense categorizer
3. Review and correct categorizations
4. Import into accounting system

Each workflow saves 2-4 hours per week per person on tasks that previously required manual attention.

## What to Automate vs. What to Keep Human

AI handles extraction, summarization, categorization, and formatting well. Keep humans in the loop for:

- **Judgment calls** — When a vendor contract has non-standard terms that require negotiation, AI identifies the issue but humans decide how to handle it
- **Relationship management** — Vendor relationships involve trust and context that AI can't replicate
- **Exception handling** — When something doesn't fit the normal pattern, human judgment is required
- **Final approvals** — AI output should be reviewed before consequential decisions are made

The goal isn't to remove humans from operations—it's to remove humans from the mechanical work so they can focus on the judgment-intensive work.
