# How to Use AI for Grant Writing and Management

Grant writing is competitive, time-consuming, and document-intensive. For nonprofits and research institutions, grant funding is existential — but the process of winning and managing grants consumes staff capacity that could otherwise be spent on mission delivery.

AI tools are changing the math.

## The Grant Management Document Load

A typical grant cycle involves:

**Pre-award (grant writing):**
- RFP and funder guidelines analysis
- Literature and precedent research
- Budget development and justification
- Proposal drafting and editing
- Compliance review

**Post-award (grant management):**
- Funder meeting documentation
- Progress report preparation
- Financial reporting and expense categorization
- Amendment requests and change orders
- Grant closeout documentation

The document volume at every stage is substantial.

## Where AI Tools Provide the Most Value

### RFP and Funder Guidelines Analysis

Before writing a single word of a proposal, you need to thoroughly understand what the funder is looking for. RFPs can run 20–50 pages with appendices, eligibility requirements, scoring criteria, and submission instructions.

AI contract/document analysis tools can process an RFP and extract:
- Eligibility requirements
- Funding priorities and focus areas
- Evaluation criteria and weighting
- Required narrative sections and page limits
- Budget constraints and allowable costs
- Compliance requirements and certifications needed

This analysis takes minutes instead of hours, and it produces a structured checklist you can use throughout the writing process.

### Funder Meeting Summarization

Pre-submission technical assistance calls with program officers and post-award check-in calls with funders contain critical information about expectations, flexibility, and program priorities. Taking notes during these calls while also participating fully is difficult.

AI meeting summarizers produce structured summaries from call recordings or transcripts:
- Key funder priorities expressed
- Guidance on scope or approach
- Action items for the grantee
- Clarifications on allowable activities

This documentation is valuable both for current grant management and for institutional knowledge about funder relationships.

### Progress Report Preparation

Progress reports — typically due quarterly or annually — require synthesizing program data, narrative updates, and financial information into a structured report. AI tools can:

- Summarize collected program documentation into narrative sections
- Organize meeting notes and call summaries into coherent progress narratives
- Help structure budget variance explanations
- Flag required sections from the grant agreement that need to be addressed

The starting point is a structured draft rather than a blank page.

### Financial Documentation and Expense Categorization

Grant financial management requires careful expense categorization against approved budget categories. AI expense tools can:
- Categorize expenses by grant budget line
- Flag expenses that may require prior approval
- Identify allocation issues across multiple grants
- Speed up monthly financial reconciliation

### Grant Agreement Review

Grant agreements — especially from government funders and large foundations — contain extensive compliance requirements. AI contract analysis can extract the key obligations, reporting deadlines, allowable cost provisions, and audit requirements so grant managers can build a compliance calendar without reading 80 pages of legalese.

## Practical Implementation for Nonprofits

**Grant development teams** benefit most from RFP analysis and proposal review workflows.

**Grant managers and program staff** benefit most from meeting summarization and progress report preparation.
