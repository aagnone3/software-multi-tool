Financial statement tie-out is one of the highest-friction steps in close.

Teams have to prove that source schedules, subledgers, and final statements all agree before signoff. That usually means too many spreadsheets, too much manual checking, and late-cycle surprises.

AI can automate most of the tie-out workflow while keeping approval control with finance.

## Why tie-out work slows down close

Most teams run into the same problems:

- version drift across supporting schedules
- inconsistent account labels between systems
- manual cross-foot checks that are easy to miss under deadline pressure
- unresolved breaks that bounce between accounting, FP&A, and operations

The result is slower signoff and less confidence in reporting quality.

## A practical AI tie-out workflow

1. **Ingest source schedules and statement drafts**
   Pull trial balance exports, subledger summaries, and statement line-item mappings into one structured workspace.

2. **Standardize account and entity mappings**
   Normalize naming differences so the same account does not appear as multiple items.

3. **Run automated tie-out checks**
   Compare statement balances to supporting schedules, flagging mismatches by amount, account, entity, and period.

4. **Rank exceptions by materiality and risk**
   Prioritize breaks that are large, recurring, or near reporting deadlines.

5. **Generate first-pass exception narratives**
   AI drafts likely causes based on historical adjustments and recent journal activity.

6. **Route exceptions with owner + due date**
   Assign each break to a responsible owner and track status to closure.

7. **Produce signoff-ready tie-out packet**
   Export a reviewer packet with resolved items, open risks, and supporting evidence links.

## Where to start

Pilot on 3 to 5 high-impact statement sections first:

- cash and cash equivalents
- accounts receivable and allowance
- revenue and deferred revenue
- accrued liabilities

Track these metrics over two close cycles:

- time to first complete tie-out pass
- number of late discovered breaks
- number of review-round reopenings

## Controls that keep automation audit-friendly

- **Human approval required for final signoff**
- **Immutable logs of checks and exception status changes**
- **Period-locked snapshots of tie-out evidence**
- **Role-based access to sensitive schedules**

## What success looks like

When this process is working, teams usually see:

- faster tie-out completion before close deadlines
- fewer surprise adjustments in final review
- clearer accountability for unresolved breaks
- higher confidence in statement accuracy at signoff

If close quality is strong but tie-out still takes too long, automate this workflow next. It reduces manual reconciliation load and gives finance leaders cleaner evidence for final approval.
