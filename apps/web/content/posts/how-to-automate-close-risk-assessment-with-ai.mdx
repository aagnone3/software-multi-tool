Most close delays are not caused by one catastrophic error.

They come from many small unresolved risks that were visible early but not prioritized fast enough.

AI helps finance teams score close risk continuously so reviewers can focus where failure probability is highest.

## Why close risk assessment is usually reactive

Teams often discover risk too late because:

1. **Exception lists are too broad** and lack severity ranking
2. **Account reviewers use inconsistent thresholds** for escalation
3. **Dependencies across tasks** are tracked manually in spreadsheets
4. **Late adjustments** are treated as isolated events rather than risk signals

The result is last-minute fire drills and uneven close quality.

## What to automate first

### 1) Risk scoring for close tasks

AI can score each task or account based on risk indicators such as:

- prior-period rework frequency
- current-period variance size and direction
- aging unresolved reconciling items
- preparer/reviewer SLA misses

Use the score to drive reviewer queue order instead of first-in-first-out.

### 2) Exception severity classification

Not every exception should block close.

AI can classify exceptions into:

- **informational** (monitor)
- **warning** (resolve before final review)
- **critical** (requires escalation and sign-off)

This keeps high-impact issues from being buried in low-priority noise.

### 3) Dependency-aware escalation

Close risk compounds when upstream tasks slip.

AI can detect dependency chains (for example, subledger tie-out -> accrual posting -> management reporting) and automatically escalate downstream tasks likely to miss cutoff.

### 4) Daily risk digest for controllers

Generate a daily close-risk digest with:

- top 10 highest-risk tasks/accounts
- newly elevated critical items
- unresolved items approaching cutoff
- owners and recommended next action

This creates fast, action-oriented visibility for close leadership.

## Rollout plan

1. Start with one entity or one close workstream (e.g., AR/AP reconciliations).
2. Define risk factors and thresholds with controllership.
3. Run AI scoring in shadow mode for 1-2 closes.
4. Compare AI risk calls against actual close incidents.
5. Move from dashboard-only to automated escalation workflows.

## Metrics that prove impact

Track quality and predictability metrics:

- number of critical close issues identified before day -2
- % high-risk items resolved before cutoff
- average escalation response time
- close timeline variance month over month
- post-close adjustment volume

## Final take

Close risk management is a prioritization problem. AI gives finance teams a consistent, always-on way to detect and rank risk early, so close outcomes become more predictable with less heroics.
