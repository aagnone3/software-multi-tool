Entity-level review is where many close delays first become visible.

A parent-level P&L can look stable while one entity has unusual margin shifts, accrual swings, or unexplained balance-sheet movements. If those issues are found late, the entire close gets compressed.

AI helps by running a consistent first-pass review across every entity before leadership review starts.

## Why entity-level close review breaks down

Most teams run into the same pattern:

1. **Review depth is uneven.** Larger entities get attention while smaller ones are scanned quickly.
2. **Thresholds drift by reviewer.** "Material" means different things across teams.
3. **Commentary quality varies.** Some entities provide clear explanations; others send one-line notes.

That inconsistency creates avoidable escalation work in the final close window.

## What AI should handle first

The goal is to produce a prioritized review queue, not replace accounting judgment.

### 1) Detect unusual entity movements

For each legal entity, score movements across key metrics:

- revenue and gross margin changes
- operating expense swings
- working capital deltas
- unusual balance-sheet account movement

Use both absolute and percentage thresholds so small and large entities are treated fairly.

### 2) Surface likely root-cause categories

Classify each flagged movement into practical buckets such as:

- timing difference
- reclass or mapping error
- accrual/reversal mismatch
- one-time transaction
- operational volume shift

This gives reviewers a structured starting point for investigation.

### 3) Draft standardized review commentary

Generate draft commentary per entity with:

- what changed
- likely driver
- evidence needed to confirm
- owner and due date

Standardized draft language reduces rework in leadership review packs.

## Implementation pattern that works

Use a simple repeatable flow:

1. Pull entity-level trial balance and prior-period baseline.
2. Run AI scoring with agreed materiality thresholds.
3. Route flagged exceptions to entity owners.
4. Require owner confirmation or correction of draft commentary.
5. Export finalized commentary into close review materials.

Keep all outputs editable and linked to source data.

## Governance controls to keep confidence high

Before scaling, define a few non-negotiables:

- human sign-off on every material exception
- audit log of AI output and reviewer edits
- fixed threshold policy by account class
- mandatory "evidence attached" field for cleared items

These controls keep automation fast while preserving control quality.

## Metrics to track in the first quarter

Track outcomes that matter to close quality:

- time to complete entity-level review
- number of late-cycle entity escalations
- percentage of issues resolved in first review pass
- amount of commentary rewritten by controllers

If those trend in the right direction, your entity-review workflow is improving.

## Where this fits in close automation

Entity-level review automation pairs well with adjacent workflows like close variance analysis, reconciliation escalation routing, and signoff package preparation.

AI handles repeatable triage and first drafts. Finance leaders still own judgment, approvals, and final accountability.
