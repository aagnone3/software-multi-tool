Write-off reviews usually happen too late.

Collectors keep chasing low-probability accounts while high-impact decisions sit in queues waiting for context to be assembled.

AI can help you standardize write-off reviews, surface the right evidence, and route each case to the right approver.

## 1) Define objective write-off criteria first

Before automation, set clear policy inputs:

- Days overdue thresholds by customer segment
- Balance amount thresholds for escalation levels
- Minimum collection attempts required
- Dispute and bankruptcy handling rules
- Required approvers by amount/risk

AI performs best when the decision framework is explicit.

## 2) Build a daily candidate list from your AR ledger

Generate a daily queue of accounts potentially eligible for write-off review.

Include:

- Total exposure and oldest invoice age
- Last payment date and payment trend
- Promise-to-pay history and outcomes
- Open dispute flags
- Existing collection notes and contact history

This gives the team one prioritized queue instead of ad hoc spreadsheets.

## 3) Use AI to summarize account-level recovery likelihood

For each candidate account, have AI summarize:

- Collection efforts completed
- Signals of recovery vs non-recovery
- Root cause category (dispute, insolvency risk, no response, etc.)
- Recommended next step: continue collections, escalate, or submit for write-off review

Keep this as a recommendation layer—not final approval.

## 4) Auto-generate a write-off review memo

When an account meets policy thresholds, create a memo draft with:

- Exposure breakdown
- Timeline of attempts and outcomes
- Key risk factors
- Recommended action and rationale
- Policy criteria satisfied

Approvers should get a decision-ready packet, not raw notes.

## 5) Route approvals by deterministic rules

Use hard routing logic based on exposure and risk.

Examples:

- Under $5k: AR manager
- $5k–$25k: finance controller
- Over $25k or legal risk: CFO + legal review

AI can suggest urgency and provide summaries, but routing should stay rule-based.

## 6) Keep a full audit trail automatically

Every write-off recommendation should log:

- Input data snapshot
- Policy checks passed/failed
- AI summary version used
- Final approver, timestamp, and decision

This is critical for audit readiness and policy compliance.

## 7) Monitor write-off quality monthly

Track outcome metrics:

- Write-off rate by segment
- Recoveries after "pending write-off" stage
- Approval cycle time
- Exception rate (approver overrides)

Use these metrics to tighten thresholds and improve recommendation quality.

## Common mistakes

1. **Treating AI output as final decision**
   Keep human approval as the control point.

2. **No standard evidence packet**
   Approvers make slower, inconsistent decisions without structured context.

3. **Mixing dispute and non-response paths**
   Disputed balances need different workflows than silent accounts.

4. **No feedback loop**
   If you don’t measure override and recovery outcomes, policy quality stalls.

## Final take

Write-off review is a decision workflow, not a one-off judgment call.

Use AI to prepare evidence, rank candidates, and draft decision memos—while deterministic rules and human approvals control final write-offs.

That combination speeds decisions, improves consistency, and strengthens financial governance.
