Real estate transactions involve mountains of documents: purchase agreements, leases, inspection reports, environmental assessments, title reports, financial statements. AI document analysis is transforming how investors handle due diligence.

## The Due Diligence Document Problem

A typical commercial real estate acquisition involves:
- Purchase and Sale Agreement (50-100 pages)
- Multiple leases (20-60 pages each for anchor tenants)
- Inspection reports (environmental, structural, mechanical)
- Rent rolls and historical operating statements
- Title commitments and exception documents
- Zoning and permit history

Reviewing all of this manually takes weeks. AI can compress that timeline significantly.

## AI Use Cases for Real Estate Investors

### Lease Abstraction

Lease abstraction — extracting key terms from long leases — is one of the clearest AI wins in real estate. AI can pull:
- Lease commencement and expiration dates
- Rent amounts and escalation schedules
- Renewal and termination options
- Tenant improvement allowances
- CAM and expense obligations
- Assignment and subletting restrictions

What takes an attorney 2-3 hours per lease takes AI 2-3 minutes.

### Inspection Report Summarization

Environmental Phase I reports and property condition assessments are detailed technical documents. AI can produce executive summaries highlighting material findings, recommended actions, and estimated remediation costs.

### Financial Statement Analysis

Operating statements and rent rolls contain patterns that AI can identify quickly: below-market rents, expense anomalies, vacancy trends, NOI calculation issues.

### Purchase Agreement Review

AI can flag non-standard terms in purchase agreements, identify due diligence deadline dates, summarize contingencies, and compare against market-standard language.

## Residential vs. Commercial

**Residential investors** benefit most from lease analysis (for rentals), inspection report summarization, and purchase contract review.

**Commercial investors** get the most from lease abstraction (especially for multi-tenant properties), offering memorandum summarization, and financial statement analysis.

## Getting Started

Most real estate investors start with lease abstraction on existing portfolio leases — immediate value without waiting for a new deal. From there, add inspection report summarization for active due diligence, then purchase agreement review for new acquisitions.

The time savings compound: instead of spending your due diligence period reading documents, you spend it making decisions based on synthesized information.
