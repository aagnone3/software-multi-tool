Risk management is fundamentally a documentation and analysis discipline. Risk managers read hundreds of documents, analyze data across multiple domains, and synthesize findings into actionable guidance. AI tools are becoming essential for keeping up with that volume.

## What Risk Managers Actually Do (And Where AI Helps)

Risk managers spend significant time on:

- **Reading and synthesizing reports** — vendor assessments, audit findings, incident reports, regulatory updates
- **Contract analysis** — reviewing vendor agreements for risk language, liability caps, data protection clauses
- **Documentation** — writing risk assessments, maintaining risk registers, preparing board presentations
- **Meeting documentation** — capturing risk committee discussions and decisions

AI tools don't replace risk judgment. They handle the reading, summarizing, and first-draft creation so risk managers can apply judgment to a smaller, better-curated set of information.

## Practical Use Cases

### Third-Party Vendor Risk Review

When onboarding a new vendor:

**Before AI:** Read the full vendor agreement (30-60 min), manually identify risk clauses, write assessment memo (60-90 min).

**With AI:** Run contract through Contract Analyzer (3 min), review flagged clauses (15 min), add context to AI-extracted findings for the memo (20 min).

**Time saved:** 60-90 minutes per vendor review.

### Regulatory Change Management

When a new regulation or guidance document drops:

**Before AI:** Read full document (2-4 hours for lengthy regulations), highlight relevant sections, write gap analysis.

**With AI:** Upload to Document Summarizer, get structured summary of key requirements and deadlines (5 min), review and validate AI interpretation (30 min), develop gap analysis from verified summary.

**Time saved:** 1-3 hours per regulatory update.

### Risk Committee Documentation

**Before AI:** Manually write meeting minutes, action item tracker, and distributable summary after each risk committee meeting.

**With AI:** Record meeting, run through Meeting Summarizer, get speaker-labeled transcript and structured summary with action items automatically.

**Time saved:** 1-2 hours per meeting cycle.

## Integrating AI Into Your Risk Framework

A few principles for responsible AI use in risk management:

1. **Verify AI extractions** — always review AI-identified contract clauses against the source document before acting on them. AI catches most things but isn't perfect.

2. **Document your AI use** — note when AI tools assisted with analysis in your risk documentation. This is increasingly important for audit trails.

3. **Calibrate threshold expectations** — AI summaries are first drafts. For high-stakes risk decisions, the AI summary is a starting point, not the end point.

4. **Start with low-stakes documents** — test AI tools on internal documents first before using them for external-facing risk assessments.
