Close adjustments don’t usually fail because of accounting logic.

They fail because approvals get stuck in the wrong queue, too late in the cycle, with weak context.

AI can route each adjustment to the right reviewer, at the right priority, with the right evidence attached.

## Why adjustment routing breaks during close

Most teams still route approvals with static rules like “send all adjustments above X to Controller.”

That breaks when close pressure rises:

1. High-risk entries wait behind low-risk entries
2. Reviewer capacity gets overloaded unevenly
3. Escalations happen too late to protect deadlines
4. Approval evidence is scattered across systems

AI helps by combining risk context and routing policy in one repeatable decision layer.

## Build an AI-assisted adjustment approval flow

### 1) Define approval policy by risk tier

Segment adjustments using practical dimensions:

- Amount and materiality threshold
- Account sensitivity
- Entry timing (early vs final-close window)
- Supporting documentation completeness

Then map each tier to required approver levels and evidence standards.

### 2) Classify each adjustment before queueing

When an adjustment is created, AI should assign:

- Risk tier (low, medium, high)
- Required approver role
- SLA deadline by close checkpoint
- Missing-document flags

This prevents “first-come, first-served” routing from dominating high-stakes days.

### 3) Route to the right owner with explicit fallback

Each routed item should include:

- Primary approver
- Backup approver path if SLA is missed
- Escalation trigger timing
- Current close phase context

Fallback rules matter most when leaders are in overlapping review meetings.

### 4) Attach approval-ready context automatically

Package context directly in the approval task:

- Proposed entry and account impact
- Why the adjustment is needed
- Source support references
- Prior similar adjustments this quarter

Approvers should not have to assemble evidence manually while the clock is running.

### 5) Capture decision evidence by default

For every decision, store:

- Approver identity and timestamp
- Decision (approve/reject/request changes)
- Rationale text
- Linked documentation snapshot

This turns late-close approvals into defensible audit artifacts.

## Example routed adjustment

- **Adjustment:** Revenue deferral correction
- **Amount:** $142,000
- **Risk tier:** High
- **Route:** Senior Accountant → Controller fallback at 90 minutes
- **SLA:** Final decision before close checkpoint T-3 hours

## Common rollout mistakes

- Routing based only on amount, ignoring account risk
- No backup approver path for SLA misses
- Requiring manual evidence gathering at review time
- Measuring queue volume instead of cycle-time and quality

The objective is faster, safer approvals—not just more tickets moved.

## KPIs to track each month-end cycle

- Median approval cycle time by risk tier
- Percent of high-risk adjustments approved within SLA
- Escalation rate due to missed primary approver deadlines
- Percent of approvals with complete evidence package

These measures show whether routing is improving control quality and close speed.

## Final takeaway

AI should orchestrate adjustment approval flow, not replace accounting judgment.

Use it to route, prioritize, and document decisions so your finance team can close faster with stronger control confidence.
