Prepaid expense amortization looks straightforward until month-end pressure hits.

Teams still track schedules in spreadsheets, copy entries manually, and chase document links when auditors ask for support. The accounting is deterministic — but the process is often messy.

AI can make this workflow faster and more reliable by standardizing source data, drafting schedules, and flagging anomalies before entries are posted.

## Where prepaid amortization usually breaks

Most problems are operational, not conceptual:

- contract terms are buried in PDFs and email threads
- start/end dates are interpreted inconsistently
- schedule changes are not reflected in the next close
- support docs are not linked to each journal entry

That creates late entries, reversal mistakes, and avoidable audit questions.

## What AI should do (and what it should not)

Use AI to prepare and validate. Keep final posting and policy decisions with finance owners.

Good automation targets:

1. **Extract key terms** (amount, term length, service period, renewal clauses)
2. **Generate amortization schedules** with monthly allocation logic
3. **Detect exceptions** (date mismatches, unusually large period swings)
4. **Assemble support packets** tied to each journal entry

Do not allow unsupervised posting to the GL without review controls.

## A rollout that works in real teams

### 1) Create one intake format for prepaid documents

Require a consistent set of fields:

- vendor
- total prepaid amount
- service start and end date
- amortization method
- cost center / department
- source document link

Even a simple form-based intake can eliminate half the downstream cleanup.

### 2) Auto-build draft schedules from extracted terms

For each new prepaid item, generate:

- monthly amortization amount
- first and final period adjustments (if partial month)
- remaining balance by period
- expected journal entry metadata

Flag any schedule where extracted terms are ambiguous.

### 3) Add policy checks before close

Before entries are approved, run checks for:

- missing source documents
- term dates outside policy boundaries
- overlap with existing prepaid schedules
- material variance from prior month trends

Send exceptions to a reviewer queue instead of blocking all entries.

## Controls that keep automation safe

- preparer/approver separation for every posted entry
- immutable link from entry to source contract/invoice
- threshold-based escalation for high-dollar prepaids
- monthly reconciliation of prepaid roll-forward to schedules

If these controls are absent, automation will only hide problems faster.

## How to measure whether it's working

Track impact over at least two close cycles:

- time spent preparing prepaid schedules
- count of late or corrected prepaid entries
- reviewer turnaround time
- audit follow-up requests on prepaid support

You want fewer corrections and faster reviewer decisions — not just faster draft generation.

## Bottom line

Prepaid amortization should be predictable work, not end-of-month firefighting.

AI helps when it is used to standardize inputs, automate schedule prep, and surface exceptions early. Keep final approvals with finance leadership, and you get speed **and** control.

Start with one prepaid category (for example software subscriptions), prove the process, then expand.
