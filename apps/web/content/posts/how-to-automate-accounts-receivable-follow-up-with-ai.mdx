If your collections process depends on someone remembering to “send a reminder,” cash will always lag.

The problem is not effort — it’s consistency.

AI helps AR teams run a repeatable follow-up system: who to contact, when to contact them, what to say, and when to escalate.

## 1) Build a follow-up cadence by invoice age

Start with a clear sequence tied to days past due:

- **3 days before due date:** friendly reminder with payment link
- **Day 1 overdue:** confirmation-style nudge
- **Day 7 overdue:** firmer reminder + offer to clarify billing details
- **Day 14 overdue:** escalation to finance manager
- **Day 21+ overdue:** hold/collections policy action

AI can automatically queue each touchpoint based on invoice metadata.

## 2) Prioritize accounts by collection risk, not just amount

A $2,000 invoice from a chronically late payer may need more attention than a $10,000 invoice from a reliable customer.

Use AI to score AR risk using:

- Historical payment behavior
- Dispute frequency
- Invoice age trends
- Contract/payment term complexity

This lets your team focus human effort where it changes outcomes most.

## 3) Personalize reminder language without manual rewriting

Most reminders fail because they sound generic.

AI can generate message variants by customer segment:

- Enterprise account (formal, includes PO references)
- SMB account (concise, direct payment CTA)
- Disputed invoice path (problem-solving tone)

Keep templates approved by finance/legal, then let AI fill context fields and tone.

## 4) Detect likely blockers early

Late payment is often a process issue, not refusal.

Use AI to detect common blockers from reply text and notes:

- Missing PO number
- Incorrect billing contact
- Invoice mismatch
- Pending internal approval

Route each blocker to the right owner automatically (billing ops, account manager, finance).

## 5) Track collection performance with weekly AR metrics

At minimum, monitor:

- DSO trend
- Overdue buckets (1–30, 31–60, 61–90)
- Promise-to-pay conversion rate
- Follow-up completion rate
- Collection velocity by customer segment

AI can summarize what moved week-over-week and highlight which actions actually improved collections.

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **One-size-fits-all escalation**
   Not every customer should get the same cadence.

2. **No payment-link friction check**
   If paying is hard, reminders won’t fix it.

3. **Ignoring response classification**
   “We need a corrected invoice” is not the same as silence.

4. **No owner for exceptions**
   Automation should route exceptions, not hide them.

## A practical 30-day rollout

**Week 1:** Define reminder cadence and escalation rules.
**Week 2:** Connect invoice data and build customer risk segments.
**Week 3:** Launch AI-generated reminders with approved templates.
**Week 4:** Add blocker routing and weekly AR summary reporting.

Success metrics:

- Reduction in DSO
- Increase in on-time payment rate
- Reduction in 60+ day overdue balance
- Collections workload per AR team member

## Final take

AR follow-up should run like a system, not a scramble.

AI gives finance teams consistent execution, better prioritization, and faster issue detection — which means cash arrives sooner and forecasting gets easier.
