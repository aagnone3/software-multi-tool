Client reporting is one of the most time-consuming recurring tasks in professional services. AI can reduce the time dramatically without sacrificing quality.

## What AI Can (and Can't) Do for Client Reports

**AI can:**
- Summarize source data documents into executive summaries
- Extract key metrics from financial statements, analytics reports, and spreadsheets
- Convert meeting transcripts into structured report sections
- Generate first drafts of narrative commentary
- Flag anomalies or notable changes worth highlighting

**AI can't (yet):**
- Replace judgment about what matters to a specific client
- Make final editorial decisions
- Handle highly regulated content without human review
- Access real-time data without integration

## A Practical Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Source Materials
Collect the documents that will feed the report: data exports, meeting transcripts, previous period reports.

### Step 2: Run Through AI Summarization
Feed each source document through an AI summarizer. Configure the output format to match your report structure — bullet points, executive summary, detailed section.

### Step 3: Assemble the Draft
Combine AI-generated sections into your report template. Most of the narrative is done; you're editing, not writing.

### Step 4: Human Review Pass
Review for accuracy, tone, and client-specific context. Add any forward-looking commentary or strategic recommendations that require judgment.

### Step 5: Distribute
Send final report. Log the output for future reference and compliance documentation.

## Choosing the Right AI Tool

Look for tools that:
- Support your document types (PDF, Excel, audio transcripts)
- Maintain formatting consistency
- Allow customizable output templates
- Support batch processing for multiple clients
- Provide audit trails for compliance

The best tools treat AI as an accelerator for your existing workflow, not a replacement for your expertise.
