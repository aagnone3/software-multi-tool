# How to Use AI for Knowledge Management

Most organizational knowledge exists in three places: people's heads, unstructured documents, and meeting recordings nobody rewatches. When someone leaves or a project ends, that knowledge often disappears.

AI is changing how teams capture and retrieve institutional knowledge.

## The Knowledge Management Problem

Traditional approaches to knowledge management fail because:
- Wikis require manual updates nobody has time for
- Meeting notes are rarely comprehensive
- Documents pile up faster than they can be organized
- Search across disparate sources is painful

AI addresses the capture and retrieval problem.

## AI Use Cases for Knowledge Management

### 1. Automated Meeting Documentation

Every meeting contains decisions, context, and institutional knowledge. AI meeting summarizers automatically convert recordings into searchable, structured notes.

**What gets captured automatically:**
- Key decisions and rationale
- Action items and owners
- Questions raised and answers given
- Follow-up topics

## Building a Knowledge Management System with AI

A practical AI-powered knowledge management workflow:

1. **Capture**: All meetings auto-summarized → searchable notes
2. **Extract**: Key documents processed → structured data
3. **Monitor**: Industry news filtered → relevant updates surfaced
4. **Retrieve**: Semantic search across all captured knowledge

## What to Look for in AI Knowledge Management Tools

- **Accuracy**: Does the AI correctly capture what was said and decided?
- **Integration**: Does it connect to your existing tools (Notion, Confluence, Slack)?
- **Privacy**: Where is the data stored? Who has access?
- **Search quality**: Can you find information with natural language queries?

## ROI of AI Knowledge Management

Teams that systematically capture meeting and document knowledge with AI report:
- 30-50% reduction in "reinventing the wheel" time
- Faster onboarding for new team members
- Fewer repeated discussions about already-resolved questions
