Intercompany reconciliations are one of the fastest ways to lose time during close. Two entities book the same transaction differently, references don’t align, and someone has to manually untangle the mismatch.

AI helps by surfacing those breaks earlier and routing each exception to the right owner before close week turns into cleanup week.

## Why intercompany reconciliations break down

Most teams hit recurring issues:

- entity A and entity B post in different periods
- document references are inconsistent across ERPs
- FX treatment differs between local and reporting currency books
- one side books net while the other books gross
- dispute notes live in email instead of a shared workflow

Without a structured exception process, the same issues repeat every month.

## A practical AI-assisted intercompany workflow

1. **Collect both sides of each intercompany pair**
   Pull source entries from each entity and map them by counterparty, account, amount, and transaction date.

2. **Normalize IDs and supporting metadata**
   Standardize invoice IDs, contract references, and currency fields so matching logic compares like-for-like records.

3. **Run deterministic matching first**
   Auto-match exact pairs on amount/date/reference before introducing AI-assisted matching.

4. **Classify exceptions by root cause**
   Use AI to categorize mismatches into timing differences, FX translation issues, missing accruals, mapping errors, and duplicate postings.

5. **Generate action queues per entity owner**
   Route each exception with recommended next step, evidence links, and materiality score.

6. **Produce reconciliation-ready summaries**
   Auto-draft account and entity-level summaries showing matched volume, open exceptions, and aging.

## What to automate first

Start where mismatch volume is highest:

- intercompany revenue and COGS pairings
- management fee / shared service allocations
- intercompany loans and interest accruals
- cross-entity recharges

You’ll get the fastest payoff by automating high-frequency flows before edge-case transactions.

## Guardrails that keep reconciliations auditable

- **Keep rule-based matching as the baseline:** AI should handle ambiguity, not override clear accounting rules.
- **Persist match rationale:** store why each pair matched (rule or model signal).
- **Apply materiality thresholds:** prioritize what can actually impact reporting.
- **Require human approval for proposed adjustments:** AI suggests, controllers approve.

## What success looks like

When this is implemented well, teams usually see:

- fewer late close escalations between entity owners
- shorter intercompany issue resolution cycles
- cleaner audit support with less manual stitching
- more predictable close timelines across entities

---

If intercompany reconciliation still depends on email threads and ad hoc spreadsheets, start by automating exception classification and owner routing. That typically delivers a measurable close-time reduction within a quarter.
