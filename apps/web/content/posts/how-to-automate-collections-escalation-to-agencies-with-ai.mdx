Escalating accounts to a collections agency is a leverage decision.

Done too early, you damage customer relationships and pay unnecessary fees.
Done too late, recovery odds collapse.

## 1) Define escalation policy before automation

Start with deterministic rules the business agrees on:

- Minimum aging thresholds (for example 90/120+ days)
- Balance thresholds by customer segment
- Required in-house contact attempts before escalation
- Exclusion rules (active disputes, strategic accounts, legal holds)

AI should support this policy, not invent it.

## 2) Standardize account-level escalation inputs

Most placement delays come from fragmented notes.
Create one required account summary payload:

- Total overdue balance and aging buckets
- Invoice count and oldest invoice date
- Contact history (email/call cadence and last response)
- Promise-to-pay history and broken commitments
- Dispute status and open issues
- Account priority tier and relationship owner

Consistent inputs produce consistent outcomes.

## 3) Use AI to generate escalation briefs

Approvers do not need raw notes—they need signal.
Generate a concise brief for each candidate account:

- Current risk and likelihood of recovery in-house
- Key reasons for escalation recommendation
- Missing evidence or unresolved blockers
- Suggested next step: keep internal, final in-house attempt, or agency placement

This reduces review time while improving decision quality.

## 4) Route by policy gates, not model opinion

Use hard controls for routing:

- **Auto-queue for review** when policy thresholds are met
- **Block escalation** if disputes or legal constraints exist
- **Escalate tier** for high-balance strategic accounts
- **Return to in-house queue** when evidence is incomplete

Policy owns authority. AI improves triage speed.

## 5) Add explainability to every recommendation

Every recommendation should be auditable with clear reason codes:

- Aging exceeds threshold
- Multiple failed outreach cycles
- Repeated broken promise-to-pay
- No active dispute / legal block
- DSO impact above target tolerance

Explainability prevents black-box escalation decisions.

## 6) Build an agency handoff packet automatically

Once approved, generate a clean handoff package:

- Customer profile and account contacts
- Invoice ledger and overdue timeline
- Communication log summary
- Supporting documents and dispute notes
- Escalation approval metadata

Good handoffs improve recovery speed and reduce agency back-and-forth.

## 7) Track post-escalation performance

Measure whether the workflow is improving outcomes:

- Recovery rate by escalation cohort
- Time-to-placement and time-to-first-payment
- Agency fee-to-recovery ratio
- False positives (accounts that could have been recovered internally)
- Complaint/dispute rates after escalation

Optimization should focus on net recoveries, not just faster escalation.

## Common mistakes

1. **Escalating based only on aging**
   Balance, customer tier, dispute context, and contact history all matter.

2. **Letting AI overrule policy**
   Recommendations should never bypass approval controls.

3. **Inconsistent handoff quality**
   Poor documentation causes agency delays and lower recovery.

4. **No feedback loop**
   If outcomes are not tracked, placement logic never improves.

## Final take

Agency escalation is a high-impact decision point in collections.

AI helps by turning fragmented account history into clear, policy-aligned escalation briefs—but strong governance, deterministic routing, and outcome tracking are what make the system reliable.
