Vendor contract review is one of those tasks that feels urgent but rarely gets the attention it deserves. Contracts pile up, legal bandwidth is limited, and business teams just want to move fast.

AI tools can help you review vendor contracts faster and more consistently — without replacing the judgment call that still requires a human.

## What Makes Vendor Contracts Hard to Review

Every vendor contract has its own structure, its own language, and its own traps. Common problem areas include:

- **Auto-renewal clauses** that lock you in without notice
- **Liability caps** that don't match the size of the deal
- **IP ownership language** buried in service schedules
- **Termination-for-convenience restrictions** that make exit difficult
- **Data processing obligations** that conflict with your privacy policy

A lawyer will catch these. An AI tool can flag them first so you know where to look.

## Step-by-Step: AI-Assisted Vendor Contract Review

### Step 1: Run a quick summary

Before anything else, get a structured summary. Upload the contract to a tool like the [Contract Analyzer](/tools/contract-analyzer) and review:

- Contract type and parties
- Term length and renewal mechanics
- Payment terms and pricing structure
- Key obligations on each side

This takes 30 seconds and tells you what you're dealing with.

### Step 2: Review risk flags

The AI will highlight unusual clauses or missing standard provisions. Common flags include:

- Uncapped liability language
- Unilateral amendment rights
- Automatic renewal without notice period
- Broad indemnification obligations

You don't need to act on every flag — but you need to know they exist.

### Step 3: Extract key dates and obligations

The contract analyzer pulls out the dates and obligations that matter:

- Effective date and initial term
- Renewal notification window
- Payment due dates
- Service level commitments

Build a tracker from these outputs rather than re-reading the contract every time a date approaches.

### Step 4: Cross-reference with your standard positions

If your organization has standard positions on common clauses (liability cap = 12 months of fees, data processing = your DPA, etc.), compare the vendor's proposed terms against those positions before escalating to legal.

AI summaries make this comparison much faster than reading the full contract.

### Step 5: Flag to legal with context

When you do escalate, send legal the AI summary with your specific questions rather than the raw contract. This makes the review faster and more focused.

## What AI Can't Do

AI can summarize, flag, and extract. It cannot:

- Negotiate terms on your behalf
- Assess strategic business risk
- Provide legal advice
- Catch every nuance in complex agreements

Use AI to do the preparation work so that when a lawyer or senior stakeholder reviews the contract, they're doing the high-value judgment call — not re-reading boilerplate.
