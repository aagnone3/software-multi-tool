You just sat through a 90-minute meeting and now you need to write up the notes. Or maybe you recorded a client call and need a quick summary. Either way, you're looking for a free AI meeting summarizer — and you don't want to pay $30/month for something you'll use twice a week.

Good news: there are solid free options. Bad news: "free" usually means something specific.

Let me break down what's actually out there.

## What Makes a Good AI Meeting Summarizer?

Before comparing tools, here's what actually matters:

- **Accurate transcription** — garbage in, garbage out. If the transcript is wrong, the summary is wrong.
- **Speaker identification** — knowing who said what is the whole point
- **Structured output** — bullet points, action items, key decisions — not a wall of text
- **File upload support** — for recorded meetings, not just live calls
- **No-code interface** — you shouldn't need to write a prompt

### 2. Otter.ai — Best for Live Meetings

Otter.ai joins your Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet calls automatically and transcribes in real time.

**What's free:** 300 minutes of transcription per month on the free plan. AI summaries are limited.

**Limitations:** The free plan doesn't include AI-generated summaries — you get a transcript but have to summarize it yourself. The AI summary feature requires a paid plan.

**Best for:** Teams that want a live meeting bot.

### 3. Fireflies.ai — Best for CRM Integration

Fireflies joins calls and can push notes to your CRM automatically.

**What's free:** Very limited. The free plan gives you a transcript but heavily limits storage and AI features.

**Best for:** Sales teams that already have a CRM workflow.

### 4. tl;dv — Best for Video Call Recording

tl;dv works specifically with Zoom and Google Meet. Free plan includes unlimited recordings and AI summaries on a limited number.

**Best for:** Video call teams who want timestamps and clip sharing.

### 5. ChatGPT — Best for Manual Paste-and-Ask

You can paste a transcript into ChatGPT and ask it to summarize. This works, but it's manual.

**What's free:** ChatGPT free tier with usage limits.

**Limitations:** No built-in transcription. You have to get the transcript yourself (from Zoom, Teams, etc.), paste it in, and write your own prompt.
