# How to Use AI for Contract Management: A Practical Guide

Contract management is one of those functions that everyone acknowledges is important but few organizations do well. Contracts sit in email threads, shared drives, and legal software — often reviewed once at signing and then ignored until a dispute arises.

AI is changing the economics of contract management by making systematic review practical even for smaller teams.

## What AI Contract Management Tools Actually Do

Modern AI contract tools do three things well:

1. **Extract key terms** — parties, dates, payment terms, renewal clauses, liability limits, termination rights
2. **Flag risk language** — indemnification overreach, missing limitation of liability clauses, unusual IP ownership terms
3. **Compare to standards** — identify where a contract deviates from your organization's standard terms

The best tools combine extraction with structured output, so you can query and report across your contract portfolio rather than reading PDFs one at a time.

## A Practical Workflow

### Step 1: Intake and Initial Review

When a new contract arrives — whether inbound from a vendor or outbound from a customer — run it through an AI contract analyzer before it goes to legal. This:

- Extracts all key dates and financial terms
- Flags unusual or high-risk clauses
- Generates a structured summary your team can act on immediately

This doesn't replace legal review for high-stakes agreements. It makes legal's time more efficient by surfacing only the clauses worth discussing.

### Step 2: Obligation Tracking

Contracts create obligations that have to be monitored: renewal notice windows, payment milestones, reporting requirements, audit rights. AI extraction creates a structured database of these obligations, which can feed calendar reminders and workflow tools.

The most common contract management failure isn't a bad deal — it's a missed renewal notice that automatically extends unfavorable terms for another year.

### Step 3: Portfolio Analysis

Once contracts are stored as structured data rather than PDFs, you can answer questions like:
- Which vendor contracts auto-renew in the next 90 days?
- Which customer agreements have price escalation clauses we haven't invoiced?
- Where are we carrying uncapped liability?

This level of visibility used to require a dedicated contracts team. AI tools make it tractable for a single operations or legal coordinator.

## What AI Can't Do

AI contract management tools are powerful, but they're not legal counsel. They:

- **Cannot give legal advice** — only humans with legal training can interpret contract terms in the context of your specific situation
- **May miss context** — industry-specific norms and jurisdiction-specific interpretations require human judgment
- **Should not be used for high-stakes negotiations** without attorney review

Use AI to do the systematic work — extraction, flagging, tracking — so your legal resources can focus on the judgment-intensive decisions.
