# How to Automate Close Technology Stack Evaluation with AI

Evaluating your financial close technology stack is one of those tasks that never gets done well. It's too time-consuming to do thoroughly, too political to do objectively, and too technical for most finance teams to tackle alone. The result: most organizations stick with what they have long past the point where it's costing them.

AI changes the economics of this problem. What used to require a multi-month consulting engagement can now be done in days — with better data and less bias.

## Why Technology Stack Evaluation Gets Stuck

Most close technology evaluations stall for predictable reasons:

**Too much data to process.** A comprehensive evaluation means reviewing current contracts, usage logs, support tickets, user feedback, vendor roadmaps, competitive alternatives, and integration dependencies. That's hundreds of documents and data points.

**No clear methodology.** Without a consistent scoring framework, every stakeholder weights criteria differently — and the evaluation devolves into opinion.

**Vendor relationships cloud judgment.** Long-term vendor relationships, implementation sunk costs, and IT preferences all create bias that's hard to acknowledge let alone correct.

**Finance doesn't speak technology.** Most close teams can articulate their pain points but struggle to translate them into technical requirements that IT can evaluate.

AI helps with all four.

## Step 1: Inventory and Document What You Have

Before evaluating alternatives, you need a clear picture of your current stack. AI can help you build this faster than any manual process.

**Use AI to extract and organize contract data.** Upload your current vendor contracts and let the AI pull out key terms: contract value, renewal dates, SLA commitments, integration clauses, and termination conditions.

**Summarize support ticket history.** Export your support tickets for each system over the past 12-24 months and feed them to an AI. Ask it to categorize issues by type, identify recurring problems, and flag patterns that suggest systemic weaknesses.

**Interview notes and user feedback.** AI is excellent at synthesizing qualitative data. Collect feedback from close team members via structured interviews or surveys, then use AI to identify themes, common pain points, and capability gaps.

The output: a clear inventory of what you have, what it costs, how it performs, and where users are frustrated.

## Step 2: Define Your Requirements

AI can help you build better requirements by:

**Translating pain points into functional requirements.** Give the AI your user feedback and support ticket analysis and ask it to generate a draft requirements list.

**Benchmarking against industry standards.** AI models can tell you what close technology typically supports at your company size and industry, helping you distinguish table-stakes from nice-to-haves.

**Prioritizing requirements by business impact.** Use AI to model the business impact of addressing each requirement — faster close cycle, reduced manual effort, lower error rates.

A good requirements document should have 20-40 requirements grouped by category, each with a priority and acceptance criteria.

## Step 3: Score Your Current Stack

**Extract feature lists from vendor documentation.** AI can read all vendor documentation and generate a feature matrix against your requirements much faster than your team can.

**Identify gaps.** Ask the AI to flag requirements your current tools don't meet, partially meet, or meet only with significant workarounds.

**Score current vendor performance.** Combine the feature matrix with your support ticket analysis and user feedback for a composite score.

## Step 4: Evaluate Alternatives

**Automate RFI/RFP response analysis.** AI can read all vendor materials and score each vendor against your requirements — giving you a comparable, objective matrix.

**Benchmark pricing.** AI can help you model total cost of ownership for each alternative: license fees, implementation costs, training, integration work, and ongoing support.

## Step 5: Model the Business Case

**Quantify current costs.** How many hours does your team spend on tasks a better tool would automate? AI can help estimate these from your process data.

**Project benefits by scenario.** Build models for what different improvement levels mean — AI can stress-test assumptions against industry benchmarks.

**Build the presentation.** AI can help structure the business case narrative and generate executive summaries.

## A Realistic Timeline

A manual close technology evaluation takes 3-6 months. With AI support:

- **Week 1:** Inventory current stack, collect user feedback, extract contract data
- **Week 2:** Build requirements list, score current stack, identify gaps
- **Week 3:** Evaluate alternatives, score vendors
- **Week 4:** Build business case, finalize recommendation

Four weeks from start to recommendation, with more thorough analysis than most manual evaluations produce.

## Getting Started

The biggest barrier isn't technology — it's getting the right data into the AI. Start by collecting:

1. Current vendor contracts
2. Support ticket exports (last 12-24 months)
3. User feedback (structured survey or interview notes)
4. Current pricing and renewal dates

With that foundation, you can move through the rest of the evaluation in days rather than months.
