Most collections workflows still run on fixed playbooks.

Every account gets similar outreach, regardless of payment behavior, dispute status, or response history.

AI can recommend the next best action for each account so collectors spend time on the move most likely to get paid.

## Why fixed collections playbooks plateau

Static cadences are easy to run but inefficient at scale.

Two accounts in the same aging bucket may need completely different actions:

- One needs a payment-plan offer
- One needs dispute escalation
- One needs executive outreach
- One just needs a reminder at a better time

When teams apply one-size-fits-all actions, touches increase while recovery rates stall.

## What “next best action” should optimize for

A useful model should optimize for both cash outcome and operational efficiency:

1. **Payment probability:** likelihood an action results in payment within target window
2. **Cycle-time impact:** expected reduction in days-to-resolution
3. **Cost-to-collect:** effort required for the recommended action
4. **Relationship risk:** chance of creating avoidable customer friction

The recommendation should include confidence and reason codes so collectors understand why it was chosen.

## Examples of AI-driven action recommendations

For each open account, AI can suggest practical, ranked options such as:

- Send reminder with invoice-level detail
- Offer structured payment-plan template
- Route to dispute resolution queue
- Escalate to account owner or manager
- Trigger credit-hold warning before fulfillment

This gives collectors a prioritized action queue instead of a generic list.

## How to deploy without disrupting operations

Start with “assist mode” before full automation:

- Show recommendations inside current collector workflow
- Let collectors accept, edit, or reject suggestions
- Capture outcomes from each action decision
- Use that feedback to retrain recommendation quality

Once recommendation quality is stable, automate low-risk actions and keep high-impact cases human-reviewed.

## Guardrails that keep recommendations trustworthy

To avoid blind automation, enforce clear controls:

- Confidence threshold before auto-executing actions
- Human approval for escalations and legal-sensitive moves
- Suppression rules for accounts in active dispute or litigation
- Full audit trail of recommendations, overrides, and outcomes

These controls preserve compliance while still improving speed.

## Metrics to track after rollout

Measure whether next-best-action logic is actually creating value:

- Cash collected per collector hour
- Promise-to-pay kept rate
- Average touches per resolved account
- DSO and aging-bucket migration
- Override rate on AI recommendations

A falling override rate with improving recovery is a strong sign the model is getting better.

## Final takeaway

Collections teams do not need more reminders; they need better decisions.

AI-based next-best-action workflows help collectors focus on the right action for the right account at the right time, improving recovery without increasing headcount.
