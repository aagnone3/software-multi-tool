Reconciliation teams usually know which accounts are risky. The challenge is proving it fast enough to act before close pressure spikes.

AI can run a consistent aging analysis across every reconciliation, flagging stale items and weak owner commentary before they become late-cycle escalations.

## Why reconciliation aging analysis breaks down

Aging reviews often fail for predictable reasons:

1. **Exception notes are inconsistent.** Different preparers describe the same issue in completely different ways.
2. **Ownership is unclear.** Open items get rolled forward with no explicit next action or due date.
3. **Risk is buried in volume.** Teams review a long queue manually and miss the oldest/highest-impact items.

When this happens, controllers discover concentration risk too late.

## What AI should do in an aging workflow

The objective is better prioritization and cleaner reviewer context — not replacing accounting decisions.

### 1) Normalize exception metadata

AI can standardize open-item records into consistent fields:

- age bucket (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+)
- account + entity owner
- issue category (timing, classification, support gap, dispute)
- expected resolution date

That alone makes trend analysis significantly easier.

### 2) Score stale-item risk

Use risk factors such as:

- item age and repeated carry-forward count
- dollar magnitude relative to account materiality
- proximity to filing/close deadlines
- missing or low-quality remediation notes

The result should be a ranked queue, not a flat list.

### 3) Draft escalation summaries

For high-risk aged items, AI can generate a concise escalation draft:

- what is unresolved
- why it is high risk now
- what evidence is missing
- who needs to act next and by when

Reviewers edit and approve the summary, but the first draft is already structured.

## Practical implementation pattern

A rollout that works in production:

1. Pull open reconciliation exceptions from your close platform.
2. Enrich with amount, owner, prior-period history, and due dates.
3. Run AI normalization + risk scoring daily during close.
4. Route top-tier exceptions to controller review queues.
5. Log reviewer disposition and feed outcomes back into scoring.

Keep humans as final approvers for all escalations and signoffs.

## Controls that keep audit confidence high

Use explicit guardrails:

- mandatory human approval for material exceptions
- immutable audit trail for AI output and reviewer edits
- threshold policies by account class/entity
- forced escalation for 90+ day unresolved items

This preserves accountability while speeding triage.

## Metrics to track in the first 2 close cycles

Measure operational impact directly:

- count of 61+ day and 90+ day open exceptions
- average days unresolved by account category
- % of aged items with complete owner/action/due-date metadata
- late-cycle escalations per close

If these improve, the system is doing its job.

## Where this fits in close automation

Reconciliation aging analysis works best alongside flux analysis, certification workflows, and close risk scoring.

AI handles first-pass structuring and prioritization. Finance leadership still owns judgment, materiality calls, and final signoff.
