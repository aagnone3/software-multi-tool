Paralegals are increasingly being asked to do more with less. AI tools can now handle significant portions of the routine document review, summarization, and intake work that used to consume entire afternoons. Here's a practical breakdown of what's worth using in 2025.

## Contract and Document Review

The most time-consuming paralegal task is reading through long documents — contracts, leases, NDAs, settlement agreements — to extract key information.

**AI contract analysis tools** can:
- Extract parties, dates, obligations, and key clauses in seconds
- Flag unusual terms or missing standard provisions
- Generate structured summaries for attorney review

For a paralegal handling a stack of 20 NDAs before a deal closes, this can reduce review time from several hours to under 30 minutes.

## Deposition and Meeting Summaries

Depositions, client intake calls, and witness interviews generate long transcripts. AI meeting summarization tools can:
- Identify speakers and attribute statements
- Extract key facts, timelines, and admissions
- Flag inconsistencies across multiple sessions

**Practical use case:** Upload a deposition recording and get a structured summary with timestamps — ready for attorney review in minutes.

## Legal Research Support

AI tools can accelerate the initial stages of legal research by:
- Summarizing case law and statutes
- Answering factual questions about established legal standards
- Generating research outlines for attorney review

Note: AI research tools should be used to accelerate and organize — attorney review and verification remains essential.

## Document Drafting Assistance

AI writing assistants can draft routine correspondence, client updates, and form motions based on templates and prior examples. This works best for:
- Standard cover letters and transmittals
- Routine status update emails
- First drafts of form pleadings

Paralegals use these as starting points, not finished products.

## Invoice and Expense Processing

Law firms generate significant document volume around billing — client expense reports, vendor invoices, and disbursement records. AI invoice processors automate extraction and categorization.

## What AI Cannot Replace

- Legal judgment and case strategy
- Client relationship and communication
- Courtroom preparation and presence
- Ethical review and attorney supervision requirements

The best use of AI in paralegal work is removing the rote, repetitive document processing so paralegals can focus on the work that requires their legal training and judgment.

## Getting Started

The lowest-risk starting point is document summarization — no privileged information goes anywhere you wouldn't already send it, and the time savings are immediate.

1. Start with a batch of non-privileged documents (vendor contracts, vendor agreements)
2. Use an AI summarizer to extract key terms
3. Review the output for accuracy
4. Gradually expand to more workflows as you gain confidence

Most paralegals find the tools reliable enough for routine work within a few sessions.

## Conclusion

AI tools for paralegal work in 2025 are most valuable for document review, transcript summarization, and routine drafting assistance. They won't replace legal judgment, but they will free up hours every week for the work that matters most.
