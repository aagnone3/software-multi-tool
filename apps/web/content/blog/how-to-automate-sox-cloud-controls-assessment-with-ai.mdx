## The Cloud Controls Assessment Problem

As organizations migrate workloads to AWS, Azure, and GCP, SOX auditors have followed. Cloud environments introduce new control families — infrastructure-as-code configurations, IAM policies, logging and monitoring settings — that don't map cleanly to legacy ITGC frameworks.

The result: assessment teams spend weeks manually pulling screenshots, reviewing configuration exports, and cross-referencing policy documents against control objectives. Cloud environments change constantly, so evidence gathered in week one may be stale by week four.

AI tools can compress that cycle dramatically.

## What Cloud Controls Assessments Actually Require

A typical SOX cloud controls assessment covers:

- **Identity and access management** — who can access production, with what permissions, under what conditions
- **Change management controls** — infrastructure changes follow an approved pipeline with separation of duties
- **Logging and monitoring** — CloudTrail, Azure Monitor, or GCP Audit Logs enabled and retained appropriately
- **Data protection** — encryption at rest and in transit, key management practices
- **Incident response** — detection, alerting, and escalation controls are configured and tested

Each control requires evidence, a control owner attestation, and a risk rating. For organizations running dozens of services across multiple cloud providers, this is a substantial coordination burden.

## Where AI Helps Most

### Evidence Summarization

Raw cloud configuration exports are verbose. A single IAM policy document or Terraform state file can run hundreds of lines. AI can parse these artifacts and produce a plain-language summary of what the configuration does, what risks it introduces, and whether it aligns with the control objective.

**Example prompt pattern:**
> "Summarize this AWS IAM policy. Identify any overly permissive permissions, missing condition keys, or deviations from least-privilege principles relevant to SOX access control requirements."

### Control Gap Identification

Once you have evidence summaries, AI can cross-reference them against your control matrix and flag gaps. Rather than manually comparing each configuration to your control description, you can batch the comparison.

This works well for:
- Identifying missing logging configurations
- Flagging IAM roles with excessive permissions
- Spotting cloud resources without encryption enabled

### Attestation Drafting

Control owners often struggle to write meaningful attestations. AI can draft an initial attestation based on the evidence summary, which the owner then reviews and approves. This cuts attestation cycle time significantly and improves consistency across the control population.

### Remediation Tracking Summaries

When controls fail, remediation tracking becomes a coordination problem. AI can generate structured remediation status reports from issue tracker data, JIRA tickets, or email threads — consolidating the narrative without manual re-aggregation.

## A Practical Workflow

**Week 1 — Evidence collection:**
Pull cloud configuration exports via CLI or API. Upload to your document management system. Use AI to generate initial summaries for each control artifact.

**Week 2 — Control mapping:**
Use AI to cross-reference evidence summaries against your control matrix. Flag missing evidence and generate gap reports for control owners.

**Week 3 — Owner review:**
Send AI-generated attestation drafts to control owners for review and sign-off. Track responses in your issue management system.

**Week 4 — Remediation and reporting:**
For failed controls, generate remediation action items and assign owners. Use AI to produce the executive summary of assessment results.

## What AI Can't Do

Cloud controls assessment still requires human judgment at key decision points:

- **Risk materiality assessment** — whether a gap is material to financial reporting requires context AI doesn't have
- **Compensating control evaluation** — whether a compensating control adequately mitigates a deficiency requires professional judgment
- **Final sign-off** — management and auditor sign-off cannot be delegated to AI

The goal is to eliminate the low-judgment, high-volume work so your team focuses time on decisions that matter.

## Getting Started

Start with one cloud service and one control family. Pull the configuration exports, run them through an AI summarization tool, and compare the output to your existing control documentation. You'll quickly identify where AI adds the most value for your specific environment.

The teams that benefit most treat AI as a first-pass reviewer — one that reads every configuration, every policy, and every log file — so the human reviewer can focus on exceptions rather than routine confirmation.
