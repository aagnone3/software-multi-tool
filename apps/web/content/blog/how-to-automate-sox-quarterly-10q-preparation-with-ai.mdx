Quarterly 10-Q filings are a recurring pressure point for finance and accounting teams. The same rigor required for the annual 10-K must be applied under compressed timelines — often with fewer resources available due to competing close priorities.

AI tools are changing that equation. Teams using AI for 10-Q preparation are cutting review cycles, reducing manual tie-out work, and catching disclosure gaps earlier in the process.

## What Makes 10-Q Preparation Hard

Unlike the annual 10-K, 10-Qs don't require full audits — but they still require:

- **Accurate financial statements** tied to the general ledger
- **MD&A narratives** explaining quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year changes
- **Disclosure checklists** confirming all required items are addressed
- **Management review and sign-off** before filing

Compressing this work into a tight post-close window creates pressure for errors.

## How AI Accelerates 10-Q Preparation

### 1. Automated Tie-Out Verification

AI tools can compare draft 10-Q financial tables against trial balance exports, flagging discrepancies before they reach the review queue. Instead of manually tracing each number, teams get a summary of what matches and what needs investigation.

### 2. Disclosure Checklist Automation

Standard 10-Q disclosure checklists contain dozens of items — segment reporting, related party transactions, subsequent events, accounting policy changes. AI can scan the draft filing and map coverage against the checklist, highlighting gaps.

### 3. MD&A Variance Analysis Support

AI can analyze period-over-period financial movements and draft initial variance commentary for each line item, which management then reviews and refines. This is especially valuable for recurring explanations like FX impact, seasonality, and headcount-driven cost changes.

### 4. Cross-Referencing Exhibits and Footnotes

AI can verify that exhibit references, footnote cross-references, and defined terms are consistent throughout the document, reducing the risk of stale references from prior quarters.

### 5. Prior-Period Comparison

AI can pull the prior quarter's 10-Q, identify structurally similar sections, and flag changes that may require additional disclosure or explanation.

## Implementation Approach

For most teams, the practical starting point is:

1. **Export draft 10-Q** from the drafting tool as a structured document
2. **Run AI tie-out check** against trial balance and supporting schedules
3. **Run AI disclosure checklist** against SEC requirements for the current quarter
4. **Use AI variance commentary** as a first-draft input for MD&A
5. **Route to management review** with AI-generated summary of open items

## Benefits Teams Are Seeing

- **Faster review cycles** — fewer iterations because issues are caught earlier
- **Fewer disclosure misses** — systematic checklist coverage vs. manual memory
- **More consistent MD&A quality** — AI-drafted variance commentary as a starting point
- **Reduced reviewer burden** — management spends time on judgment, not hunting for ties

## What AI Doesn't Replace

AI accelerates preparation and review — it doesn't replace the judgment required for:

- Determining what material changes require disclosure
- Assessing the appropriate accounting treatment for non-routine transactions
- Making final decisions on MD&A tone and emphasis

Those remain human responsibilities.

## Getting Started

If your team is looking to reduce 10-Q cycle time, start with the highest-friction task: disclosure checklist coverage. Running an AI scan against your draft 10-Q before management review typically surfaces 3-5 items that would otherwise require a second round of revisions.

From there, variance commentary automation and tie-out verification can be layered in as the team gets comfortable with AI-assisted review workflows.

The goal isn't to remove human judgment — it's to make sure human judgment is focused on the decisions that actually require it.
