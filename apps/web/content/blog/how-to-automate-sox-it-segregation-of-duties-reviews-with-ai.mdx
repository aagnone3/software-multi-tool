# How to Automate SOX IT Segregation of Duties Reviews with AI

Segregation of duties (SoD) reviews are one of the most labor-intensive components of SOX IT compliance. Auditors require evidence that no single user has conflicting access rights that could enable fraud or error — and in large enterprises with thousands of users and hundreds of systems, manual reviews quickly become unsustainable.

AI tools are changing this equation. By automating access data analysis, conflict detection, and remediation tracking, teams can complete SoD reviews in days instead of weeks.

## What Makes SoD Reviews Difficult

### Scale and Complexity

Enterprise systems often have:
- Thousands of active user accounts across dozens of applications
- Hundreds of defined roles with overlapping permissions
- Frequent user provisioning and deprovisioning events
- Role inheritance chains that obscure actual access

Manually reconciling all of this against a SoD conflict matrix is a substantial project every quarter.

### Keeping Conflict Matrices Current

Business processes change. New systems are added. Roles evolve. A conflict matrix that was accurate last year may have gaps today. Maintaining it manually is a continuous burden.

### Evidence Documentation

Auditors need to see not just what access exists, but who reviewed it, what exceptions were approved, and what compensating controls are in place for accepted conflicts.

## How AI Automates SoD Reviews

### 1. Access Data Aggregation

AI tools can ingest access exports from ERP systems, cloud platforms, and identity providers into a unified view. Instead of manually joining spreadsheets, the system builds a complete picture of who can do what.

*Example prompt:*
> "Analyze these access exports from SAP, Workday, and AWS. Identify all users with roles that include both payment initiation and payment approval permissions."

### 2. Conflict Detection

With a defined SoD conflict matrix, AI can scan all user access and flag conflicts automatically — including indirect conflicts through role inheritance.

*Example prompt:*
> "Using the attached SoD conflict matrix, identify all users with conflicting access in the attached user-role assignment file. Group results by conflict type and severity."

### 3. Exception Documentation

For accepted exceptions, AI can generate standardized documentation capturing the business justification, approver, compensating controls, and review date.

*Example prompt:*
> "Draft an exception documentation memo for the following SoD conflict: [user], [conflict type], [business justification]. Format it for inclusion in our SOX audit package."

### 4. Remediation Tracking

AI can help track open conflicts through remediation, sending reminders and updating status based on HR and provisioning system inputs.

## Reducing the Review Cycle

Here's how AI compresses a typical SoD review:

| Step | Manual | With AI |
|------|--------|---------|
| Access data aggregation | 3-5 days | 2-4 hours |
| Conflict detection | 5-7 days | 1-2 hours |
| Business owner review | 5-10 days | 3-5 days |
| Exception documentation | 2-3 days | 4-8 hours |
| **Total** | **3-4 weeks** | **5-7 days** |

The biggest gains come from eliminating the manual join and analysis work — the parts where spreadsheet errors tend to accumulate.

## Building Your AI-Assisted SoD Workflow

### Step 1: Standardize Your Data Exports

Before AI can help, you need consistent access data formats. Work with your IAM and ERP administrators to define standard exports that include user ID, role assignments, and effective dates.

### Step 2: Digitize Your Conflict Matrix

If your SoD conflict matrix lives in a spreadsheet, convert it to a structured format AI tools can consume. Define conflict severity levels (critical, high, medium) to prioritize remediation.

### Step 3: Run AI Conflict Analysis

Feed access exports and the conflict matrix to your AI tool. Review the flagged conflicts with business process owners to confirm validity and collect business justifications for exceptions.

### Step 4: Generate Documentation

Use AI to draft exception memos, compensating control descriptions, and remediation plans. Human reviewers validate and approve — the AI handles the writing and formatting.

### Step 5: Track Through Close

Maintain an open items log for unresolved conflicts. Use AI to generate status updates and escalate items approaching audit deadlines.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

**Trusting access data without validation:** AI analysis is only as good as the source data. Validate exports against your identity governance system before running analysis.

**Ignoring compensating controls:** Not all SoD conflicts are equal. AI can help document compensating controls that reduce the risk of accepted exceptions.

**Skipping the human review step:** AI identifies conflicts efficiently, but business context for exceptions requires human judgment. Build review time into your timeline.

**Inconsistent conflict matrix definitions:** Ambiguous conflict definitions lead to inconsistent results. Invest time upfront in clear, precise conflict definitions.

## Connecting to Your Broader SOX Program

SoD reviews don't exist in isolation. Integrate your AI-assisted SoD process with:

- **User access reviews:** SoD conflicts often surface during periodic access reviews
- **Change management controls:** New role assignments should trigger SoD checks
- **Audit trail documentation:** Maintain evidence of each review cycle for auditors

## Getting Started

If you're managing SoD reviews manually today, start with a single high-risk system — typically your ERP or financial reporting platform. Run a pilot AI-assisted review to benchmark time savings and identify gaps in your process before scaling to the full enterprise.

The goal isn't to eliminate human judgment from SoD reviews — it's to focus that judgment on the decisions that matter, rather than the data wrangling that precedes them.
