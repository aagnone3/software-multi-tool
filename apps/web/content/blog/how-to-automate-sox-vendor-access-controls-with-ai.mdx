# How to Automate SOX Vendor Access Controls with AI

Vendor access controls are a critical — and frequently painful — component of SOX IT general controls (ITGCs). Auditors want evidence that third-party vendor access is properly provisioned, reviewed, and revoked. In practice, this means pulling access logs, reconciling against approved vendor lists, and documenting reviews that happen quarterly or annually.

Most teams do this manually. It takes days and produces inconsistent documentation.

AI can change that.

## Why Vendor Access Controls Are Hard

The challenge isn't just volume — it's fragmentation. Vendor access exists across multiple systems: cloud infrastructure, ERP platforms, VPN credentials, service accounts, and developer toolchains. Each system has its own export format and review process.

Common problems:
- **Stale access**: Vendors retain access after project completion
- **No audit trail**: Reviews happen in email threads, not ticketing systems
- **Inconsistent scope**: Different reviewers apply different standards
- **Manual reconciliation**: Matching vendor contracts to active accounts takes hours

## Where AI Helps Most

### 1. Access Entitlement Analysis

AI can ingest access reports from multiple systems and cross-reference them against your approved vendor list. Instead of manual lookups, you get a structured gap analysis: which vendors have access that's not in the approved list, which approved vendors have no active accounts, and which accounts haven't been used in 90+ days.

**What to feed the AI:**
- Exported user/service account lists from each system
- Current vendor contract list with access scope
- Last access timestamps

**What you get back:**
- Flagged anomalies requiring review
- Suggested remediation actions
- Pre-drafted evidence memo

### 2. Quarterly Review Automation

AI can generate the review packet for each vendor automatically: what systems they have access to, what roles/permissions, last login dates, and whether the access aligns with their current contract scope.

Reviewers go from building spreadsheets to just approving or flagging items.

### 3. Termination Verification

When a vendor contract ends, AI can check whether access has been revoked across all systems — and flag any gaps within hours rather than weeks.

### 4. Audit Evidence Documentation

AI can synthesize the review outcomes into a formatted evidence document suitable for auditor submission: review date, reviewer name, population reviewed, exceptions identified, and remediation status.

## Sample Prompt for Access Gap Analysis

```
I have two lists:
1. Active vendor accounts from our ERP system (attached as CSV)
2. Approved vendor list with contract end dates (attached)

Please:
- Identify accounts where the vendor contract has expired
- Flag accounts with no login in the past 90 days
- Note any accounts not on the approved vendor list
- Format findings as a table with recommended action for each
```

## Implementation Tips

**Start with one system**: Don't try to automate everything at once. Pick your highest-risk system (usually ERP or cloud infrastructure) and build the workflow there first.

**Document the AI's role**: Auditors need to understand how the review was conducted. Make clear in your evidence that AI was used for analysis, with human sign-off on the conclusions.

**Keep the human in the loop**: AI handles the analysis; a qualified reviewer signs off on the findings. This satisfies auditor requirements for management review.

**Version your prompts**: Treat prompts like code — version them, test them, and review them when your vendor list or access policies change.

## Integration with Your SOX Program

Vendor access control automation fits naturally into your broader ITGC testing program. Once you've automated the access review process, you can:

- Run reviews on-demand rather than just quarterly
- Reduce the sample size auditors need to test (consistent, documented reviews reduce audit risk)
- Build continuous monitoring into your control environment

## The Bottom Line

SOX vendor access controls don't have to be a manual slog. AI can handle the data reconciliation and documentation work that takes your team days — leaving human reviewers to focus on judgment calls rather than spreadsheet gymnastics.

The tools exist now. The question is whether you build the workflow before your next audit or after it.
