# How to Automate ESG Physical Risk Assessment with AI

Physical climate risk — floods, wildfires, heat stress, sea-level rise — is now a mainstream concern for boards, lenders, and regulators. The TCFD framework and emerging SEC climate rules expect companies to quantify and disclose these exposures. Doing that manually across dozens of facilities and a global supply chain is slow, expensive, and inconsistent. AI changes the equation.

## What Is Physical Risk Assessment in ESG Context?

Physical risk assessment maps a company's assets and operations against climate hazard data to estimate:

- **Acute risks**: Extreme weather events (hurricanes, flooding, wildfires)
- **Chronic risks**: Long-term shifts (temperature rise, precipitation changes, sea-level rise)
- **Transition risks**: Stranded assets if carbon regulations tighten

TCFD recommends scenario analysis under 1.5°C, 2°C, and 4°C warming pathways.

## Why Manual Assessment Fails at Scale

Traditional physical risk workflows involve:

1. Downloading climate datasets (IPCC scenarios, flood maps, heat indexes)
2. Geocoding every facility address
3. Cross-referencing coordinates against hazard layers in GIS tools
4. Scoring exposure by asset type and revenue concentration
5. Writing scenario narratives for the annual report

For a company with 50+ sites and a multi-tier supply chain, this takes months and usually produces stale results by the time it's published.

## How AI Accelerates Physical Risk Assessment

### 1. Automated Facility Geocoding and Hazard Scoring

AI can batch-process facility lists, geocode addresses, and cross-reference against climate hazard APIs (e.g., Jupiter Intelligence, Four Twenty Seven, ClimateAI) to produce risk scores in minutes rather than weeks.

### 2. Scenario Narrative Generation

Large language models can draft TCFD-compliant scenario narratives based on quantitative hazard scores, asset value data, and sector-specific exposure benchmarks — giving your sustainability team a polished first draft rather than a blank page.

### 3. Supply Chain Exposure Mapping

AI can analyze supplier locations and procurement volumes to estimate portfolio-weighted physical risk exposure, flagging concentration in high-hazard geographies.

### 4. Materiality Triage

Not every facility carries equal financial significance. AI can rank exposures by revenue contribution, replacement cost, and operational criticality — letting risk teams focus on what matters most.

### 5. Document Summarization for Existing Reports

If your company has prior risk assessments, AI can extract key findings, compare year-over-year changes, and identify gaps against current TCFD or SEC disclosure expectations.

## Sample AI Workflow for Physical Risk Assessment

```
Input: Facility list (name, address, asset type, annual revenue)
       Preferred climate scenario (1.5°C / 2°C / 4°C)
       Disclosure framework (TCFD / SEC / CSRD)

Step 1: Geocode all facilities → lat/lon coordinates
Step 2: Query climate hazard API → flood, heat, wildfire, hurricane scores per site
Step 3: Weight scores by asset revenue contribution
Step 4: Rank top 10 material exposures
Step 5: Generate TCFD scenario narrative draft
Step 6: Flag supply chain concentration risks

Output: Risk register, board summary, disclosure-ready narrative
```

## What AI Can and Can't Do

**AI can:**
- Process large datasets quickly and consistently
- Generate structured disclosure language
- Identify patterns across hundreds of facilities
- Flag missing data and coverage gaps

**AI cannot:**
- Replace physical site assessments for highly material assets
- Guarantee accuracy of third-party climate hazard data
- Make judgment calls on risk tolerance or capital allocation

## Getting Started

1. **Consolidate your facility data** — name, address, asset type, estimated replacement value
2. **Select a climate data provider** — Jupiter, Moody's ESG Solutions, MSCI Climate, or open-source IPCC datasets
3. **Define your materiality threshold** — which sites are financially significant enough to assess in depth
4. **Choose your disclosure framework** — TCFD, SEC climate rule, CSRD, or internal board reporting
5. **Run a pilot** — start with your top 10 sites to validate the workflow before scaling

## Bottom Line

Physical risk assessment doesn't have to be a once-per-year manual exercise. AI enables continuous monitoring, faster scenario updates, and disclosure-ready outputs that keep pace with evolving regulatory expectations. Companies that automate this process now will have a material advantage when mandatory climate disclosure requirements kick in.
