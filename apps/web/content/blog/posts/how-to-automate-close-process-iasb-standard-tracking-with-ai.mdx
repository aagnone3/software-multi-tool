# How to Automate IASB Standard Tracking in the Close Process with AI

The International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) regularly issues new standards, amendments, and interpretations that affect how finance teams conduct their financial close. Tracking these changes, assessing their impact, and updating close procedures accordingly is time-consuming work that often falls through the cracks until audit season.

AI tools are changing that by automating the monitoring, analysis, and documentation work that keeps finance teams current with IASB pronouncements.

## The Challenge of Manual IASB Standard Tracking

Finance teams trying to track IASB standards manually face several problems:

- **Volume**: IASB issues standards, exposure drafts, amendments, and IFRIC interpretations continuously
- **Impact assessment complexity**: Determining how a new standard affects your specific close procedures requires deep contextual knowledge
- **Documentation burden**: Every standard change requires updating accounting policies, close checklists, and training materials
- **Cross-functional coordination**: Implementing new standards requires coordination between technical accounting, FP&A, IT, and external auditors
- **Deadline pressure**: IASB effective dates create hard deadlines that are easy to miss in the daily close rush

The result is that many finance teams learn about standard changes reactively — from auditors or peer benchmarking — rather than proactively.

## How AI Automates IASB Standard Tracking

### Continuous Monitoring and Alerting

AI tools can monitor IASB publications and related commentary continuously, surfacing relevant updates to finance teams as they occur. Rather than manually checking the IASB website or relying on newsletter subscriptions, your team gets filtered, relevant alerts.

A typical AI-powered monitoring workflow:

1. **Source monitoring**: Tracks IASB website, IFRS Foundation publications, Big 4 technical accounting updates, and regulatory commentary
2. **Relevance filtering**: Applies rules based on your industry, entity type, and current accounting policies
3. **Summary generation**: Creates plain-language summaries of new standards and amendments
4. **Impact tagging**: Flags standards that may affect specific close areas (revenue recognition, leases, financial instruments, etc.)

### Impact Assessment Documentation

When a new standard is identified as relevant, AI can help with the initial impact assessment:

- **Gap analysis**: Comparing new requirements against current accounting policies and close procedures
- **Affected process identification**: Identifying which close steps, journal entries, and disclosures need to change
- **System change assessment**: Noting where ERP configuration, chart of accounts, or reporting templates may need updates
- **Timeline planning**: Mapping implementation requirements to IASB effective dates and your close calendar

### Policy and Procedure Updates

One of the most labor-intensive parts of standard adoption is updating documentation. AI can:

- **Draft policy updates**: Generate first drafts of accounting policy revisions based on the new standard
- **Checklist revisions**: Suggest additions or modifications to close checklists
- **Training material updates**: Flag which training materials reference superseded guidance
- **Disclosure template updates**: Identify financial statement disclosure templates that need revision

### Audit Trail Maintenance

AI tools also help maintain the documentation trail that auditors expect:

- **Standard tracking log**: Automated log of IASB standards reviewed, assessed, and adopted or not applicable
- **Impact assessment archive**: Records of impact analyses with rationale for implementation decisions
- **Policy change history**: Version-controlled accounting policy documents with change reasoning
- **Effective date tracking**: Calendar integration for standard adoption deadlines

## Practical Implementation for Finance Teams

### Step 1: Baseline Your Current Standards Landscape

Before deploying AI monitoring, document your current IASB standard adoption status:

- Which IFRS standards you currently apply
- Effective dates for standards you're in the process of implementing
- Any standards for which you've taken early adoption elections
- Known upcoming standards in your industry's impact zone

This baseline lets AI monitoring focus on genuinely new developments rather than relitigating already-assessed standards.

### Step 2: Configure Relevance Filters

Most AI monitoring tools allow configuration based on:

- **Entity type**: Listed vs. non-listed, industry sector
- **Size thresholds**: Standards with simplified approaches for smaller entities
- **Geographic reporting**: Jurisdiction-specific IFRS modifications
- **Existing policy elections**: Standards where you've made accounting policy choices

### Step 3: Integrate with Close Workflow

The real leverage comes from integrating IASB tracking with your close workflow tools. When a relevant standard is identified:

- A task should appear in your close management system for impact assessment
- The relevant technical accounting contact should receive notification
- A deadline should be set based on the standard's effective date
- Progress should be tracked like any other close task

### Step 4: Build Review Cadence

While AI handles continuous monitoring, finance leadership should establish a quarterly review cycle to:

- Review all IASB developments tracked since the last review
- Confirm or update impact assessments
- Check implementation progress for in-flight standard adoptions
- Assess disclosure requirements for upcoming period-end filings

## Example: Tracking an IFRS Amendment

Here's how a finance team might use AI to track a new IASB amendment to IFRS 9 Financial Instruments:

**AI monitoring** surfaces the amendment with a summary: classification guidance updated for certain financial instruments, effective for annual periods beginning on or after a specified date.

**AI impact assessment** identifies that the company holds instruments of the affected type, flags the revenue recognition and financial instruments close modules as potentially impacted, and notes that existing hedge documentation may need review.

**AI drafts** an impact assessment memo summarizing the amendment, the company's affected positions, required policy changes, and an implementation timeline.

**Close management system** receives an automated task: "Complete IFRS 9 amendment impact assessment by [date]," assigned to the technical accounting manager with the AI-generated draft attached.

**At period-end**, AI checks that the impact assessment is complete, that policy documentation has been updated, and that required disclosures are included in the draft financial statements.

## Benefits for Finance Teams

Finance teams implementing AI-powered IASB tracking report several concrete benefits:

- **Earlier awareness**: Standard changes identified months earlier than before, allowing more implementation lead time
- **Reduced audit surprises**: External auditors find fewer gaps in standard adoption tracking
- **Better cross-functional coordination**: IT and operational teams notified earlier about accounting system changes needed
- **Documentation quality**: More complete, consistent documentation of the standard adoption process
- **Staff efficiency**: Technical accounting staff spend less time on monitoring and more on complex judgment calls

## Conclusion

Keeping current with IASB standards shouldn't require constant manual effort or create audit-season panic. AI tools can handle the continuous monitoring, initial impact assessment, and documentation drafting that keeps your finance team ahead of standard changes — letting your technical accounting expertise focus on the genuinely complex judgments that automation can't replace.

The teams that will adapt most successfully to future IASB changes are those building the AI monitoring infrastructure now, before the next major standard takes effect.
