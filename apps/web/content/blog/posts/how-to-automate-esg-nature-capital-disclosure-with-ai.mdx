## What Is Nature Capital Disclosure?

Nature capital disclosure refers to the process of identifying, measuring, and reporting a company's dependencies on and impacts to natural ecosystems—biodiversity, water, soil, air, and other natural resources that underpin business operations.

Frameworks like the **Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD)** and **GRI 304 (Biodiversity)** are pushing organizations to systematically assess nature-related risks and opportunities alongside traditional climate risk.

For many finance and sustainability teams, this is new territory. The data is dispersed, the methodologies are emerging, and the stakeholder expectations are evolving quickly.

## Why AI Is Well-Suited for Nature Capital Workflows

Nature capital disclosure involves several tasks that AI can meaningfully accelerate:

- **Site-level risk screening** – Identifying which operating locations are in or near sensitive ecosystems using geospatial and business data
- **Dependency mapping** – Linking business activities to ecosystem services (water purification, pollination, carbon sequestration) that the business relies on
- **Supply chain exposure** – Scanning procurement data and supplier profiles for nature-related risks
- **Regulatory change monitoring** – Tracking emerging TNFD, CSRD, and biodiversity-related regulations across jurisdictions
- **Disclosure drafting** – Generating narrative sections aligned to TNFD LEAP process outputs

## The TNFD LEAP Process and Where AI Fits

TNFD's LEAP approach (Locate, Evaluate, Assess, Prepare) provides a structured methodology for nature-related risk assessment:

**Locate** – Identify where your operations and upstream/downstream activities interact with nature. AI can parse business unit data, supplier addresses, and asset lists to map exposure geographically.

**Evaluate** – Determine which ecosystem services your business depends on or impacts. AI can cross-reference activity data with ecosystem service databases to surface material dependencies.

**Assess** – Quantify the materiality of nature-related risks and opportunities. AI can help structure scenario analysis and model financial exposure using existing risk frameworks.

**Prepare** – Draft disclosures and governance documentation. AI can turn assessment outputs into board-ready narrative, aligned to TNFD recommendations and GRI 304 requirements.

## Practical AI Use Cases

### 1. Site-Level Sensitivity Screening

Many organizations have hundreds of operating sites. AI can help triage them by:

- Cross-referencing site addresses with biodiversity hotspot databases (e.g., IBAT, ENCORE)
- Flagging locations in protected areas, key biodiversity areas, or water-stressed regions
- Producing a risk-tiered site inventory for deeper assessment

### 2. Procurement Data Analysis

Nature risks often concentrate in supply chains—especially for companies sourcing agricultural commodities, timber, or minerals. AI can:

- Parse procurement spend data and supplier lists to identify nature-exposed categories
- Flag commodity categories linked to deforestation, land conversion, or water stress
- Summarize supplier sustainability certificates and audit reports

### 3. Policy and Regulatory Monitoring

The nature disclosure regulatory landscape is evolving rapidly. AI can:

- Monitor regulatory updates across TNFD, EU CSRD, UK TCFD/TNFD guidance, and national biodiversity strategies
- Summarize what's changing, which jurisdictions are affected, and what reporting obligations are triggered
- Flag gaps between current disclosure practices and emerging requirements

### 4. Disclosure Narrative Drafting

Once assessments are complete, AI can help prepare:

- TNFD-aligned governance and strategy disclosures
- GRI 304 biodiversity impact summaries
- Executive summaries for annual report integration
- Stakeholder Q&A briefings on nature-related risk

## Common Challenges (and How AI Helps)

**Data fragmentation** – Nature capital data sits in procurement systems, ERP, environmental permits, and third-party databases. AI helps consolidate and normalize this data for assessment use.

**Methodology uncertainty** – TNFD is still evolving. AI can help teams stay current with framework updates and benchmark against peer disclosures.

**Cross-functional coordination** – Nature disclosure requires input from procurement, facilities, finance, legal, and sustainability. AI can support structured data collection workflows and keep assessments moving forward.

**Materiality determination** – Deciding which nature impacts are financially material is judgment-intensive. AI can help model scenarios and structure the evidence base for materiality calls.

## Getting Started

If your organization is beginning a TNFD or GRI 304 disclosure process, here's a practical starting point:

1. **Run a site-level screen** – Use AI to map your operating footprint against known biodiversity sensitivity databases
2. **Identify your top 3 nature-exposed spend categories** – Start with procurement and flag the highest-risk commodity exposure
3. **Assign a pilot disclosure scope** – Pick one reporting entity or product line to pilot the full LEAP process
4. **Draft the governance section first** – AI can help structure your board oversight and management accountability narrative early, before full quantitative data is ready

Nature capital disclosure is complex, but it rewards early movers. Starting with AI-assisted screening and drafting keeps the process manageable while building the internal capability you'll need as requirements mature.
