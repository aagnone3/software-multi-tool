Entity-level controls (ELCs) sit at the top of every SOX compliance framework. They govern the tone at the top, risk assessment processes, and the overall control environment — and auditors scrutinize them closely each year.

But for compliance teams, ELC documentation is often one of the most time-consuming and least systematized parts of the SOX cycle. Policies live in SharePoint. Evidence is scattered across email threads. Control owners change and documentation doesn't get updated.

AI can bring order to this chaos — without requiring a compliance overhaul.

## What Are Entity-Level Controls?

Entity-level controls are controls that operate at the organization level rather than the process or transaction level. They include:

- **Tone at the top**: Board and senior management commitment to ethics and compliance
- **Risk assessment**: How the organization identifies and responds to financial reporting risks
- **Control environment**: HR policies, code of conduct, organizational structure
- **Monitoring activities**: Internal audit, management reviews, hotline programs
- **Information and communication**: Financial reporting policies, whistleblower protections

Under the COSO framework, ELCs directly influence the effectiveness of all other controls. Auditors use them to calibrate how much reliance they place on process-level controls.

## Why ELC Documentation Is Hard to Automate

Traditional automation tools struggle with ELCs because:

1. **The evidence is unstructured** — board minutes, policy documents, org charts, meeting notes
2. **Ownership is diffuse** — CFO, General Counsel, HR, Internal Audit all own pieces
3. **The testing is qualitative** — you're not reconciling numbers, you're evaluating whether a policy actually gets followed
4. **Requirements change** — PCAOB guidance, external auditor preferences, and internal standards shift year to year

AI handles unstructured, qualitative content well — which makes it a natural fit for ELC work.

## How AI Helps With Entity-Level Controls

### 1. Policy Summarization and Gap Analysis

Compliance teams typically maintain dozens of policies that support ELCs: code of conduct, anti-fraud, whistleblower, related-party transactions, and more.

AI can:
- Summarize policy documents into plain-language control descriptions
- Flag gaps between current policy language and what auditors expect to see
- Identify policies that haven't been updated in more than 12 months
- Cross-reference policy language against COSO requirements or internal control frameworks

**Example prompt for a document analysis tool:**
> "Summarize the key commitments in this Code of Conduct and identify any gaps relative to typical SOX ELC requirements for tone at the top."

### 2. Control Narrative Generation

Writing control narratives for ELCs used to mean staring at a blank document and hoping the right words came. AI can draft narratives based on:

- Policy documents you upload
- Prior-year control descriptions
- Interview notes with control owners
- Internal audit reports

The result isn't final — but it's a 70% draft that cuts writing time significantly.

### 3. Evidence Collection Tracking

One of the most painful parts of ELC testing is chasing down evidence: board meeting attendance records, risk committee agendas, ethics hotline reports, training completion rates.

AI can:
- Build and maintain an evidence tracker from a list of required items
- Flag overdue evidence requests
- Summarize responses when evidence arrives in email form
- Generate status reports for the audit committee

### 4. Walkthroughs and Interview Summaries

Auditors typically require walkthroughs for ELCs — conversations with the CFO, General Counsel, or board members to confirm controls are operating as designed.

AI can:
- Transcribe and summarize walkthrough conversations (see: meeting summarizer tools)
- Extract control-relevant quotes and evidence points
- Generate draft walkthrough memos ready for auditor review
- Flag inconsistencies between what was said and what policy documents state

### 5. Annual Assessment Packages

At year-end, compliance teams must produce ELC assessment packages for management sign-off and external auditors. These typically include:

- Control descriptions for each ELC
- Evidence of operating effectiveness
- Management's assessment of design and operation
- Identified deficiencies and remediation plans

AI can assemble these packages from individual components — policies, evidence files, testing results, walkthrough summaries — reducing package assembly from days to hours.

## Practical Workflow: AI-Assisted ELC Documentation

Here's a streamlined ELC documentation workflow using AI tools:

**Step 1: Inventory current ELCs**
Upload prior-year ELC documentation and ask AI to extract a structured inventory of controls, owners, and evidence requirements.

**Step 2: Update for current year**
Ask AI to flag controls where ownership has changed, policies have been updated, or evidence requirements have shifted based on auditor feedback.

**Step 3: Collect evidence**
Use an AI-assisted tracker to send evidence requests to control owners and summarize responses as they arrive.

**Step 4: Conduct and document walkthroughs**
Transcribe walkthrough conversations and generate walkthrough memos automatically.

**Step 5: Assemble assessment package**
Ask AI to compile control descriptions, evidence, walkthrough summaries, and any deficiency language into a structured assessment document.

**Step 6: Review and finalize**
Human review remains essential — AI handles the scaffolding, compliance professionals handle the judgment calls.

## What AI Can't Do for ELCs

Be realistic about limitations:

- **AI can't assess tone at the top** — that's a human judgment about whether leadership actually behaves consistently with stated values
- **AI can't replace auditor conversations** — it can prepare you for them, not substitute them
- **AI can generate inaccurate summaries** — always verify AI-generated control descriptions against source documents
- **AI doesn't know your audit history** — context about prior-year findings, auditor preferences, and material weaknesses must be provided explicitly

## Getting Started

The lowest-friction entry point for AI in ELC work is policy summarization. Take your three most complex ELC policies, run them through a document analysis tool, and compare the AI summary to how your team currently describes those controls.

If the AI description is more accurate or complete — that's your signal to expand the workflow.

For teams managing 20+ ELCs across multiple subsidiaries, the time savings compound quickly. The goal isn't to remove compliance judgment — it's to redirect it toward the decisions that actually require it.
