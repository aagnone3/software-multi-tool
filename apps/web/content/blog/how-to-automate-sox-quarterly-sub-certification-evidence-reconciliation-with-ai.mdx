# How to Automate SOX Quarterly Sub-Certification Evidence Reconciliation with AI

Sub-certification cycles are where control quality gets tested in practice. Teams must collect attestations, resolve exceptions, and tie evidence back to certification statements under hard deadlines.

AI can help by standardizing evidence intake, surfacing mismatch risk early, and producing reviewer-ready reconciliation outputs.

## Why Sub-Certification Reconciliation Becomes a Bottleneck

Most teams encounter the same failure points:

- Business-unit attestations arrive in inconsistent formats
- Supporting evidence is incomplete or hard to map to claims
- Exception statuses are updated in multiple disconnected trackers
- Escalation ownership is unclear for unresolved items
- Final reconciliation decks are assembled manually under time pressure

The result is slower close cycles and higher late-stage review risk.

## Where AI Delivers the Most Value

### 1. Normalizing incoming attestations and support

AI can convert varied submissions into a standard structure with owner, control scope, evidence links, and exception flags.

### 2. Matching assertions to evidence automatically

AI can map each sub-certification statement to source artifacts and flag unsupported or stale references.

### 3. Prioritizing unresolved exceptions

AI can rank open issues by severity and filing impact, helping teams focus review time where it matters most.

### 4. Drafting reconciliation summaries for reviewers

AI can produce concise quarter-over-quarter summaries with status changes, key exceptions, and required approvals.

## Practical Rollout Plan

Adopt in controlled phases:

1. **Define a canonical sub-certification schema** for submissions and evidence metadata.
2. **Centralize exception tracking** with clear owner and due-date fields.
3. **Generate AI-assisted reconciliation drafts** with source references and change highlights.
4. **Require human approval checkpoints** before controller and executive review.

## Governance Guardrails

For SOX-critical workflows, keep controls explicit:

- Enforce role-based review and sign-off for high-risk exceptions
- Preserve immutable version history for attestations and reconciliations
- Require evidence links for every material statement
- Log AI-generated vs human-edited sections for traceability
- Trigger escalation workflows for overdue unresolved exceptions

## Metrics to Track

Measure both speed and control quality:

- Time from attestation deadline to first reconciled package
- Percentage of statements with complete evidence linkage
- Open exception count at certification cutoff
- Review rounds required before approval
- Late-cycle exception discoveries after package draft

## Bottom Line

AI can turn SOX quarterly sub-certification reconciliation from a manual scramble into a controlled, repeatable workflow.

With strong templates, evidence discipline, and human sign-off, teams can move faster while improving audit readiness.
