# How to Automate SOX Quarterly Disclosure Committee Reviews with AI

SOX quarterly disclosure committee reviews sit at the intersection of legal, finance, and investor relations—making them one of the most coordination-heavy compliance workflows in any public company. AI can compress the preparation cycle, improve coverage, and give committee members the context they need to make faster, more confident decisions.

## What the Disclosure Committee Process Looks Like Today

For most public companies, the quarterly disclosure committee review follows a predictable but labor-intensive pattern:

1. **Document collection** — Gathering 10-Q/10-K drafts, press releases, earnings scripts, management certifications, and supporting schedules
2. **Cross-reference review** — Verifying consistency across documents (numbers in the earnings release match the 10-Q; footnotes align with the financial statements)
3. **Risk identification** — Flagging new or changed disclosures that could be material
4. **Committee preparation** — Assembling briefing packages, drafting talking points, preparing certifier summaries
5. **Meeting facilitation** — Walking through open items, documenting decisions, tracking follow-ups
6. **Sub-certification collection** — Gathering sign-offs from department heads before CEO/CFO certifications

Each of these steps involves significant manual work—often spread across multiple teams with tight deadlines between earnings close and filing.

## Where AI Adds the Most Value

### 1. Cross-Document Consistency Checking

AI document analysis tools can ingest your draft 10-Q, earnings release, press release, and supplemental schedules simultaneously and flag any numerical or narrative inconsistencies:

- Revenue figures that differ between the earnings release and the 10-Q
- Risk factor language that conflicts with MD&A disclosures
- Non-GAAP reconciliation numbers that don't tie to the audited statements
- Footnote references that don't match the main body of the filing

What used to take a team of reviewers several days can be reduced to a focused review of AI-flagged discrepancies.

### 2. Material Change Detection

AI can compare the current draft against prior quarter filings to surface:

- New risk factors or substantially modified language
- Changes in accounting policies or estimates
- New related-party transactions or off-balance-sheet arrangements
- Shifts in segment definitions or revenue recognition approaches

This gives the committee a targeted list of new disclosures requiring specific attention rather than requiring a full re-read of every section.

### 3. Briefing Package Automation

Instead of manually assembling committee briefing packets, AI can:

- Summarize each disclosure section with key changes highlighted
- Extract open items requiring committee decisions
- Generate a risk-ranked agenda with supporting context for each item
- Draft follow-up questions based on identified inconsistencies

This shifts committee preparation from document assembly to decision-making.

### 4. Sub-Certification Workflow Management

AI can automate the sub-certification tracking process:

- Draft individualized sub-certification templates for each department head based on their areas of responsibility
- Track completion status and send automated reminders
- Flag incomplete or qualified certifications for CFO/general counsel review
- Maintain a timestamped audit trail of all certifications

### 5. Meeting Documentation and Follow-Up Tracking

AI meeting summarization tools can transcribe committee discussions and automatically:

- Extract decisions and open items
- Assign follow-up owners and deadlines
- Generate meeting minutes in the format required for your audit file
- Track resolution of prior quarter open items

## Implementation Approach

### Phase 1: Document Analysis (Weeks 1-2)

Start with cross-document consistency checking. Upload your draft filings to an AI document analysis tool and generate an inconsistency report. This creates immediate value without changing any existing workflows.

Tools like Claude, GPT-4, or specialized financial document analysis platforms can handle this with appropriate prompting.

### Phase 2: Change Detection Automation (Weeks 3-4)

Build a comparison workflow that runs your current draft against the prior quarter filing. Most document analysis tools support structured comparison; the key is establishing a standard format for the output that the committee can review efficiently.

### Phase 3: Briefing Package Generation (Month 2)

Once you have reliable document analysis, use AI to generate the first draft of committee briefing materials. Plan for human review and editing—AI will get you 70-80% of the way there, with your securities counsel handling final language.

### Phase 4: Full Workflow Integration (Month 3+)

Integrate the AI tools into your quarterly close calendar with automated triggers: when the 10-Q draft reaches a certain completion milestone, automatically kick off the AI review process and notify committee members.

## What AI Can't Replace

**Legal judgment** — Materiality assessments and disclosure decisions require experienced securities counsel. AI can surface issues; lawyers decide how to address them.

**Auditor coordination** — Your external auditors need to review specific disclosures. AI can prepare the materials, but the auditor relationship stays human.

**CEO/CFO certification** — The certifiers need genuine understanding of the disclosures, not just AI-generated summaries. Use AI to help them understand quickly, not to substitute for their review.

**Novel situations** — New accounting standards, first-time disclosures of significant events, or unusual transactions require experienced human judgment that AI isn't reliably equipped to provide alone.

## Measuring the Impact

Track these metrics before and after implementing AI disclosure committee support:

- **Preparation cycle time** — Days from 10-Q draft completion to committee review-ready
- **Issues caught pre-committee** — Inconsistencies or gaps identified before (not during) the committee meeting
- **Committee meeting duration** — Time spent in committee review meetings
- **Sub-certification cycle time** — Days from request to completion of all certifications
- **Post-close adjustments** — Corrections or amendments required after filing

Most companies see 30-50% reduction in preparation time within the first two to three quarters of consistent AI-assisted review.

## Getting Started This Quarter

You don't need a full implementation to start getting value. Before your next quarterly disclosure committee review:

1. Run your 10-Q draft through an AI document analysis tool and compare it against your earnings release
2. Have AI generate a list of changed risk factors compared to the prior quarter
3. Use AI to draft the committee agenda based on identified issues

These three steps alone will give your committee better preparation with less manual work—and they can be done with tools you likely already have access to.

The goal isn't to automate the committee's judgment. It's to give the committee better information, faster, so their time is spent on decisions rather than document review.
