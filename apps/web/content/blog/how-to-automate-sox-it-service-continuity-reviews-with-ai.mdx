# How to Automate SOX IT Service Continuity Reviews with AI

SOX compliance requires companies to demonstrate that critical financial systems have robust business continuity and disaster recovery (BCP/DR) plans. IT auditors must review these plans annually, verify RTO/RPO targets are documented, confirm tests were conducted, and assess whether gaps were remediated. It's thorough — and it's slow.

This guide explains how AI tools are helping compliance teams automate the evidence collection, documentation review, and gap analysis work that makes SOX IT service continuity reviews so labor-intensive.

## Why SOX IT Service Continuity Reviews Take So Long

For each in-scope financial system, auditors must typically:

- Locate and review the current BCP/DR plan document
- Verify RTO (Recovery Time Objective) and RPO (Recovery Point Objective) targets are defined
- Confirm a test was conducted within the required period
- Review test results and identify any gaps or failures
- Verify gap remediation was completed and documented
- Assess whether the plan was updated after organizational or system changes

Across dozens of in-scope systems, this becomes a substantial project — especially when documentation is scattered across SharePoint folders, email threads, and ticketing systems.

## Where AI Adds the Most Value

### 1. BCP/DR Document Extraction and Summarization

AI can read through lengthy BCP/DR plan documents and extract:

- Defined RTO and RPO targets per system
- Recovery procedures and step owners
- Dependencies on third-party vendors or infrastructure
- Last review and approval date

This turns a 45-minute document review into a 2-minute extraction.

### 2. Test Evidence Matching

After BCP/DR tests, evidence files (test scripts, screenshots, sign-off emails) are typically stored inconsistently. AI can scan a folder of evidence files and:

- Match each piece of evidence to the system it covers
- Flag systems with missing or outdated test evidence
- Summarize test outcomes (pass/fail, gaps identified)
- Highlight where test results diverged from RTO/RPO targets

### 3. Gap Remediation Tracking

When BCP/DR tests reveal gaps, remediation must be tracked to closure. AI can:

- Parse ticketing system exports or email threads to identify open remediation items
- Cross-reference gaps against completed remediation evidence
- Flag items where remediation is overdue or insufficiently documented
- Draft gap summary tables ready for the audit workpaper

### 4. Plan Currency Verification

Plans that haven't been updated after a major system change, migration, or vendor change represent a compliance risk. AI can:

- Compare the current BCP/DR plan against a changelog or migration documentation
- Identify sections that reference outdated infrastructure or personnel
- Flag systems where the plan's last-modified date predates known changes

### 5. Audit Workpaper Population

Once evidence is collected and reviewed, AI can populate structured workpaper templates with:

- System name and in-scope classification
- Defined RTO/RPO targets
- Test date, method, and outcome
- Gap count and remediation status
- Overall conclusion (effective / ineffective with exceptions)

## Practical Workflow

Here's how a compliance team might use AI for SOX IT service continuity reviews:

1. **Export** the list of in-scope financial systems from the IT asset inventory
2. **Collect** BCP/DR plan documents, test evidence, and gap remediation records into a shared folder
3. **Run AI extraction** to pull RTO/RPO targets and test dates from each plan document
4. **Run evidence matching** to confirm each system has current test evidence on file
5. **Generate gap report** highlighting systems with missing tests, failed tests, or open remediation items
6. **Populate workpapers** with AI-drafted summaries for auditor review
7. **Auditor review** — the auditor focuses on exceptions rather than reading every document

## What AI Can't Do

AI handles evidence processing and summarization well, but auditors still need to:

- Make professional judgments about whether a gap is material
- Assess whether RTO/RPO targets are appropriate for the business risk
- Evaluate whether test scenarios adequately simulate real failure conditions
- Apply experience and judgment when documentation is ambiguous

AI speeds up the evidence-gathering phase significantly, but the auditor remains the decision-maker on sufficiency and conclusions.

## Tools That Help

Several AI tools support this workflow:

- **Document summarizers** — extract key fields from BCP/DR plan PDFs and Word documents
- **Meeting/call transcription tools** — useful when BCP/DR test debrief meetings are recorded and you need to extract action items
- **Contract and document analysis tools** — can compare plan versions or flag outdated references
- **Spreadsheet-linked tools** — populate tracking matrices directly from extracted evidence

For teams already using an AI-powered document analysis platform, extending it to cover BCP/DR document review is often a straightforward configuration.

## Getting Started

If you're running SOX IT service continuity reviews manually today, the quickest win is usually document extraction: train an AI tool to pull RTO/RPO targets and last-test dates from your BCP/DR plan library. Even partial automation of this step can save hours per review cycle.

From there, evidence matching and gap reporting are natural extensions.
