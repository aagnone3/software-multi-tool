# How to Automate SOX Quarterly SEC Form Proxy Statement Preparation with AI

Preparing SEC proxy statements is one of the most labor-intensive tasks in quarterly SOX compliance. Finance and legal teams spend days gathering compensation data, reviewing governance disclosures, and reconciling prior-period language — all under tight regulatory deadlines.

AI tools are changing that. This guide explains how to use AI to automate the most time-consuming parts of proxy statement preparation while maintaining the accuracy and auditability your external auditors require.

## The Problem with Manual Proxy Statement Preparation

Quarterly proxy statement prep typically involves:

- Pulling executive compensation data from HR and payroll systems
- Reconciling beneficial ownership tables with transfer agent records
- Reviewing and updating governance section language from prior filings
- Cross-referencing with 10-K and 10-Q disclosures for consistency
- Routing through multiple review cycles across legal, finance, and IR

Each step is manual, error-prone, and dependent on subject matter experts who are already stretched thin during close.

## What AI Can Automate

### 1. Document Extraction and Summarization

AI can extract relevant data from prior proxy statements, compensation committee minutes, and HR reports:

- Pull executive pay tables from PDF or Excel exports
- Summarize changes in governance policies since the prior year
- Flag new SEC rules or guidance that may require updated disclosures

### 2. Language Consistency Checks

AI can compare the current draft against prior filings:

- Identify inconsistencies in how metrics are described across documents
- Flag sections where language differs from the 10-K or 10-Q
- Suggest boilerplate updates when disclosure requirements change

### 3. Change Tracking and Audit Trail

AI tools can produce a structured change log:

- Highlight every section modified from the prior filing
- Annotate why each change was made (regulatory update, business change, etc.)
- Generate a review-ready summary for legal and audit sign-off

### 4. Compensation Table Automation

AI can parse structured compensation data and populate standard proxy tables:

- Summary Compensation Table
- Grants of Plan-Based Awards
- Outstanding Equity Awards
- Option Exercises and Stock Vested

This eliminates manual data entry and reduces the risk of transposition errors.

## Implementation Approach

**Step 1: Centralize Source Documents**
Gather prior proxy statements, compensation committee minutes, HR compensation exports, and SEC correspondence in a shared repository.

**Step 2: Run AI Extraction**
Use an AI document analysis tool to extract key data points, flag year-over-year changes, and generate a structured summary for review.

**Step 3: Populate Templates**
Feed extracted data into your proxy statement template. Most AI tools can output structured JSON or Excel that maps directly to standard disclosure formats.

**Step 4: AI-Assisted Review**
Run the draft through an AI consistency checker to compare language across the 10-K, 10-Q, and prior proxy. Flag any discrepancies for human review.

**Step 5: Document the Process**
Log every AI-assisted step in your audit trail. Include input documents, extraction outputs, and review sign-offs so auditors can follow the preparation chain.

## What to Keep Human

AI accelerates preparation but does not replace judgment. Keep humans in the loop for:

- Final compensation decisions and narrative framing
- Legal review of new disclosure requirements
- Sign-off from the compensation committee and general counsel
- Filing decisions and SEC correspondence

## Getting Started

If you're still preparing proxy statements entirely by hand, start with a focused pilot:

1. Choose one section — say, the Summary Compensation Table — and run it through an AI extraction tool
2. Compare the AI output against your manually prepared version
3. Measure accuracy and time saved before expanding to other sections

Most teams find they can cut proxy prep time by 40–60% with AI on the extraction and consistency-check steps alone.
