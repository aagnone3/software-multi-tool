# How to Automate SOX Quarterly 10-K Preparation with AI

Annual 10-K preparation is one of the most resource-intensive processes in public company finance. Teams spend weeks coordinating across departments, reviewing disclosure requirements, tying out financial statements, and managing the endless review cycles before filing.

AI tools are changing this—not by replacing human judgment, but by automating the mechanical, repetitive work that consumes most of the time.

## The 10-K Preparation Bottlenecks AI Can Solve

**Disclosure checklist management** is the first major drag. Ensuring every required SEC disclosure is addressed across hundreds of checklist items is tedious, error-prone manual work. AI can compare your draft against current disclosure requirements and flag gaps automatically.

**Financial statement tie-outs** are another major time sink. Cross-referencing numbers across MD&A, financial statements, and footnotes manually takes days. AI tools can scan your draft and flag inconsistencies in seconds.

**Supporting schedule compilation** typically involves gathering evidence from dozens of sources. AI can help organize, categorize, and cross-reference these materials against the disclosures they support.

**Prior year comparison** is essential for consistency but tedious to do manually. AI can flag language changes and numerical differences between current and prior year filings for human review.

## Using AI for Disclosure Requirement Reviews

Upload your current 10-K draft along with the SEC's current disclosure requirements guidance to an AI document analysis tool. Ask it to:

- Identify required disclosures that appear missing or incomplete
- Flag sections where the required elements may not be sufficiently addressed
- Compare your risk factor disclosures against peer company filings for completeness
- Review MD&A for required forward-looking statement cautionary language

This review doesn't replace legal counsel, but it catches obvious gaps before human reviewers spend time on them.

## Financial Statement Consistency Checks

AI document analysis excels at finding numerical inconsistencies across large documents. For 10-K preparation, this means:

**Cross-document tie-outs**: Upload your full 10-K draft and ask the AI to identify any numerical discrepancies between the financial statements, MD&A, footnotes, and selected financial data sections.

**Prior period comparisons**: Compare current period numbers against prior year filings to flag unexplained changes or inconsistencies in presentation.

**Definition consistency**: Check that financial terms and metrics are defined and used consistently throughout the document.

## Supporting Documentation Workflow

Structure your AI-assisted documentation workflow around preparation phases:

1. **Intake phase**: Use AI to categorize incoming support documentation by disclosure item and assess completeness
2. **Review phase**: Ask AI to summarize key assumptions in critical accounting estimates documentation for reviewer efficiency
3. **Finalization phase**: Use AI to verify that all cross-references in the document point to correct exhibit numbers and page references

## Managing the Review Cycle

10-K review cycles involve multiple stakeholders providing conflicting edits. AI can help manage this by:

- Summarizing all open review comments by section and reviewer
- Identifying conflicting instructions from different reviewers for human escalation
- Tracking comment resolution status across versions
- Generating a clean summary of changes between versions for final reviewer efficiency

## Practical Implementation Steps

**Start with the tie-out process.** It's the highest-value, lowest-risk application. Run numerical consistency checks on your current draft and see how many discrepancies you catch compared to your manual process.

**Build a disclosure checklist template.** Work with your legal team to create a machine-readable checklist of required 10-K disclosures. Use this as a structured prompt for AI review of each draft.

**Use AI for first-draft summaries.** For sections like MD&A that require plain-language explanations of financial results, AI can produce a first draft that humans refine—faster than starting from blank page.

**Document your AI review process.** As AI-assisted processes become part of your 10-K workflow, document them for SOX purposes like any other control.

## What AI Won't Replace

Judgment calls in areas like management's assessment of going concern, materiality determinations, and complex accounting estimates still require experienced human reviewers. AI is a tool for eliminating the mechanical work so those humans can focus their judgment where it matters.

Similarly, the legal review of disclosure language for litigation exposure, the CFO/CEO certifications, and the audit committee review process remain fully human activities that AI supports rather than replaces.

## Getting Started

The easiest entry point is running a free-form consistency check on your most recent 10-K. Upload the document to an AI tool and ask: "Identify any numerical discrepancies or inconsistencies in this financial document." The results will give you a concrete sense of what AI can catch—and where human review remains essential.

From there, build out a structured checklist-based disclosure review workflow for your next filing cycle.
