## The Change Management Control Challenge

SOX IT general controls (ITGCs) for change management are among the most documentation-heavy parts of any audit. Every production change needs a trail: ticket, approval, testing evidence, and sign-off. For teams running dozens of changes per month, that's thousands of artifacts to organize, verify, and package for auditors.

AI can't replace the controls themselves — but it can eliminate most of the manual effort in documenting, testing, and collecting evidence for them.

---

## What SOX IT Change Management Controls Cover

A typical SOX change management program requires demonstrating:

- **Formal change requests** — documented and logged before implementation
- **Authorization** — appropriate approvals before promotion to production
- **Testing evidence** — UAT or QA sign-off prior to release
- **Segregation of duties** — developers cannot self-approve their own changes
- **Emergency change procedures** — retroactive approvals with documented rationale

Each of these generates artifacts. The audit question is always: can you prove the control operated effectively throughout the period?

---

## Where AI Adds the Most Value

### 1. Change Ticket Summarization

Most change management systems generate verbose tickets. AI can scan change request documentation and extract the control-relevant fields: requestor, approver, change description, affected systems, test evidence reference, and go-live timestamp.

**What this replaces:** Manual review of each ticket to confirm completeness before audit delivery.

### 2. Population Completeness Checks

Before auditors sample, you need to prove your population is complete. AI can reconcile change logs from your deployment pipeline (GitHub, Jira, ServiceNow) against your change management register and flag gaps — changes that went to production without a corresponding ticket or approval record.

**What this replaces:** Hours of spreadsheet work matching deployment logs to ticketing data.

### 3. Approval Pattern Analysis

Segregation of duties failures are easy to miss at scale. AI can scan approval records and flag instances where the requestor and approver are the same person, or where emergency change procedures were invoked without proper post-hoc authorization.

**What this replaces:** Manual SoD reviews that auditors often have to do themselves — and then ask you about.

### 4. Evidence Package Assembly

For sampled changes, auditors need a coherent package: the ticket, the approval, the test evidence, and the deployment confirmation. AI can draft the evidence summary document, cross-reference the attachments, and flag anything missing before the package goes to the auditor.

**What this replaces:** The coordinator role that manually assembles each sample package under time pressure.

### 5. Exception Narrative Drafting

When exceptions occur (emergency changes, retroactive approvals, SoD violations with compensating controls), someone needs to write a clear narrative explaining the facts, root cause, and compensating control. AI can draft these narratives from the raw ticket data, which the control owner then reviews and signs.

**What this replaces:** Blank-page syndrome during an already stressful audit period.

## A Practical Workflow

Here's how a mid-size team might structure this:

1. **Monthly:** Run a completeness check — reconcile deployment logs to change tickets, surface any gaps while they can still be remediated.

2. **Quarterly (pre-audit):** Generate the full population report, run the SoD analysis, and flag any exception candidates.

3. **At audit fieldwork:** Use AI to assemble sample packages within hours instead of days. Each package gets an AI-drafted cover summary that the control owner reviews before submission.

4. **Post-audit:** Use AI to parse any auditor findings into structured action items and assign owners — preventing the same issues from recurring next period.

---

## What AI Can't Do

AI is a documentation and pattern-matching tool, not a control designer. It cannot:

- Determine whether your change control policy is appropriately designed
- Identify systemic SoD issues that require a policy change
- Provide judgment on whether an exception rises to a "deficiency" or "material weakness"
- Replace the control owner's accountability for signing off on evidence

Those require human judgment. AI handles the repetitive evidence-gathering so humans have more time for the judgment calls.

---

## Getting Started

The fastest path to value is usually evidence package assembly. Pick the last five change samples your auditors tested and use an AI tool to draft the cover summaries from the raw ticket data. Compare the result to what your team produced manually. If the quality is close, the ROI case writes itself.

From there, the population completeness check is typically the next highest-impact step — it surfaces issues early enough to fix them before auditors arrive.

---

## The Bottom Line

SOX IT change management controls will never be glamorous. But they're a permanent fixture, and the documentation burden only grows as systems get more complex. AI won't make the controls go away, but it can make the evidence trail almost effortless to produce — which is the part that actually consumes your team's time.
