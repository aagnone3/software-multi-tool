SOX IT access reviews are a recurring compliance burden: every quarter, finance and IT teams must certify that user access to financial systems is appropriate, segregation of duties conflicts are resolved, and terminated employees no longer have active accounts. Done manually, this process takes weeks.

AI can reduce that to days — or even hours.

## Why SOX IT Access Reviews Are So Painful

Access reviews are deceptively complex. You're not just checking whether someone has access — you're checking:

- Whether their access is still appropriate for their current role
- Whether any access combinations create segregation of duties (SoD) conflicts
- Whether terminated or transferred employees still have active accounts
- Whether privileged access is properly justified and documented

Multiply that across dozens of financial systems, hundreds (or thousands) of users, and quarterly frequency, and you understand why access reviews routinely run late and generate deficiencies.

## How AI Transforms the Process

### 1. Automated Access Data Aggregation

AI can pull and normalize access data from multiple systems — ERP, financial reporting tools, treasury platforms, HR systems — into a single review dataset. Instead of exporting spreadsheets from five systems and manually reconciling them, reviewers get a unified view automatically.

### 2. Role-Based Anomaly Detection

Instead of reviewing every user from scratch, AI flags anomalies:
- Users whose access doesn't match their job function
- Employees who changed roles but still have old-role access
- Accounts active longer than the employee's tenure
- Access combinations that violate SoD policies

Reviewers focus on flagged exceptions rather than reviewing clean records.

### 3. Automated Segregation of Duties Analysis

SoD ruleset analysis is deterministic — if someone can both create vendors and approve payments, that's a conflict. AI can apply your SoD matrix to the entire user population in seconds, surfacing conflicts that manual review routinely misses due to volume.

### 4. Termination and Role-Change Reconciliation

AI can cross-reference your access data against HR records to identify:
- Terminated employees with active accounts
- Employees who transferred to non-financial roles but still have financial system access
- Contractors or temporary workers whose access should have expired

This eliminates the "orphaned account" findings that auditors consistently cite.

### 5. Evidence Package Generation

For each access decision, AI can generate documentation: who reviewed it, what was approved or revoked, and the rationale. This creates an audit-ready evidence trail without manual documentation effort.

## Practical Implementation Steps

**Phase 1: Inventory your access review scope.** List every financial system in scope, the data available from each, and the SoD conflicts that need detection.

**Phase 2: Establish AI-assisted aggregation.** Use tools or scripts to pull access data into a normalized format. AI can help write the data extraction queries and transformation logic.

**Phase 3: Configure anomaly and SoD rules.** Work with your compliance team to codify your SoD matrix and define what "normal" access looks like for each role. This is a one-time setup that pays dividends every quarter.

**Phase 4: Run AI-assisted reviews.** Route flagged exceptions to reviewers with AI-generated context: why the access was flagged, what SoD rules it violates, and what action options exist.

**Phase 5: Auto-generate evidence packages.** Configure AI to produce review documentation in your required format automatically at cycle close.

## What AI Can't Replace

Access reviews still require human judgment for:
- Borderline SoD decisions where business necessity may justify a conflict with compensating controls
- New roles or access patterns AI hasn't seen before
- Final sign-off and management certification

The goal is to make the human decision-making focused and efficient, not to eliminate it.

## Getting Started

If you're not ready to build a custom solution, look at what your existing tools already do. Many GRC platforms, ERP systems, and identity governance tools have built-in AI features that you may not be fully using. Start by turning those on before investing in new infrastructure.

The access review process that used to consume three weeks of effort can become a focused two-day exception review. That's a meaningful improvement in both efficiency and control quality.
