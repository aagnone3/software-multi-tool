# How to Automate ESG Scope 3 Category 10 (Processing of Sold Products) Reporting with AI

Scope 3 Category 10 — Processing of Sold Products — covers greenhouse gas emissions generated when downstream companies process intermediate products you sell. For manufacturers and material suppliers, this can be one of the most significant yet hardest-to-measure categories in their value chain.

## Why Category 10 Is Difficult

Unlike direct emissions you control, Category 10 requires understanding how your customers use what you sell:

- **Intermediate product complexity**: You need to map how each product is further processed downstream
- **Customer data gaps**: Most customers don't share their processing emissions data
- **Allocation challenges**: When a product goes into multiple end uses, attribution becomes complex
- **Calculation method selection**: GHG Protocol allows multiple approaches (supplier-specific, hybrid, average-data)

## How AI Accelerates Category 10 Reporting

### 1. Product Classification and Mapping

AI can analyze your product catalog and automatically classify which products are intermediate goods likely to be further processed:

- Extract product specifications from ERP systems
- Cross-reference industry databases to identify processing pathways
- Flag ambiguous products for human review
- Build a structured intermediate product registry

### 2. Activity Data Collection

Gathering the underlying data is often the biggest bottleneck. AI-powered workflows can:

- Parse supplier questionnaire responses at scale
- Extract relevant data points from customer sustainability reports
- Normalize units and methodologies across different data sources
- Identify gaps and trigger automated follow-up requests

### 3. Emission Factor Application

Once you have activity data, AI can automate the calculation layer:

- Match product types to appropriate IPCC or industry-specific emission factors
- Apply the correct allocation methodology per product line
- Handle unit conversions automatically
- Flag outliers that may indicate data quality issues

### 4. Documentation and Audit Trail

Category 10 requires robust methodology documentation for disclosures. AI can:

- Auto-generate calculation methodology narratives
- Maintain version-controlled audit trails
- Produce disclosure-ready summaries for CDP, GRI, or TCFD frameworks
- Flag year-over-year changes that need explanation

## Practical Implementation Steps

**Step 1: Inventory your intermediate products**
Use AI to scan your product database and identify which items undergo further processing by customers.

**Step 2: Map processing pathways**
Leverage industry classification data (NAICS, SIC codes) and AI analysis to map likely downstream processing routes for each product category.

**Step 3: Collect activity data**
Deploy automated data collection workflows — supplier questionnaires, customer surveys, or industry average data — with AI parsing responses as they arrive.

**Step 4: Calculate emissions**
Apply emission factors using an AI-assisted calculation engine that handles edge cases, unit conversions, and methodology selection.

**Step 5: Review and disclose**
Use AI-generated summaries to accelerate internal review, then export disclosure-ready outputs for your sustainability report.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Using revenue allocation as a proxy**: This often produces inaccurate results; physical quantity allocation is more defensible
- **Ignoring multi-use products**: When a sold product has multiple downstream uses, you need weighted average processing emissions
- **Stale emission factors**: AI systems should flag when factors haven't been updated in more than 12 months

## Getting Started

AI tools like document analyzers and meeting summarizers can support Category 10 reporting today — extracting data from supplier responses, summarizing industry reports, and generating methodology documentation. The key is building systematic workflows rather than one-off manual processes.

Start with your top 20% of intermediate products by volume. Automating that slice typically captures 80%+ of Category 10 emissions with manageable effort.
