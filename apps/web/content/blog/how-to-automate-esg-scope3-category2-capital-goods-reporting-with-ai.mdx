# How to Automate ESG Scope 3 Category 2 Capital Goods Reporting with AI

Scope 3 Category 2 — **capital goods** — covers the cradle-to-gate emissions embedded in long-lived assets your organization acquires: manufacturing equipment, fleet vehicles, office buildings, IT infrastructure, and more.

Unlike operational purchases, capital goods are depreciated over their useful life, which creates a unique accounting challenge: do you recognize the full embedded carbon upfront (as GHG Protocol recommends) or amortize it? Either way, getting the underlying data right requires supplier engagement and spend-based estimation, both of which are time-intensive without automation.

## Why Category 2 Is Hard to Report

- **Asset heterogeneity** — A single year's capex might span HVAC systems, forklifts, and servers, each with different emission factors
- **Supplier data gaps** — Most equipment vendors don't publish product-level carbon data
- **Spend-based fallback** — When supplier data is unavailable, teams must apply EEIO emission factors to spend, which requires clean categorization of capital expenditures
- **Boundary decisions** — Determining what counts as "capital goods" vs. "use of sold products" or "upstream leased assets" requires policy clarity

## How AI Accelerates Each Step

### 1. Capital Expenditure Classification

AI can parse accounts payable and fixed asset registers, then auto-classify each line item against NAICS codes or EEIO spend categories. This replaces weeks of manual spreadsheet work.

**Prompt example:**
> "Classify the following capital expenditure line items into EEIO Scope 3 Category 2 spend categories. Flag any items that may belong to a different Category (e.g., Category 1 or Category 11)."

### 2. Supplier Emission Factor Lookup

When supplier-specific data is available (e.g., Environmental Product Declarations or supplier-provided PCFs), AI can extract emission factors from PDFs, match them to asset records, and flag mismatches.

### 3. Spend-Based Estimation Automation

For assets without supplier data, AI can apply current EEIO emission factors by spend category, calculate tCO₂e per asset, and roll up to a Category 2 total — producing an audit-ready worksheet.

### 4. Prior Year Comparisons and Variance Commentary

AI can compare current-year Category 2 estimates against prior periods, explain major drivers (new factory vs. normal capex cycle vs. divestiture), and draft disclosure language.

## Building a Category 2 Reporting Workflow

A practical AI-assisted workflow:

1. **Extract capex data** from ERP (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite) — AI parses structured exports
2. **Classify to EEIO categories** — LLM maps descriptions to spend codes
3. **Look up emission factors** — pull current EEIO factors from EPA or Exiobase database
4. **Calculate tCO₂e** — AI applies: `spend ($) × emission factor (kgCO₂e/$) ÷ 1000`
5. **Validate against prior year** — flag >20% variance for review
6. **Draft disclosure** — AI generates GRI/TCFD-aligned commentary

## Common Pitfalls

- **Double-counting with Category 1**: goods that are consumed quickly belong in Category 1 (purchased goods and services), not Category 2
- **Using stale emission factors**: EEIO factors update periodically; AI can flag when your factors are more than 2 years old
- **Ignoring leased assets**: if your org controls an asset under a finance lease, it may still qualify as Category 2

## Disclosure Frameworks That Reference Category 2

- **GHG Protocol Corporate Value Chain Standard** — the primary methodology
- **GRI 305-3** — Scope 3 emissions disclosure
- **CSRD/ESRS E1-6** — European mandatory disclosure from 2025
- **CDP Climate questionnaire** — C6.5 Scope 3 breakdown

## Sample Disclosure Language

> "Scope 3 Category 2 (Capital Goods) emissions totaled 12,450 tCO₂e in FY2031, representing 8% of our total Scope 3 footprint. The primary driver was capital investment in manufacturing automation equipment. Emissions were calculated using the spend-based method with EPA EEIO v2.0 emission factors, supplemented by supplier-provided EPDs for 34% of capital spend."

## Getting Started

1. Pull your fixed asset additions register for the reporting year
2. Identify your top 20 capital expenditure categories by spend
3. Use an AI tool to map each category to EEIO codes
4. Apply current emission factors and calculate totals
5. Document your methodology choices for auditors

The spend-based method isn't perfect, but it's defensible, scalable, and far better than omitting Category 2 entirely. AI makes the classification and calculation steps fast enough to be part of your standard close cycle.
