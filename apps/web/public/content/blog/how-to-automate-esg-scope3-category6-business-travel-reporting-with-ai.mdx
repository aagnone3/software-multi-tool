# How to Automate ESG Scope 3 Category 6 Business Travel Reporting with AI

Business travel emissions (Scope 3 Category 6) are notoriously difficult to track. Data is scattered across airline booking systems, hotel platforms, corporate cards, and employee expense reports — each with different formats and emission factors.

AI tools are changing this by automating data extraction, emissions calculation, and GHG Protocol-compliant reporting workflows.

## Why Category 6 Is Challenging

- **Multiple data sources**: flights, hotels, rental cars, trains, rideshare
- **Inconsistent data formats**: PDFs, CSVs, APIs, email receipts
- **Complex emission factors**: DEFRA, EPA, airline-specific factors vary by route and cabin class
- **Employee self-reporting gaps**: incomplete or delayed expense submissions

## How AI Automates Business Travel Reporting

### 1. Data Aggregation from Travel Systems

AI can connect to:
- Corporate booking tools (Concur, Amex GBT, TravelPerk)
- Expense management platforms (Expensify, Brex, Navan)
- Credit card transaction feeds

It extracts flight routes, hotel stays, and ground transport with structured output ready for emissions calculation.

### 2. Emission Factor Matching

AI maps each trip segment to the right emission factor:
- Flight segments → airline/route-specific kg CO₂e per km (DEFRA or airline-published)
- Hotel nights → location-based or spend-based factors
- Ground transport → fuel type and distance-based factors

### 3. GHG Protocol Category 6 Calculations

With structured trip data and matched emission factors, AI calculates:
- Total Scope 3 Category 6 emissions (tCO₂e)
- Per-employee and per-department breakdowns
- Business unit or cost center allocations

### 4. Variance Commentary and Trend Analysis

AI generates draft commentary explaining:
- Quarter-over-quarter changes
- Top emitting departments or regions
- Impact of travel policy changes (e.g., video-first mandates)

### 5. Audit Trail and Data Quality Checks

AI flags:
- Missing emission factors for unusual routes
- Duplicate bookings or receipts
- Gaps in coverage (employees who traveled but have no data)

## Key Benefits

| Manual Process | AI-Automated |
|---|---|
| Weeks of data gathering | Hours |
| Error-prone factor matching | Consistent, auditable factors |
| Limited drill-down | Full employee/dept breakdowns |
| Narrative writing takes days | Draft commentary in minutes |

## Getting Started

1. **Audit your travel data sources** — inventory every booking and expense platform
2. **Choose an emission factor library** — DEFRA, EPA, or supplier-specific
3. **Connect data feeds** — API integrations or scheduled exports
4. **Configure reporting templates** — aligned with GHG Protocol Category 6 guidance
5. **Review and approve AI outputs** — human oversight for material decisions

AI doesn't replace sustainability judgment — it eliminates the manual data work so your team can focus on reducing emissions rather than calculating them.
